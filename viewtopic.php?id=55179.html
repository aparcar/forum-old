kazuken wrote:

But I let it sit there after it uploads and the WPS led turns off. So should i powercycle right after the LED turns off?

I can't tell you exactly when to do it, I don't know any reliable indicators. But if you let it sit for five minutes after the file transfer is complete, you can power cycle it. Nothing else is going to happen, anyway.

I recommend downloading an official firmware image from TP-Link and stripping it. Ideally, the same firmware version that was on it before you flashed any third-party firmware. Not strictly necessary, but it ensures that your bootloader matches your firmware.
