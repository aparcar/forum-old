Do you have a router in addition to your modem? Does your modem hand out local IPs on the network? Or do you use your modem only do dial in from your PC? Normally the Whitebox asks for an IP and uses whatever is given to it. And then it acts as a transparent bridge. Also, weird that you just flashed a different firmware then. What gave you that idea and why do you want to go back now?

However that may be. To simply configure the network, you don’t need to compile anything. You can simply put the box into Failsafe Mode, edit the /etc/config/network file and it will use that setting.

To flash the whitebox firmware back, pick on of the Samknows images from the GPL source tarball’s /bin subdirectory.
