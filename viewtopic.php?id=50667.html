I'm trying to use a WDR3600 v1.5 as a router for my gigabit WAN connection (fibre).

When handling large amount of traffic the WAN port link starts to drop intermittently. This is visible on the Network > Switch page of OpenWrt and through swconfig in the shell.

The scenario that seems to trigger the 'no link' is when the remote host (like in a Skype call over WiFi on the receiving end) has varying latency and/or gets overwhelmed by the amount of traffic. When the scenario triggers packets loss rises up to 40% (Skype call information) and then the 'No link' icon appears on the OpenWrt switch page. After half a second or so the link returns to 1 gbit but the connection will keep hicking up until the amount of traffic being routed is reduced.

The ethernet ring buffers in source seem to be set fairly low; 32 for the tx and 128 for the rx. I tried setting them to 256/256 and while this increased the speed up to 170mbps of NAT'ed traffic instead of the default 130mbps with speedtest.net it does not mitigate the problem.

It's driving my slightly crazy so ANY help would be greatly appreciated. I really love OpenWrt for the amount of functionality it offers in stark contrast to the factory firmware.

Thanks for reading

TLDR; gigabit WAN connection drops link when internet traffic becomes congested
