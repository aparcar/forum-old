it is possible to mount an samba share by:

>opkg update
>opkg install kmod-fs-cifs
>opkg install mount-utils
>opkg install cifsmount
>mkdir tmp/samba
>mount -t cifs //SambaNameOrIP/Share /tmp/samba -o ip=XX.XX.XX.XX,user=john,pass=doe,dom=group

in which "SambaNameOrIP" change to hostname or IP address of your samba drive , "Share" change to name of your share on samba server, "XX.XX.XX.XX" change to your samba server's IP address (eg. 192.168.5.7), "john" change to your username on samba server ... "doe" change to your password and "group" change to your name of your workgroup or domain, if you do not send any login or password leave it blank
