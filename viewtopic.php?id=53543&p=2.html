Well, I installed OpenWRT on my Tp-Link WDR3600 and it works perfectly. I also installed and configured guest wifi, ddns, usb2 and sftp, all works as expected.

I had some problems with the atm bridge, since I have a PPPoEoA connection. Understanding the problem, sideloading the packages and installing them was a bit difficult... luckily I have some linux and embedded linux experience, this is not simple for a newbie. I strongly suggest to include atm-bridge by default.

I also noticed a problem that happened also with the stock firmware: sometimes I can't get internet. With stock firmware I had to power off and on again the router or the modem. Now I noticed that I can make it working disabling networking from Ubuntu GUI and re-enabling it. Not sure about the source of the bug.

Marco Sulla wrote:

Hello all. I want to buy a new modem/router and install OpenWRT on it. Based on my little experience, I have in mind to buy a modem/router that has:

1. 802.11n wifi
2. at least two external antennas - for what I read, the second antenna improves the signal strength
3. a wifi and power toggle button
4. at least four 10/100/1000 lan ports
5. at least one USB port (usb 2.0 is enough)
6. stable support in OpenWRT firmware
7. no more than 100$

Do you have any suggestions? I also read that Atheros chips gets a more powerful wifi signal.

Newegg had a sale for Archer C7v2 ($60 with free shipping), it's a bit better than you're looking for and supported in BB/CC. Not sure about the power toggle tho.

I've been using tp-link wdr3600 for a while (got it shortly after they came to North America) and it's rock solid with AA. Tried to upgrade to BB, wasn't happy and rolled back.
