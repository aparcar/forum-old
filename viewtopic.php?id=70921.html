mk24
15 May 2017, 19:26

When you install an application, it becomes part of the file system thus it is ready to run right after boot.  Scripts in /etc/init.d are usually used to automatically launch applications during boot.

You can use Image Builder or other binary tools to pack your application into the root image, so the whole firmware is one file.  But as eduperez said, if you want to distribute that file you have to follow GPL rules.

My two cents:

  • You already have an application developed and tested.

  • Install it into your device, and configure it to start your app on boot; follow mk24's advice, and have a look at "https://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/techref/process.boot" and "https://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/techref/initscripts".

  • You also seem capable of building your own firmware, now test it.

  • Finally, follow the instructions at "https://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/howto/build#custom_files" to add your custom files to the build.
