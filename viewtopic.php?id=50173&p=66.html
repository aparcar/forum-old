The wireless signal should be as good or better than other routers. I've deployed many of these and found this to be the case.

Keep in mind that the 5Ghz is short range and super fast. 2.4Ghz is long range and slower. If you previously had the 2.4Ghz and 5Ghz configured with the same SSID you might have been connecting to the 2.4Ghz instead of the 5Ghz at farther locations.

Is your ping test to the router or internet?
Do you have a Android mobile you can run Wifi Analyzer on to see the signal strength and wireless channels at different spots at the location?
Double check the antennas are fully screwed on.

Each ext4 partition has a lost+found folder. It was created together with the partition. Any recovered file during filesystem check will be moved here. I never deleted it, but i guess it's possible and won't make any problem. To do that you must ssh to the router with root. Dot in the beginning of the file or folder means its hidden.

KLit75 wrote:

I can see sda1 and sdb1 but neither of those are my drive. I need to find mnt/sda2. It was there last night but since rebooting I just cant find in under smb. Like I said it shows up in Luci, and I checked enable mount, saved changes, rebooted and tried various cobos of that.

Have you connected two drives to both usb ports? sda1 is the first partition on the first drive, sdb1 is the first partition on the second drive, etc.. It seems you plugged in some other drive as well in the meantime, so your sda2 changed to sdb2. Normally it should be mounted automatically on boot.

KLit75 wrote:

I don't see a ton of options under mount in the ui.

Have you installed samba package for luci? (luci-app-samba) If you did, you have a "Network Shares" tab under "Services" with more options. Like here.

Most of these are not openwrt related but generic linux questions, so if you check some generic linux howtos (mounting, filesystems, basic shell commands, etc) you will have better understanding whats going on, and that will boost your problem solving skills.
