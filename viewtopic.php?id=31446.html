Hello-

I have two Access Points (AP's), one on each side of the building.  I want them to talk to each other. Each AP is connected to the internet using an Android phone (the phones themselves act as wireless routers and the AP's just connect as a client).  Each AP also has a bunch of wired hardware that it connects to the internet.

So the AP's work fine in solo, but I can't get the two to talk to each other.  Here are the two AP's:
1.  ASUS wl-500g v2 (running the 2.4 kernel)
2.  D-Link DIR-615

Both AP's have Backfire 10.3.1 rc 5.  It seems that WDS does not work between these two devices (different drivers).  I haven't been able to get two wireless clients to work on either card (one for the android and one for the other AP).  I see comments about relayd working in the latest release of Backfire, but that just sounds like another AP client.

The core of the issue is this question:   Is it possible to have one card be a client to two AP's?

My T-mobile "unlimited internet" plan is actually limited to 5GB a month, so we really need the routing to both phones for both networks or the internet will be painfully slow (thanks t-mobile).  So, I need at least one of the routers to handle two clients.  Do I need to buy another whole router to get these two networks to talk?  What options do I have?  Any ideas would be greatly appreciated.

Thanks!

>Suppose you were able to let the two APs talk to each other. What's next? How to control the traffics?

Each AP has it's own sub-net.  Then, I would route the traffic as necessary.  Default traffic goes thru the phone, traffic to 192.168.a.x goes to router a.

Overall, I'm quite surprised about this limitation.  The Luci web interface gives no warnings that what I am trying to do is going to break and then no errors when it does break.  I spent hours debugging and troubleshooting this issue and really don't have a solid answer.  It's really a simple question: can one radio connect to two clients?

In the end, it was cheaper to just buy another router.  I like the idea of getting another entire unit instead of trying to debug a new USB device.
