How long did you wait? While you are saving stuff, just restarting it is generally a bad idea.

First of all, reset the settings using the small reset button on the back of the device. (you need a small, pen-like thing to press it) While the device is on, keep it pressed for a few seconds before releasing.

Try getting into failsafe using the reset button or try pressing both the reset button and the QSS button to get into failsafe mode. You need to keep them pressed until SYS blinks faster, by the way.

(Sidenote: I never had recvudp.exe work for me. Just sayin'.)

http://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/howto/generic.failsafe <- IMPORTANT - Everything is inside there

Telnet to your device (& log in)
mount_root (mounts the jffs partition)
firstboot (resets everything to factory settings)
reboot -f

That should do the trick since your device doesn't seem to be bricked. (Boots, SYS led blinks faster than usual which indicates failsafe mode)
