fwiw, I spent a bit of time investigating the issue on my Mynet N750.  I have two units but only one is live.  Both were originally flashed from WD stock fw to CC rc3.  Then recently I sys-upgraded them to CC 15.05 final.  I kept my settings during the sys-upgrade.

Experimenting with my spare N750, I decided to flash the CC 15.05 final 'factory' bin to the router.  I did not reload my live router settings file.

My observations with changing channel numbers on 5 GHz band were as follows.  Using Country code: GB.  There doesn't appear to be any issue if I change channel between channel numbers 36, 40, 44 or 48.  Usually the change is completed in less than 30 seconds and my laptops don't appear to report loss of wifi signal during the switch over.

If I choose to use channels 52 or higher, or 100 or higher, this is where I now observe the router can take about 1-2 minutes to effect the change.   The laptops also report loss of wifi signal but eventually the signal does return.   I understand channels 52 and above have to use DFS which may explain the behaviour.

LUCI can also sometimes be  horrendously slow at updating the wifi status on the page. May be worse because there was no WAN connection during these tests (I use PPPoE so WAN port is disconnected).   I also noticed if I edit the channel number and press 'Save & Apply' button, it some times did not appear to work.  The channel number remained unchanged after waiting patiently for the page to refresh.    I use chrome web browser in Windows.   I find if I open an 'Incognito' web session to the router, LUCI seems to behave better.    I use INSSIDer to verify the presence of the wifi signal and the channel number it is using.


Next step was to load my live router settings file into the test router to see if it made any difference.  The settings don't appear to alter the behaviour of the N750 to what I have already described above.  Note the test router is Not connected to WAN.


Next, I wired the above test router into one of the LAN ports of my live N750 router so it has access to the internet.  (WAN connection changed from PPPoE to DHCP client, and LAN subnet changed to 192.168.0.x). 

From what I learned earlier, I didn't have any difficulties changing wifi channel. 

Finally I turned my attention to my live router which has been operating uninterupted for past 9 days.  I changed the wifi channel from no.36 to 52 to 100 to 132 and back to no.36.   The changes were all completed successfully.


My conclusion is it looks like I was simply impatient when previously trying to test wifi channel no. above 48.  It appears higher channel numbers take up to 2 minutes to initialize instead of 20-30 seconds.  There also seems to be an odd issue with how LUCI was behaving when using Chrome web browser.
