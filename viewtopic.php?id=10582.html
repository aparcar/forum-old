Recently I installed a printer on the network, named hugo.  It powered up with DHCP and got address 192.168.1.17.

I wanted hugo to have a different address, in the IP region I've set aside for printers.  No big deal, really.

So I added a line to /etc/hosts:

192.168.1.112 hugo hugo.mydomain.com


but hugo got a taste for 192.168.1.17, and asks for it by number.  And dnsmasq gives it what it wants!

May  3 10:41:35 (none) kern.info dnsmasq[588]: DHCPDISCOVER(br0) 192.168.1.17 08:00:37:42:c8:ff
May  3 10:41:35 (none) kern.info dnsmasq[588]: DHCPOFFER(br0) 192.168.1.17 08:00:37:42:c6:ff
May  3 10:41:37 (none) kern.info dnsmasq[588]: DHCPREQUEST(br0) 192.168.1.17 08:00:37:42:c6:ff
May  3 10:41:37 (none) kern.info dnsmasq[588]: DHCPACK(br0) 192.168.1.17 08:00:37:42:c6:ff hugo
May  3 10:41:37 (none) kern.warn dnsmasq[588]: not giving name hugo.mydomain.com to the DHCP lease of 192.168.1.17 because the name exists in /etc/hosts with address 192.168.1.112
May  3 10:41:37 (none) kern.warn dnsmasq[588]: not giving name hugo to the DHCP lease of 192.168.1.17 because the name exists in /etc/hosts with address 192.168.1.112

Is the problem that I don't have a hardware address for hugo? This /etc/hosts thing works for most of my other DHCP systems that I want on an assigned address.

I can do workarounds, but I'd like to know what I'm doing wrong.
