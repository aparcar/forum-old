that you are in a 10.x.x.x subnet says nothing about if you have a dedicated public IP or not. E.g. in Austria every (homeuser) dsl comes with a modem with a buildin firewall/NAT, but every modem gets it own public IP. So you can configure port forwardings to your server behind it, to be reachable from the internet. But you need access to the modem. So try to open the ip of your defaultgateway with your favorite browser.
That multiple users share one public IP is only used for 3G/UMTS connections (at least in Austria)

If you really have no dedicated public ip, the only way to make your services reachable is to tunnel everything through a host with a public ip. this can be a vpn-service somewhere or a vserver with a vpn-gateway, yosti is something similar, but I'm not sure if the free service is enough an I think you will get a openvpn-gateway cheaper than their paid services.

But fist check is you really have no dedicated public ip (assigned to your modem), before you digg into tunneling
