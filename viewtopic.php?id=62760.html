Hi all,

I am building a project that will result in (hopefully) hundreds of prebuild raspberry pi's (running openwrt) in local schools in an effort to protect children from various dangers on the web.

However, since there are often power cuts in my country, I am loosing hair thinking about having tons of SD cards failing and having to drive out to replace them. As a result, I have researched ways to prevent corruption on the SD card (major problem with the default OS on RPi's) and I came across the concept of building a full read-only file system and then symlinking things like logs, etc. to /tmp (or something to that effect, maybe even to an attached USB disk).

Here is an example (even though I would rather use Openwrt) - http://archive.nutcom.hu/ipe/

My two questions

1) Is Openwrt as suseptable to SD card corruption on the Raspberry pi 2 as the default OS? From what I have learnt, it is not as it keeps the kernel, etc. (on the first partition) and that is readonly.

2) Is it possible to convert additional areas (for use of a better word) to read only to minimize the risk mentioned above?

Thanks everyone

you can use a squasfs image if you want it to be like similar filesystem on common router builds.

squashfs makes the main image as read only and use the rest of the space as overlay.
