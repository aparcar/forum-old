16 Nov 2015, 02:10

OpenWrt does not support hardware-accelerated NAT, which limits its WAN to LAN throughput.  OEM firmware on most modern routers enables hardware accelerated NAT, many supporting near gigabit speeds.  I have not heard any plans to bring hardware NAT to OpenWrt.  Last time I inquired I believe the reason given was related to no open source drivers for the hardware acceleration components.

I recently purchased an Edgerouter Lite for my firewall/NAT for gigabit internet, and relegated my OpenWrt router to a simple WiFi access point without firewall/NAT.  I could choose to run DHCP server, DNS caching, etc, on the OpenWrt router for the LAN, but I just left them enabled on the Edgerouter and turned everything off in my OpenWrt router except for WiFi AP.
