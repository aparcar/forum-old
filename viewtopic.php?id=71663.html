I left the MAC addresses in; unadulterated boot log.

57600,8,no parity, 1 stop, no hardware.

There is a very tiny T/R next to the bios chip.  I can't read/find the eeprom listed. Was a complete and utter pita to get to.

Once it comes up, it drops right into a busybox shell.  Can run whatever commands you like.

I have loved this little guy since I set it up.  The GUI was easily configured as an AP, the signal was a heck of a lot stronger than any of my other boxes, and it drops right into an outlet.  I think 2 or 3 of these will cover the whole house in mesh-like APs.

I am in the process of modifying the heat sinks (thick thermal pads) to take an aluminum heat pipe/chamber.  That'll help spread the heat faster as it does get warm.  I reduced the power on the chip (don't know what its actual output is).
