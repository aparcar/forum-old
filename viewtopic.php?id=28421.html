Hello,

I found out that after adding a self powered 3,5" hard drive (via SATA-2,5"-3,5" -> USB) to my HUB,
I got several "connect-debounce failed" lines in dmegs and USB devices started working erraticly (detach-attach a little later automatically).

I found out that this self powered 3,5" hard drive is working like a charm in other conditions.
The power adapter of this drive is cheap Chinese +5 +12V type.

When I powered the HUB from HD's power adapter (instead of its own power plug) "connect-debounce failed" problem eliminated (when I plug hard-drive).

My theory:
- More (cheap?) self powered device together could cause "connect-debounce failed" if their zero points are not grounded together.

My system:
TP-Link TP-1043ND with Backfire (10.03.1-rc4, r24045)
- 1a40:0201 TERMINUS TECHNOLOGY INC. - self powered 7 port HUB
- 090d:1001 Multiport Computer Vertriebs GmbH - flash disk
- 0a81:0101 Chesen Electronics Corp. Keyboard - keypad
- 093a:2608 Pixart Imaging, Inc. PAC7311 Trust WB-3300p - webcam
- 05e3:0718 Genesys Logic, Inc. IDE/SATA Adapter - SATA-2,5"-3,5" -> USB converter connected with 500G 3,5" self powered Western Caviar drive
- 03f0:6204 Hewlett-Packard DeskJet 5150c - self powered printer

The printer is not "connected" to this zero potential yet (testing):
I unplugged hard drive (cannot be bus powered).
When I plug printer -> "connect-debounce failed"
When I unplug HUB power (became bus powered) -> NO "connect-debounce failed" show up!!!
Plug back HUB power -> printer not working & "connect-debounce failed" show up again.
Unplug USB power -> printer show up in dmesg and work nicely.

Now (to prove my theory AND to work anything together) I will connect grounds of USB&hard drive and printer power.

I will let you know what happened.

... I have strange feeling like talking to myself :-)

Anyway if somebody will have similar problem ever, my "problem" could be a possible reason and I give solution also.
So I connected every self powered device's GND line together (which is connected through my USB HUB) and "connect-debounce failed" gone away.
GND here means the zero (ground) potential wire which goes to self powered device.

In my case I connected every GND point:
- 3,5" hard drive (where power input is like this: +5V, GND, GND, +12V)
- self powered USB HUB (where power input is like this: +5V, GND)
- old HP DeskJet (where power input is like this: +16V, GND, +32V)

... and this solved my "connect-debounce failed" problem ever.
How did I connect? Just soldered them together with a wire.

BR,
Mark
