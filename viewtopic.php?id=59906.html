My wrt54G-tm was running dd-wrt, but I wanted to try to fix my bufferbloat so I attempted to run chaos calmer only to discover that some things worked while others didn't.

After reading http://wiki.openwrt.org/toh/linksys/wrt54g#, which is old, I attempted to extrapolate the best chaos calmer bin for my device and wound up choosing /chaos_calmer/15.05/brcm47xx/legacy/openwrt-15.05-brcm47xx-legacy-linksys-wrt54g-squashfs.  This didn't set well with me because my wrt54g-tm has a Broadcom BCM5352 chip rev 0, but since there didn't appear to be a better match, and I couldn't find anyone proclaiming the right bin for a wrt54g-tm, I went with that selection and my wrt54G-tm didn't brick. 

With the freshly installed openwrt I measured my bufferbloat and tried my voip phone.  The phone quality was better and my bufferbloat A+.  Curiously, when I installed SQM per http://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/howto/sqm my bufferbloat got worse, no matter how much I screwed with upload and download settings.  So I uninstalled SQM and was happy, until I discovered that all my wifi connecting devices didn't work.  They associated fine, but opening a simple webpage took over 10 minutes.  Turning off wireless security fixed the malady, but that options no good.  Thus after screwing with all possible WPA WPA2 settings I figured something about the wifi drivers was screwy.  Openwrt was saying that the wifi card was GENERIC, and the mac address was different and goofy, EX 00:00:00:00:02:00.  In fact all the mac addresses (LAN WAN WIFI) had been changed.  If I could just get the wifi to work then I'd stick with openwrt, so if I chose the wrong bin, or of anyone's got any other ideas then I'd appreciate them.
