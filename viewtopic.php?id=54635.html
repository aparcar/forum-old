Installed software packages are by default stored in the flash so that they stay there after you turn the power off.  However, with a USB drive you can have your system's root directory onto it (look up "extroot" in the wiki) and then have basically unlimited space to install packages.  Captured data can also be stored there or, by a variety of methods, sent directly to another computer. 

The amount of software that can be running at one time is limited by the space it takes in RAM.  If you're buying a new router for this, consider a model with at least 64 MB of RAM.
