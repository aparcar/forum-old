Greetings,

I am using the lovely kismet-for-windows & drone-on-OpenWRT[1] combo but have some issues with the GPS coordinates.

My setup:
- The drone runs flawless on OpenWRT WhiteRussian RC4 [1]
- GPS device is the Holux 236 [bluetooth] [2]
- Franson GPS gate v 2.0 [source is com15, destination com1 com2 com3 & com4] [3]
- 2 * Netstumbler with a cisco 350 & the interal Broadcom [on winxp, you guessed it]
- kismet server & client & gpsd run on XP [1]

Netstumbler and anyother gps data using application are 100% happy with the output of the GPS unit [shared via Franson v 2.0] and when I check the output of the com ports with hyperterminal all seems just fine, but.

The GPS status in kismet keeps reporting: "lock, 2D lock, 3D lock, no lock, lock, 2D lock, 3D.." etc in about a 2 to 3 seconds frequency.

It makes NO difference if:
- the receiver is placed outside, inside, closer or less close.
- Netstumbler is closed
- I move fast or slow [yes, this setup runs in my car]
- more or less satelites are "seen"

Does anyone of you have a clue where to look for? Anyone seen this?

Ok, the issue has been solved.

After reading this post over at the netstumbler forums I went ahead and followed goldfndr his idea:

For anyone unable to get a 3D lock on their GPS unit that speaks only NMEA, try commenting out line 569 of libgpsd_core.c:
// gps_clear_fix(&session->gpsdata.fix);

This did the trick. Hope it helps someone who wants to run a kismet-drone on openwrt, the client, server & gpsd on windows.

Cheers,
mokum
