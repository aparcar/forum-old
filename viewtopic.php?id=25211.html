to test CA-42 you can take the following , try shorting two wires to check which combination of it is rx/tx..
when you short the wires you can type in console connected to it. you should see your typed stuff echoed back to you.

next..
most CA 42 have 3rd wire gnd, while some have gnd and power.. a simple multimeter would do the trick if there were two more wires..
else the only remaining one is always gnd.

22 Jun 2010, 03:52

Should work. You know the Rx and Tx cables but don't know which is which, and with a ca-42 cable you need Rx, Tx and Gnd connected. So have Rx and Tx connected and try each of the other 4 cables until it works. If it doesn't work, switch the Rx and Tx cables, and try each of the other 4 cables until it works. You have a 1 in 8 shot of getting it right.

Have a look at the wiki. I've just updated it with some information about the serial.
http://wiki.openwrt.org/toh/tp-link/tl-wr741nd
