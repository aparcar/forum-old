Well, you get all the features of OpenWrt in general of course - extremely stable firmware that's extensible to do just about anything you want. I use it for the stability and the superb QoS personally. Lots of people use it to enable their router to act as a file sharing device or print server. You also get a consistent interface and feature set across devices.

Stock is probably easier to use, but that's about it's only advantage. I suppose hardware NAT is one feature lost in OpenWrt because current implementations from the manufacturers are not compatible with certain parts of OpenWrt.

drawz
6 May 2015, 06:47

The Archer C5/C7 use a fairly new chipset, so full support is still being implemented. For the most stable hardware/software combination, I'd like at an 802.11n router. Most people really don't take advantage of 802.11ac speeds anyway. I believe it does work reasonably well on the Archer C5/C7 though, although probably better in the upcoming Chaos Calmer release.

Guest network is not a built-in feature unfortunately. I have argued that it should be. There are instructions for setting this up using the GUI in the wiki. http://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/recipes/gue … binterface
