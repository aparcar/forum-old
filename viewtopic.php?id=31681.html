The WR1043ND is a piece of decent hardware at a decent price. In Germany you can get it for € 42,- including 19% sales tax! Add shipping cost of none to € 7,-
http://gh.de/?phist=486889&age=9999

Also consider that TP-Link does not fuck with their customers like, e.g. Buffalo likes to do, so there should be no problems flashing OpenWrt directly with the Webinterface of the OEM firmware! There should also be no problems with the bootloader or bad surprises with new hardware revisions. AFAIK, the number behind the comma doesn't even mean a new hardware revision with TP-Link products, only new firmware. So HW 1.0 is the same hardware as 1.9. Though I am not 100% sure about this.

If you want 5GHz or even simultaneous dual radio and/or more RAM, you could look here: http://gh.de/?cat=wlanrout&xf=758_D … amp;sort=p
and then compare with the OpenWrt ToH:  http://wiki.openwrt.org/toh/start


Overall I would not pay too much for such embedded hardware, because this babies here are brand new and pretty hot:
http://gh.de/?pg=2&cat=mbson&xf … amp;sort=p

The second cheapest is already supported by coreboot! It ain't a router, but for € 80,- you can have a lot of fun with it. And at the end of this year intel comes with new 22nm hardware (no Atoms though). Thus I would not pay more than € 40,- a wireless router.

3 Sep 2011, 12:19

The WR1043ND is cheap and well supported.
I've been using one for more than a year now and have never failed to find the assistance, here on openwrt, that I need to work with it.
It's never failed me once, flash updates are easy, generic failsafe works as described.
I'm currently running bleeding edge trunk without problems.

Your basic list of requirements should include 8mb of flash minimum (which the WR1043ND has). Trying to work with 4mb is a balancing act that almost always ends with you having to leave out something that you wanted to include in your build. And if you do build yourself, you can remove things you don't use allowing you to make even better use of the 8mb that you have.

For the price, features and support, WR1043ND is a winner for me.
