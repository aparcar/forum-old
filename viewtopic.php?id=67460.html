Hello,

Coming from an old Linksys WRT54GL, I decided to upgrade and got a brand new
Linksys WRT1200AC.

The WRT54GL had a pretty standard configuration: one mac filtered wifi SSID
bridged with the LAN.

I already configured my WRT1200AC so that the LAN is split into VLAN1 and VLAN2.
Consider VLAN2 as unused.

I would like to have two wifi SSID: WifiGuest as a standalone network
and WifiTrust that would allow client devices to be on VLAN1 (same function
than the wifi configured on the WRT54GL).
Since the WRT1200AC has two wifi devices, I wondered what would be the best
solution:

1/ no bridge, each wifi device with its exclusive network:
   - 2.4Ghz for WifiGuest,
   - 5Ghz for WifiTrust, bridged with VLAN1

2/ bridge both wifi devices so that client devices can connect to whatever band
   they are compatible with.

Since my network and routing skills have a lot of space for improvements, I
would like some enlightenments.

Solution 1 is pretty simple, but doesn't seem optimal.

is solution 2 even possible ?
Should I then create WifiGuest and WifiTrust SSIDs for both device and associate
the two WifiTrust SSIDs with VLAN1 ?

Is there a real benefit to bridge the two wifi devices ?

Thanks for your inputs.

As a newbie it's unclear what you know and have read here so far, so please try to consult the wiki and search the forum first.

The wiki should have brought you to this article.  https://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/recipes/guest-wlan?s[]=guest&s[]=network

I do not own this or another dual band router on OpenWrt, so I can not comment on what is "bridged", but expect that the LAN and both wireless are so in default.  The above link indicates you can create a Guest LAN without the need for VLANs on the primary router.  The article is not specific to a dual band router, but I suspect you would need to create a wireless connection attached to the interface WifiGuest for each band.

Regarding your last question, the reason to bridge the interfaces is to be able to share across them, and this is generally desired in a home LAN.
