Hi, folks!

I like OpenWrt very much, having been able to transform my Linksys NSLU2 to a Printer Server, NFS Server etc.

But to tell you the truth, I am quite frustrated because of the really bad documentation. There *is* a lot of documentation, but scattered on very different places, making it very difficult to find the right information.

There is:
* a more or less "official", but very incomplete documentation: http://downloads.openwrt.org/kamikaze/docs/openwrt.html
* a Wiki containing much information, but which is really a mess: Often it is difficult to guess whether the docu is for Whiterussian or Kamikaze; the directory structure is really inconsequent - e.g. you have
/UsbStorageHowto
/OpenWrtDocs/Kamikaze/Timezones
/OpenWrtDocs/KamikazeConfiguration/UCI
/OpenWrtDocs/OpnenWrtDocs/KamikazeBootScriptsHowTo
All of these pages contain information for configuring kamikaze - do you consider this logical?
* the "Howtos and Documentation" section of this forum.

Today I found out something that might be interesting for other users, too; I wanted to share that, writing it to a wiki page, but I gave up, not knowing where I should put it. Is there anybody facing the same problems? Wouldn't it be great to have a well-structured wiki helping the newbies to find the information they need? Who would be able and interested in improving the situation? (I tried to rename the page /OpenWrtDocs/OpnenWrtDocs/KamikazeBootScriptsHowTo to /OpenWrtDocs/KamikazeBootScriptsHowTo but was not able to do so...)

What do you think about it?

Cheers,
