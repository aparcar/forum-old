The answer to #1 is Yes. The rule of thumb here, for PSK, is to keep the password above 15 characters, so the possibility of cracking the password in your lifetime is less likely.

Bottom line... a nosy neighbour handy with wifi cracking tools isn't going to crack your 15 character password if WPA2 with AES encryption is implemented. As CPU power continues to increase, all bets are off in 10 years
