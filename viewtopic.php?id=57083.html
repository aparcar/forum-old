You may be behind CG NAT... I use a dongle plugged in to a Banana Pi (so, in effect, the same as tethering) and get an IP in the 100.x.x.x range from my mobile provider. This means it can't be reached from 'outside'. Of course, the Android may also be acting as a router. So you could be behind triple NAT.

To get round this one can, from the router, (1) use a VPN service like StrongVPN which supplies a fixed public IP as part of its service and gives it to your VPN endpoint / client (the router), or (2) get your own VPS / server with a public IP and set it up yourself.

I did (1) then migrated to (2).
