Hi,

I have two compex WPJ558 boards with WLE900VX 802.11ac cards (2 antennas connected so 2x2 setting), and i'm trying to reach the maximum throughput.

I have installed the latest OpenWRT (trunk version) on both. The setting is :
- one board as (WDS) AP
- the other board as (WDS too) STA
- two computer connected to each board (Gigabit ethernet)
- iperf (UDP) to get the speed

I get a maximum of 550 Mbps (average is about 450-500) despite a theoretical 780Mbps max (mcs 9, 2 spatial streams, 80 MHz).

With the original CompexWRT firmware on both boards, I reach 650 Mbps average.

Is there something i could do to enhance my setting, or it is simply that the compexWRT driver perform better than ath10k, and i can't do much ?

I get around 450Mbit peak during real-world transfer to/from a Samba server using my laptop with an Intel 2x2 card connect to an Archer C7.

So I would have to agree that there seems to be some current limitations with the ath10k drivers as the speed was only about 80Mbit less using a 1x1 adapter.

That said, I do not trust iperf2 or iperf3 for benchmarking WiFi performance as it reports 300Mbit doing the TCP test and I can't get the UDP test to work reliably at all, showing anything from failed, 1Mbit or 100Mbit.
