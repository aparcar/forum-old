Hey there.

Your image is actually bettern than your description.

Your description says you put 192.168.1.1 as IP address for both routes.
Your image says the second one has 192.168.1.2 instead.
Do it the way your image says: Use differen IP addresses for both routes.

Disable DHCP on your Linksys. That's correct. Don't do anything with DHCP relay.

Your description says you want to have 192.168.1.[2-100] as IP range provided by your Livebox.
Make sure the IP address of your second router is *not* part of that range, so better go 192.168.1.[100-250] which leaves you room for other devices with static address in the lower part of the range.

Connect both devices by RJ45.
That's exactly the way to go. This makes DHCP, DNS and routing from your Livebox available within the network spanned by the Linksys device.

Currently you use different SSIDs for your routers.
I wouldn't do that. Use the same SSID and the same encryption credentials on both. Your Wifi clients don't need to know about different stations, they should be able to connect to the first fitting station.

You didn't tell anything about Wifi channels. Make sure to use different ones for both routers. If you use the same channel in both devices, they will interfere.

Regards,
Stephan.

Your text says that they are both 192.168.1.1, that will not work.  Picture shows the second one is 192.168.1.2 which is fine.  Your setup is the standard way to do it.  You can give them both the same SSID (wifi name) if you want.  Devices should connect to whichever one they find stronger when they initially connect.

Channel selection should also consider what any nearby neighbors use.  Take a survey for example with Wifi Analyzer (on Android).
