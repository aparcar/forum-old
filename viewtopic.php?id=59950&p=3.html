The first, WL-330NUL, is more expensive and less powerful than the 6414, AR150, and hootoo TM02, and probably not much smaller.  Also the GL-iNet devices come with OpenWRT pre-installed, and the hootoo is very painless to convert to OpenWRT.

The second is a usb dongle, but requires host software.  It is meant to be plugged into a computer or small embedded computer (a OpenWRT router, included; check out RangerZ's thread on his Edimax).  It requires much more from the USB port than just power, and probably won't meet your needs.  I suppose it is conceivable that a stand alone router could be that small, but it would lack hard-wire connectivity, so could only be configured wirelessly. 

But who knows.  Things will likely get cheaper, smaller, and more powerful.  I envision this thread documenting that progress going forward.
