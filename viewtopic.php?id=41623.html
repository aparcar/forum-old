Hi,
I've just bought a TL-WDR3600 to be able to reuse some old drives I had around, thanks to its advertised "Local Storage" feature...
however, as it is, it's a borderline scam as NTFS speeds are a measly 3 mb/s write and 7 mb/s read, which makes it 100% useless as a "big file repo" as I intended!

Formatting the drive as FAT32 helped a bit as now sequential transfers manage to reach a 13 mb/s speed both writing and reading, but it's still far worse than the drive speed which is around 20 mb/s... and now it doesn't support big files at all.
I've also tried formatting the drive to ext2,3 and 4 but none was supported, apparently.

I don't think the bottleneck is the USB 2.0 as it's max should be around 40, and when connected to my pc the drive can still reach max speed... it can't be the LAN itself too, as it reached 110 mb/s with SSD to SSD.
So, I guess it has to be one between a really slow CPU, not enough RAM, a terrible USB2.0 implementation, or maybe all 3.

So, do you think that switching to OpenWRT and formatting the drive with something like Ext4 would help reaching the drive max speed?
Has anyone attempted sharing volumes with this router at all, or it was just a really bad idea on my part to fall for it?

Thanks!

EDIT: just to clarify, I only used the stock firmware for now!
