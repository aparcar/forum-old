EDIT: Okay, mystery solved. Somehow the firmware on the router corrupted itself, not sure how or if this happened the exact moment he unplugged it the last time a few years ago...
I had to open it up, solder connectors to the board and get a serial connection up and running, then found out that the router ran into a Kernel Panic just after flashing the 1234 LAN and WAN LEDs. I flashed a trimmed down stock firmware onto the router via Serial and TFTP then and got it up and running again.
