KHAN85
13 Jun 2015, 22:24

You need 3 AP's in order to get a more accurate position (in 3d), with a only one AP you'll get the "radius" from the AP.

Imagine a imaginary sphere with the center in every AP of room and a radius proportional to RSSI of the client device.
The points where match 3 imaginary spheres is the most accurate position of the client device.

Wifi drivers aren't generally able to make precise timing or phase measurements to get an accurate position or distance between the AP and client.  This is mostly because wifi clients don't transmit signals with precise timing or phase.  About the best you can do is guess at the radius based on signal strength.
