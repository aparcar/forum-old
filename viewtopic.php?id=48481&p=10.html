emin wrote:

I have upgraded my wt1520 with the image downloaded from http://downloads.openwrt.org/snapshots/ … pgrade.bin

First of all: The first OpenWrt firmware you flash should always be the factory variant. sysupgrade is only for upgrading OpenWrt at a later time.

After rebooting, the system is up and running doing routing and broadcasting ssid

That would be a strong indication that OpenWrt has not actually been installed; Wifi is deactivated in OpenWrt by default. You are probably still running the manufacturer firmware.

Post #242
metai
10 Apr 2015, 20:20
hackru wrote:

to everyone: please do not order 1520, instead order 3020. they priced identically(+-) but 3020 have modern low power consumption chip. 3020 supported in openwrt too.

Not to mention the WT3020 (except the first revision) comes with 8 instead of 4 MB flash memory, which helps a LOT in OpenWrt, especially with trunk builds.

That being said, the WT1520 is very stable and works just fine with OpenWrt. I myself am using it exclusively as an "ethernet condom" (essentially just the default firewall/NAT between WAN and LAN) for my notebook whenever I don't particularly trust the wired network I'm connecting to. Aside from it getting a bit warmer to the touch than other devices, I can't say that it draws an excessive amount of power.
