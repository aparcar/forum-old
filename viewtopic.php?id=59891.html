In case you need the 3G Stick and the second wifi at the same time, a dual band router may be a better solution.
But there is yet another way: get a USB-WLAN that works with OpenWRT. In case you need directional radio this may be a problem, since many cheap USB-WLAN sticks cannot be configured to use lower output power settings. (E.g. rtl8188 based 2.4GHz USB-sticks with detachable antennas tend to deliver constant 16dB, diminished by approx 1dB for the connector and increased by 3-5 dB antenna gain. = 19-20dB EIRP.). Such a device could not be used for the following setup without violating regulatory rules of the harbour's national laws.
If the stick can regulate it's sending power, there are dishes on the market, that allow you to plug the stick itself in as the dish's feed.
That way you can use either the usb-wlan as wan (in ap station mode) or the 3G Stick as a USB-WWAN, but not a the same time.
The internal radio of the router will serve the clients on board of your vessel.
This may consume about 18 Watt or lower, that the TL-WR1043NDv1 power supply is delivering, while giving a maximum of performance with minimum of means. You could also use a TL-WR842ND, but that model isn't produced any no longer. It doesn't use Gigabit ports and is therefore equipped only with a 12 Watt power supply.
