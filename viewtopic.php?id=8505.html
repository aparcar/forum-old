Many people seem to expect to load a 5,000 kilo cargo onto a VW beetle. It's simply not made for it.   If you break the cargo down into smaller chunks at a time you can do it.

To expect a cheap $50 router, to hold up under heavy demands from an un-named P2P client running on a probably very expensive and fast PC, is not reasonable.   With P2P software, the first place to look is choosing good software, and then configuring it to NOT open too many connections at once, and for it to NOT try and use 150% of your connection.   I strictly limit my P2P app to a small number of connections and 50% of my bandwidth, the upload limit matters most.  Reading the FAQ, forums, and documentation for your P2P software is a good place to find this information.  Not this forum.

Same with me; I have Azeureus running full-bore on two machines, and run a samba server for media, provide SSH and OpenVPN access.  There's 22 torrents running on this machine (who knows about the other -- it's not my machine), and am able to browse the web fine as I write this.  Also, I have 7 files being copied to the USB storage over samba.  This is on my WL500GP box, though I was able to do this (sans the VPN) on my wl54gl as well.

The key was in both the QoS scripts, and setting several parameters in the proc sys net ipv4 stuff.  For the QoS scripts I used rudy's qos-re.  You have to adjust those to your environment.  For the tweaking of some netfilter settings I added the following to my /etc/sysctl.conf:

net.ipv4.ip_conntrack_max=2048
net.ipv4.netfilter.ip_conntrack_tcp_timeout_established=120
net.ipv4.netfilter.ip_conntrack_udp_timeout_stream=120
net.ipv4.tcp_westwood=1

This gave an immediate improvement in things like sporadically dropped connections/connection timeouts while doing heavy p2p.
