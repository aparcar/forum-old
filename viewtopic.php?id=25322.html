If your cable modem uses PPPoE, then you will need to configure your WAN on your OpenWrt router to authenticate to your ISP with your PPPoE username/password.  Otherwise, you should just be able to set your WAN to DHCP to automatically obtain an IP address from your ISP.  Your LAN should be set to a static IP (usually 192.168.1.1) and act as a DHCP server (to assign your network nodes an IP address automatically).  Then you should have internet from any of the 4 ethernet ports.

Your OpenWrt router should have a static IP address of 192.168.1.1 by default.  You can connect to the router from a client computer via telnet/SSH or HTTP (if you have LuCI or webif installed).  Your client computers should be assigned an address via DHCP of 192.168.1.XXX (e.g. 192.168.1.100).
