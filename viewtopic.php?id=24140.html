Hello all,

I am trying to interface an LCD display (http://www.crystalfontz.com/product/CFA632-YMC-KS) to a Wiligear WBD-500 board through the GPIO lines. I got it to work using the serial line without much difficultly; however, I want to use the serial lines to communicate with a PIC microcontroller. I've seen packages to use the GPIO lines as an SPI interface, but nothing to make it a pseudo rs232 line.

So, has anyone does this before? Is it really even feasible, or would the timings be too far off even at 2400 baud?

For short bursts of communication, you can probably create a software UART in a kernel module, by disabling interrupts and then doing your stuff, but your clock (and possibly other stuff) will get messed up if you take too long.

You can also use realtime extensions (https://www.rtai.org/) to give yourself a periodic task.  At low baud speeds the interrupt jitter will probably be small enough for it to work.
