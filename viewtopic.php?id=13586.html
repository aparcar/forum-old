it's actually very simple. telnet 192.168.1.1 9000 within 5 seconds of boot and load your kernel and root filesystem images. There is a procedure in the wiki. (no you cannot upload a .bin file, like you did with dd-wrt).

I've switched from dd-wrt to openwrt, never looked back.

Post #4

30 Nov 2007, 21:00

you install it once you've booted up for the first time and logged on with ssh (download putty) simply by typing 'ipkg install webif'. You will need to manually modify your network interfaces to get your fonera on the internet. All configuration is stored files in /etc/config/. Read the wiki for further guidance on setting everything up once you've booted your fonera for the first time.
