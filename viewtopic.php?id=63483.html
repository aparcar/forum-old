I am not a genius nor a hardware or firmware hacker/expert.  But I have read lots of threads on these boards with problems.

Maybe you overwrote essential partitions and data on your router's flash RAM by making a mistake when you did your first unsuccessful flash?  Then because of this every later flash seems unsuccessful -- it didn't "take" -- when you reboot, and this never changes.

If it appears dead, no tftp connection to upload stock firmware to restore the router (which doesn't seem to be your case ), maybe the u-boot partition got messed up.  Also, the ART partition can get overwritten if you make a bad mistake flashing.  See:

https://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/techref/flash.layout

https://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/howto/rest … _partition

There could be other flash RAM problems or data overwritten but I'm not enough of an expert to diagnose this.  I don't think the above pages will tell you how to fix your problem.  But you may learn what questions you need to ask and what documentation you need to read

The fact that you have connected to the serial terminal for boot logs and console control is a BIG step forward.  If you ask the right questions politely, real experts will read and answer.  Good luck!

Clemmitt

P.S.: The default manufacturer IP address is 192.168.0.1, but make sure to check 192.168.1.1, etc., with ping.  Any replies?  Learn how to use nmap to scan an IP subnet, e.g., 'nmap -sn 192.168.0.0/24'.  Do the serial port boot logs give you any info about default IP addresses or tftp service connections and IP addresses?

Have you cleared your browser's cache?  Clear it *completely* to make sure then try again.  These are simple things to try just in case it's something minor (which it probably isn't ).

http://www.dd-wrt.com/phpBB2/viewtopic.php?t=85237

They work.  If version 3, I can't find a stripped a.k.a. webrevert file.  Maybe an expert on here can help.  They're supposed to be easy to strip with dd on UNIX but I don't want to do that and give you a bad flash file.  OTOH things may not get any worse.

DON'T flash any stock firmware that isn't stripped or webrevert.  If the TP-Link filename has "_boot" in it it's bad and needs to be stripped before flashing!  Please BE CAREFUL!

Thanks for pointing me to the serial console "erase" command   I am still learning this stuff.

tftp 0x80060000 firmware.bin

Info on backing up and restoring ART partition (just one of multiple flash partitions), and on flash partitions:

https://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/howto/rest … _partition

https://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/techref/flash.layout

You will have to read and study these pages as well as others you need while you're in the process of learning how to fix your problem.  Most experienced users are not willing to hold your hand every step of the way while you work on this.  Being patient, reading, studying and learning is the most important thing in solving a problem like this.  Please be diligent!  Take the time and effort necessary.

Since you didn't make a backup you'll have to ask for someone else who uses a TP-Link WR1043ND v2.1 router to make backups of the partitions and post or e-mail them to you.  I recommend starting a new thread.  Please ask politely   Explain that you need flash partition backups for this model and version router.  Hopefully someone with the necessary experience will take the time to help you.

Then you need additional understanding or help.  At the very least you need to reset the MAC address(es) in the ART partition backup image to agree with your router's factory settings.  Once modified to work on your router -- which may be difficult or involve some small amount of guesswork -- you need to upload and flash.  Follow the documentation.  Just restoring the ART partition may fix your problem.  Or perhaps some of the u-boot and its environment flash memory is corrupted, too.  That is probably more complicated to fix???  I'm not sure how to flash those to the proper memory locations.  I'm out of my league here.

You might also politely ask for partition backups for your model/version on the dd-wrt Atheros board.  There are real experts who follow that project, too.

Remember, I'm not a flash memory or device expert.  I'm just helping you as best I understand.  There may be another way to correct things because I don't understand the problem fully.  Sorry I can't be of more help.  If no one with more experience than me replies on the boards or the IRC channel, you're on your own
