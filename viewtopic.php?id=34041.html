AR9331 is quite new. But I guess there will be a lot of new devices. e.g. TP-link is using it for their "travel-routers" like the wr703 and this soc is used in newer revisions of some of their normal routers  e.g. wr741nd wr740n.
I have a bunch of this devices running and they are working quite well, and they are quite cheap (15-20euro). But maybe a take a look into the RT3050, and try one of these.

hi,

tp-link seems to release all sources that are under the gpl. (take a look on their homepage under support - gpl

and I don't own a plug-device, but I own a wr703n (https://forum.openwrt.org/viewtopic.php?id=32888) and it really small and I have no thermal problems at all, actually the case get only a little warm.

ap- and station-mode works with out any problem, I didn't try WDS, repeater or multi-ssid but it should be possible as well.

But be aware that you need at least 4MB Flash and 16MB RAM to run openwrt on it. some have only 2MB Flash
