Post #9
barryware
9 Jun 2009, 19:59

I do not have just the cfe. I picked up that wholeflash when I was working on a V3.1. I own a V2 but the cfe's are not compatable.

If your flash is stalling at 3%:

erase wholeflash

tjtag -erase:wholeflash /noemw (twice)

now without power cycling the device flash the wholeflash

-tjtag -flash:wholeflash /noewm /noreset /nobreak

If it stalls at "clearing watchdog", add the /nocwd switch.

See if that helps.

If you have flashed a cfe, the default IP is 192.168.1.245. If you flash dd-wrt (micro only), then the ip will be 192.168.1.1 after the flash. dd-wrt does not change the cfe on a wap. If you watch arp while the wap is booting, it will start at .245, then once the fw takes over, it is now .1

And... If you put dd-wrt on the box from linksys fw, you just flash through the upgrade gui, you do not need jtag.

My hands on experience is only with a V2. It could be that a V1 is different.. Don't think so though.
