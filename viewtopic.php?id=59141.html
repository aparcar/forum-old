Alas, if only that were the case. For my Belkin router, a bunch of bad things happen if it is disconnected from the Internet:
When off the Net:

1. On a PC if you connect to the router, it actually puts up a page that indicates the router is not on the Internet, AND it then shows the Router password so you can correct the password. Want to hack into a Belkin router? Just un-plug it from the Internet, do an initial WiFi connection... and there's the router password shown on the page.
2. Android devices refuse connection altogether.
3. The router drops all WiFi connections at about 2 minute intervals, and then re-connects.

I called Belkin Tech Support to discuss, and they assured me, for their consumer and industrial products, all their routers work that way: they must have a connection to the Internet to work properly.

If you have knowledge of another vendor's products that do not have these types of issues while not connected to the Internet, please do share!

Thanks.
