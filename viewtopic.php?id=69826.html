There are lots of ways to brick your router, the good thing about some current linksys routers like the wrt series is that they have 2 partitions, so pretty much impossible to brick. To boot into the other partition if you can't ssh, you use the 3 failed boot method, meaning you turn it on, wait a few seconds and then turn it off before it successfully boots, do that 3 times and on the 4th time leave it turned on and hopefully you'll be in the working partition, so -

on/off

on/off

on/off

on/leave on

It might take a few tries to get the timing right, so if it doesn't work the first time, try again.

As for your leds, there were some problems with the leds on the wrt series and the 3200 had the most problems from what i remember, they were working on it over at lede but i don't know what the situation at openwrt is with the leds on the wrt series. In Luci you can go to System>Led Configuration and try to set up the leds there, it's safer that way than going into configs.

edit - since it just kills the wifi you don't need to bother with the 3 failed boots

adri
14 Feb 2017, 14:00

Firmware flash always goes to the non-active partiton.
So if you have OpenWrt active, it will overwrite the Linksys partition.
If you have Linksys active, it will overwrite the OpenWrt partition.
You can force a switch to the non active partition, by using the 'failsafe' power trick.

  • Power on, wait for 1 or 2 seconds then power off.

  • Repeat another 2 times.

  • Then power on normally and it should boot from the other partition.
