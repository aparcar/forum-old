I was thinking about adding "a OpenWrt recipes" page in the wiki where someone with experience (or anyone really) could put down a bunch of lists of packages needed to make images with imagebuilder in a way to make firmwares that have the usual flash chip mb sizes.

As it is now, making a firmware is mostly down to guesswork and trial-and-error to figure out if I can fit "my favourite list of packages" in X mb of flash size, which is tedious as %$££, even while the autobuild/image-maker tools make this a pretty easy and painless process from the compilation side of things.

Like the one you find in the extroot (root swap page), about a VERY minimal image to get a working firmware that can extroot.
( https://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/howto/extroot )

An example list:

"recommended 4mb wifi-router firmware"

"recommended 4mb firmware for extroot"

"recommended 8mb wifi-router firmware with X but without Y" (for example: a more media-oriented firmware)

"recommended 8mb wifi-router firmware with Y but without X" (another example: a more hardcore-networking firmware with wifi mesh and whatever)

Also "modules" like "+8mb of filesharing and downloaders by user1" (assorted to the liking of "user1"), or "+6mb linux filesystems and utilities", that are modules added on top of an already working firmware.

Doing something like this is more flexible and free than just making a ton of different or maybe conflicting metapackages like I've seen with some of the more obvious stuff (USB) where it actually makes sense.

This wiki page would mimick a bit dd-wrt's own page you can see here, where they standardized things much more. http://www.dd-wrt.com/wiki/index.php/Wh … d_Features

Ah, this would also help out a lot people that want to buy a router and aren't already OpenWRT gurus.
