VLAN-stuff get important if you have multiple accesspoints and you want to route the traffic in a secure way to your mainrouter (with the internet-uplink). In this case it would be a good idea to have 2 VLANs on your LAN to separate the "privat"- and "guest"-traffic over one ethernet-cable. This would increase the security and I would really recommend this. 
If you have only one router which works as  private- and guest-accesspoint you don't need to deal with VLANs. If you follow the documentation you will get a guest-wlan which is a separate virtual wifi-interface which is not bridged to your lan (and private wlan). So it's not on the same lan-segment as your private network. the firewall-rules are only needed to allow internet-access and you have to set this up even if you use VLANs.

So VLANs are usefull for a "guest-lan" or if you want to integrate many guest-accesspoints in your lan in a secure way, but there is no advantage if you want to setup up a guest-wlan on your main router.

In contrast to NICs, WNICs can operate in multiple modes.

If you want to imprison your "guest" in an own network, and they connect over the wireless, simply make your WNIC spawn a second AP for your "guest", then configure ip addresses and also, adjust your firewall.
