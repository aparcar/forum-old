TheRealTachyon
11 Jun 2014, 18:43

I've been lurking WRT1900AC related topics all over the net for a while now and the situation is pretty disappointing. Like an idiot, I bought one based on Linksys' promises of OpenWRT compatibility without verifying that at the OpenWRT site. <sigh>

The current situation is especially frustrating because you can just feel the potential of the hardware on this device. With the stock firmware it's blazing fast and the wireless is amazing. The real kick in the nuts though is the factory firmware. It's so feature crippled and dumbed down as to be insulting. How could they ship such a feature lacking, dumbed down firmware to the type of customer that would want a $300 router promised to be capable of alt firmware? It's like some kind of horrible bait and switch. Hell, you can't even check (let alone set) AP Isolation.

OK, all that said is to lead up to this. And this is going to make some people mad, but here it goes.

I'd much rather have a WRT1900AC with OpenWRT that runs perfect, fast, and stable and doesn't give up any speed or features but uses a binary blob than have something hacked together that gives up performance, features and stability.
Of course the ideal solution is for Marvell to release a fully working, no compromise, source driver for the 88W8864. However if that weren't to happen, I'd much rather integrate a binary blob than either a crippled F/OSS driver from Marvell or some other workaround from the community. It's not a perfect F/OSS world and there are not perfect solutions. Hope for the best, but plan for the worst.
With that in mind, if Linksys/Belkin fail to get Marvell to commit to releasing the driver, is there anyone willing to negotiate with them to build blobs configured for current OpenWRT kernels?
I don't care if this results in an offsite, unofficial OpenWRT fork for the WRT1900AC. I'd rather have a working device than a $300 heartache.

Thoughts?
