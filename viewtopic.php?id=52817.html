after some struggles (i.e. flashing the wrong firmware) i now have an open-wrt derivative (superwrt) running on my tp-link 3420 board.
I am planning on installing a long range Wifi-Bridge in an uninhabited area (7km - line of sight) using a 0,5m Yagi antenna as well as a parabolic antenna (on the "receiving" end).
At some point i had a test setup running with
- my TP link 3420 on the transmitting side - running an openwrt build with LuCI and TX power of definitely more than 20 dBm
- my other TP-Link 1043ND on the "receiving" side attached to the parabolic antenna and also transmitting at 501 mW (27dBm)

for some reason the maximum TX i can set now on my 3420 is 20 dBm when creating 1 WIFI, when i have 2 running the maximum tx is reduced to 18 dBm. i have tried setting the country to Bolivia - no change. i have tried to disable internal antennas the Router doesnt have - no change.
does anyone have ideas as to how to increase the tx power?
