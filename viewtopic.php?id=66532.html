Bracco, I agree that you should stay away from TP-Link as a brand unless there is an overwhelming reason to do so. The lockdown isn't "coming", it's already here. TP-Link has already issued firmwares which have intentionally done what they can to lock out modders. Their recently released Archer c2600 removed the level-shifter needed to make the TTL-serial port work, making console access difficult.

That D-Link DIR-860L uses Mediatek chips (not OSS friendly), and AC is only supported on 2.4Ghz. That doesn't look like a good router. On the up-side, it's super cheap and console looks easy to get.

You didn't say what your needs/requirements were. USB? Flash space? You did say you wanted 1000BASE-T.

The WZR-600DHP2 doen't support 802.11ac, so I'd not consider it.



Since I don't know what you want, I can't advise anything. Look for Atheros/QCA chips for the best support. There are a bunch of routers based on QCA's recent chips ("AC2600 routers"). I am currently working on trying to get the TRENDnet TEW-827DRU supported, but it will be a few more weeks likely. There is also the Linksys EA8500 and a couple of Netgear models priced around $150 USD, though the Netgears are a bit more expensive (better hardware).

I am personally biased againt the Linksys 1900ACS and the modern Linksys brand for what should be well-known reasons.
