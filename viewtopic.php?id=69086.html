Hi OpenWRT devs and users!

Short version: dnmasq 2.73rc6, which ships with Chaos Calmer 15.05.1 r48532, has a bug in it that causes a crash, and I'm wondering whether I should build my own version of CC, wait for a new CC image, or start using a nightly snapshot.

Long version:

I recently upgraded to Chaos Calmer, and I decided to add some DNS-based ad blocking to make my home network browsing experience smoother.

Fast forward a few days - my wife and I notice that our network will occasionally "crash" - we can't open new connections, and new devices can't connect to the network.  Unsurprisingly, when I log onto the router I discover that dnsmasq has crashed, so I start it back up and everything works again.  After some investigation, I was able to determine that it has something to do with empty AAAA records that are also present in my adblock blacklist - the problem is present in the dnsmasq included in Chaos Calmer (2.73rc6) and is fixed in dnsmasq 2.76 (specifically commit 41a8d9e).

(End Long Version)

So now I'm at a bit of a crossroads - I would like to maintain my ad blocking setup, but I would also like dnsmasq to stay running.  I see there haven't been any new releases for Chaos Calmer since it first came out, but I'm a little nervous to use a nightly snapshot since they're experimental and I would prefer something more stable.  Should I just build my own image of CC with some patches to dnsmasq, or should I just bite the bullet and move up to nightly?

Thanks!
