Catfish wrote:

The problem is that the WD drive, like any hard-disk drive, needs much more power than a USB flash-memory stick or a phone. Your Windows and Wii devices are capable of providing the necessary power to the USB bus, but the WGT634U is not.

If you can find a powered USB hub - a hub which has its own power supply - it will probably provide enough power to make your WD drive work with the router. Otherwise, I don't think you're going to get this drive attached to the router.

This is a pretty good explanation of USB power limits: Your USB device has exceeded the power limits...
