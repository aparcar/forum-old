So a few weeks ago I was configuring my router (TP-Link MR3220) for my flat's internet (provided by my university, I'm living in halls of residence). Turns out the internet provider does weird things with MAC filtering and NAT detection, which meant when I plugged in my router and ssh'd into it, I could ping sites like google and openwrt.org. Because of the NAT detection, I couldn't do that from my laptop.

This confused me. A lot.

At the time, I thought the OpenWRT firewall was to blame, and that the best solution for this would be to repeatedly click things until it worked.

Long story short, I set the firewall up so that nothing from the LAN can access the router. The worst part is that the reset button isn't working (this is a known bug/lack of implementation) so I can't get the router into failsafe.

Since then, I did lots of googling and talking to campus IT support and figured out why connecting didn't work. I've bought a WR-1043ND and, now it's set up right, that works perfectly.

Trouble is, I now have my original wireless router in a box, bricked. I'm not a fan of leaving hardware around to die, so I'm trying to get it working again.

So far, I've checked the following:
Portscanning the WAN port - all ports are closed.
Wireshark on the WAN port - My laptop gives the router an IP address and it proceeds to do NTP stuff. Nothing I can hijack though :s
Portscanning the LAN port - nothing. all ports are filtered [firewall]
Wireshark on the LAN port - My laptop also gives the router an IP (it was configured to be just a dumb AP, not using NAT, so the LAN doesn't have a DHCP server) but past that, nothing
Various combinations of pressing buttens (reset button & the QSS button) to no avail.

Does anyone else have any other suggestions? I don't have the kit to connect to the serial port, but if I need to I could try to borrow it.

Thanks in advance!
