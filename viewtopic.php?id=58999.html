Latrina wrote:

I am wondering if anybody knows what is the resistor voltage I need to solder. I read of somebody guessing 10k ohms, but I'd rather be sure and avoid frying it up.

10k seems the right value for other routers, so it should be fine. Since you use the routers Vcc, which is 3,3 V, you can't damage the device by using any other resistance, as that is the same voltage of the serial adapter. The resistance mainly matters to meet the voltage levels that the router can sense as distinct high or low.
