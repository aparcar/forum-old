Post #4
RangerZ
4 Nov 2016, 17:56

There are extremely slim pickings in the device class you want. 

The only currently produced 3g/4g device with a battery that I know of is the GLi MIFI.

The Turris Omnia and the PC_Engines APU2 (http://pcengines.ch/pdf/apu2.pdf) both have sim card slots on the MOBO, but neither a battery or portable.  The APU is 12V, the Turris is not speced.  Both will require a PCI-E card. like the MIFI.

The amount of RAM you want is excessive for a consumer router's typical tasks. You are really looking for a business class device that has a 4G backup to get more horse power.

Do you need RAM or can you use external storage?

A big cost of the GLI is the PCI-e modem card ($50).  These are a rare low volume product as compared to a USB modem and there for are expensive.  If you shop the Sierra Wireless cards they are 2-3 times the price, but do cover more bands.

With out the card the device is competitive with most of the other battery powered routers.  Hootoo a little less, Kingston a bit more.
