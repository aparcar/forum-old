  1. You should use your second router as AP, of course, because it performs routing. You can configure firewall on it to enable management via WAN interface.

  2. AP, because bridge just connects two interfaces directly as switch.

  3. http://www.electronicsfaq.com/2016/05/c … o-use.html It is OK, but autostart of OpenVPN connection is performed rather clumsy - see my manual. See also my manual to prevent traffic leakage outside the tunnel.

You can also use just one router, in this case you can configure two subnets - one for direct connection, second for VPN.
