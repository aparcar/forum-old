To answer your 1st question: No, you do not need aircrack-ptw for aircrack-ng.  Aircrack-ptw is not part of the aircrack-ng suite.

Your problem with installing aircrack-ng is you don't have enough space left on your jffs partition.  You may be able to make it fit if you remove other packages... or build your own image and remove unnecessary packages to make it lighter.

Alternatively, you could install aircrack-ng on your USB flash drive (or whatever is mounted at /mnt/point - looks like this is what you were trying to do).  You can have opkg install packages at alternative locations (other than root).  Check out this wiki (scroll down to "Installing and using OPKG packages in mount point other than root"):

http://wiki.openwrt.org/oldwiki/usbstoragehowto

As LinkZ posted the easiest way (possible only way) to fix the spaceissue is to build your own image.
So I would recommend you create your own squashfs-image, disable everthing you don't need (luci etc.) and select aircrack-ng
Aircrack-ng supports some sort of "drone"-mode (at least in backfire), so you don't need space for captured packages on your router.
Just run "airserv-ng" on your router which opens an tcp-port and run everthing else on your workstation and instead of a local wifi-interface connect to <routerip>:666

for example: airodump <routerip>:666
