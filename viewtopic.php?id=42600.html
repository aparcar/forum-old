I have tried many loads and cannot figure out why I cannot telnet to my TL-WR703N v1.7. My laptop has a static IP of 192.168.1.100 with a mask of  255.255.255.0 and the laptop's eth0 Ethernet cable is directly connected to the TL-WR703N's Ethernet port. My current TL-WR703N load is 'openwrt-ar71xx-generic-tl-wr703n-v1-squashfs-factory.bin' from 'http://downloads.openwrt.org/attitude_a â€¦ x/generic/' but I've tried numerous other binaries from multiple places. As soon as the load is finished (I am loading via serial) I attempt to "telnet 192.168.1.1" and it fails "Unable to connect to remote: no route to host". I have tested SSH to another Linux box (passworded so need ssh) with success so the laptop's network setup is ok. I have tried multiple Ethernet cables, even a turnover cable. I cannot figure out why I cannot telnet to a virgin OpenWRT TL-703N load when everyone else seems to be able to do so. Any ideas? Help will be much appreciated as this is driving me nuts.

SOLVED - Never mind. Apparently I was a victim of the v1.7 eth bug. I just installed 'openwrt-ar71xx-generic-tl-wr703n-v1-squashfs-factory.bin' as posted by nebbia88 on December 30th, which has the Ethernet fox for v1.7. As soon as I installed this telnet worked!

A question, however - are there ANY builds in the main distribution that will work with v1.7?
