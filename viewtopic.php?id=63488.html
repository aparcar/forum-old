It says the default interfaces for my router are: br-lan, eth0, eth1 (WAN), wlan0 and wlan1

However when I installed firmware all I got showing is br-lan, and two occurrences of eth1 one is for WAN and the other is WAN6

Question is I dont understand why it has two occurrences for WAN, both are of eth1, same MAC address etc. If I stop the WAN6 one I still have internet so can i just delete this interface?

Also what does it mean when it has a /24 after the ip address. eg my ip address for the actual router is set to 192.168.0.1 but it has /24 at the end. What is the 24 for? ta

The WAN6 interface is for IPv6. If your ISP doesn't support IPv6, the WAN6 interface isn't going to do anything for you. On the other hand, its also not doing you any harm. Leave it there and you won't have to recreate it when/if your ISP adds IPv6 support.

As for the /24, that is the IPv4 prefix size, which is basically the subnet mask.  It means that the first 24 bits of the 32 bit IP address describe the network, and the last designates the host. So, your router is in host position 1 of the 192.168.0 network, and there are 254 other possible host addresses on that network (255 is reserved for broadcasts to all hosts on the subnet).
