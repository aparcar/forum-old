Hi. I'm getting a family member a new 802.11n network, and since I've had good experiences with OpenWRT in my own (802.11g) network, I'll be looking for something that works with OpenWRT in this case aswell.

Below is a list of N-hardware that is easily accessible at my geographical location, and also in the "Supported" section of the TOH. I'd appreciate any suggestions, thoughts or experiences regarding the hardware listed (or recommendations outside the list). The unit will only be used as a WLAN access point and a wired router coming in to the house (no file serving or anything like that). Stability and transfers speeds matter the most, and I'd like to use Backfire (preferably released versions, but I can use trunk if neccessary). Since this is not for my own use, I'd like something that's easily flashable without opening the container and soldering. Radio conditions could be a bit problematic, so a good radio is preferable.

Easily available hardware in my price range:
* D-Link DIR-615
* [s]D-Link DIR-300[/s] (Oops, this isn't an n-unit)
* (Netgear WNR3500L, listed as WIP)
* Linksys WRT160NL (listed under supported, but wiki page says "work in progress" - comment?)
* TP-Link TL-WR1043ND
* D-Link DIR-825 (a bit in the high end of my price range, but if it's good...)
* Netgear WNDR3700 (a bit in the high end of my price range, but if it's good...)

Did you ever consider an inexpensive Seagate DockStar that can be turned into even a 3G/4G WiFi router using a 3G/4G USB2 WiFi dongle through one of its USB2 port? You can even hook gIGE switch to its 10/100/1000Mbps RJ45 port to share the connection. The best part of this is this device come with a whoping 128MB RAM. With that amount of RAM, the router will operate smoother, let alone to host a lot of applications, i.e. Asterisk / FreeSWITCH PBX System, etc.

My first choice would be the Netgear WNDR3700.  It's a great N router that works well with OpenWrt, but may be more than you need for your setup.  The best value is the TP-Link TL-WR1043ND.  It also works well with OpenWrt, but costs considerably less.

In case someone comes across this post and wonders about my conclusions: I ended up with the WNDR3700. It's now running 10.03.1-rc4 and seems to be working great. It's only been stress-tested for 12 hours, but both radios seem to be working perfectly.

Thanks a bunch to all the people behind OpenWRT - you've really done a great job! Thanks also to the people suggesting hardware in this thread.
