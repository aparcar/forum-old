I think the previous answers misunderstood the question. Somebody who's more versed than me please correct me if I'm wrong. But I'll go out on a limb here and say:

dpsi wrote:

If I let a client that is behind a firewall dial into my VPN server, could the computers on the server side network access the computers on the client side?

Computers, as in plural: No, not out of the box.

Of course, it can access and receive an answer from the VPN client machine itself, that is kind of the purpose, and it goes both ways unless the services on the client machine are configured to answer on the local network interface only.

However, other machines in the client's network would not know about the VPN tunnel that a single machine in their network opened. They would probably be able to receive requests that originated from the VPN tunnel, but they would not know where to send a response to a request to -- unless the individual machines (or their gateway) were deliberately instructed to send responses back through the VPN tunnel that was opened by the VPN client's machine.
