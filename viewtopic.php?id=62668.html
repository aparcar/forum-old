I want to replace an TL-WR841N/ND v9 running OpenWrt Barrier Breaker r40730 / LuCI Trunk (svn-r10180)
with a TL-WR1043ND *V3.0* running the latest OpenWrt trunk .
The WAN port is connected to an Arris cable modem, which gives one public IPv4-Address via DHCP.
Everything works for years with the TL-WR841N/ND v9.

The new TL-WR1043ND V3.0 doesn't get an IP-Address from the cable modem via DHCP. I have to remove  the cable of the WAN port for a short time to get an IP Address from the cable modem. I have to do this after every reboot and when the DHCP lease expires.

Connecting the WAN port of the TL-WR1043ND V3.0 to a LAN port of the old TL-WR841ND and it gets an IP-Address instantly (sure, 2 different Networks), even after a reboot.

The Warning/Gotchas in https://wiki.openwrt.org/toh/tp-link/tl-wr1043nd apply only to V1 and V2 of the TL-WR1043ND

The WAN port works, but not with the cable modem....
udhcpc parameters are the same on both routers.

What can I do for further investigations ?

I got another TP-Link TL-WR841N/ND v9 and flashed it with OpenWrt Designated Driver r48648. It works out of the box ..
It seems not to be an udhcpc-software problem.
