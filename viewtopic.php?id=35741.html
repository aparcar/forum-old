I have a Buffalo WZR-AGH300 with a USB2 disk connected. I also have a Mac Mini running OSX 10.6, connected via wired Ethernet to the Buffalo. I also-also have a Lenovo Thinkpad X201 running Fedora 16 connected via Wifi to to the Buffalo. Transfer speeds via SCP between the devices are thusly:

Copy files from USB2 on the Buffalo to the Mac: ~3.5MB/s
Copy files from the Mac to the Thinkpad: ~5.5MB/s
Copy files from USB2 on the Buffalo to the Thinkpad: ~500KB/s

I expect that the difference between the Buffalo to the Mac and the Mac to the Thinkpad is the difference of USB2 vs internal SATA, but I can't explain the transfers off the Buffalo to the Thinkpad. Anyone know where I could start figuring out why speeds from the Buffalo to the Thinkpad are so abysmal?

scp isn't the perfect tool the check wired or wirless performance. It isn't the right tool for file transfer either, if it should be fast, because scp encrypt the complete traffic and the cpu of the router is too slow for fast encyption.

so too your "test":

3.5MB/s is the performance of your routers cpu encrypting with AES for sending it through scp
5.5MB/s seems to be your wireless performance
the 500kB/s is your wireless performance when your routers CPU is maxed out.

So use iperf for connection testing and samba, nfs, or even ftp for filetransfer.
