My ISP speeds can fluctuate a lot, so standard QOS is not really useful for me. Setting the upload and download speeds to worst case scenario's results will cause my speeds to suffer during the rest of the day. Setting them too high will cause QOS not to work during lower speeds on the ISP side.

Gargoyle has this amazing thing called "Active Congestion Control" (ACC) which decides which upload/download speeds to use automatically based on your current ping. Does anyone know whether such a thing also exists for openwrt? Or whether I can use ACC on openwrt, since Gargoyle is based on openwrt?

I'd rather stick with openwrt because of the more advanced options and stuff that it offers, but I would really like to use the QOS software from Gargoyle.

Thank you very much in advance for the information

As much as I like to advertise sqm-scripts, it will not work for the OP, as it does not dynamically track the available bandwidth. I believe gargoyle does track the downstream bandwidth only. The experimental qdisc cake also promises to allow automatic shaper adjustment on the downstream, but cake is only included in a few community builds. I believe hnyman's build to be the one with the most recent cake (which can partly be controlled from sqm-scripts and luci-app-sqm. See https://forum.openwrt.org/viewtopic.php?id=28392 for his build, but be aware that it only targets a few router models. Arokh targets a few more models and also includes cake, if I recall correctly, but I am uncertain which cake version is included, see https://forum.openwrt.org/viewtopic.php?id=50914
