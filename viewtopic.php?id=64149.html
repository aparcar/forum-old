I have an ALIX and am very happy with it.  I do not run a wireless card in it, but use a separate "Dumb" AP. 

It's very unclear in the literature, but you need different cards for each band if you want dual band.  While the cards can run either band, they do not do it simultaneously.  You have two slots, but the case only two holes. Sure you can use a drill. 

I was also able to locate the router where the cable comes in and put the AP in a much better location for wireless coverage.

Post #4
RangerZ
13 Apr 2016, 14:18

I can not suggest any of the above cards from experience or readings. 

I would not suggest this device as a dumb AP.  It's only 100Mbps.  You should really try to run GB internally.     It makes a better Router than AP IMHO.as long as the router is faster than your ISP you should be OK.

You did not answer if you want single or dual band.  For dual band the price does not make sense (2 cards and 4 antenna).

I would not use trunk but choose one of these:
https://downloads.openwrt.org/chaos_cal … x86/geode/
https://downloads.openwrt.org/chaos_cal … x86/geode/

There are some concerns about 15.05.1.  Do some homework.  I am on 15.05 RC2 (older prerelease) and have not felt a need to upgrade.  If you have the spare CF card, just build both and then you can swap.  One of the nice things about this box is how easy it is to change the OS.

Size is not relevant.  It will fit on a 1 GB CF card with plenty of spare room.
