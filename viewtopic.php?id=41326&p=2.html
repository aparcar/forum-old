Hi,
How can I reset my MR3420 V2 from openwrt to TP-Link firmware?
THX,
R

Download firmware: http://www.tp-link.com/Resources/softwa … 121206.zip (the filename contains "boot" & the size=4063744)
So
Cut the first 0x20200 (that is 131,584 = 257*512) Bytes from original firmware
Linux: dd if=orig.bin of=tplink.bin skip=257 bs=512 (source: http://wiki.openwrt.org/toh/tp-link/tl-wr1043nd )
Win: Hexedit will do.
Flash the stripped file (size=3932160)
Voila.

R

Post #45
MBS
6 Feb 2013, 11:10

/tmp is a RAM-disk, so everything stored in there will be lost on reboot. There are different ways to increase the space for the rootfs. The most convenient way is to connect an usb flash drive and use extroot (see the wiki for more information), but this might not the most stable solution. Other options are to replace the flash chip with one of a bigger capacity (mostly known as flash mod), but this requires at least the ability to backup a few partitions from the original flash to the replacement one. A third option is to connect a sd card to some free gpios (mmc over gpio are the key words).
