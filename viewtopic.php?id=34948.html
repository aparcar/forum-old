Its interesting problem and I think its perfectly possible to completely switch internet access to 4G or even LTE .

Ad. 1  - LTE is a new thing and OpenWrt starts to support LTE modems. The problem lies not in OpenWrt per se but to properly switch modem to LTE communication. In Poland where LTE tests starts more than a Year ago  and Huawei E398 modem is widely available, its fully supported in OpenWrt. As You provide 4 USB dongles names - search forums for those modems and find which one is supported and properly switches itself to LTE.
Otherwise those LTE modems act as standard 3.5/3G device which, again, is perfectly supported by OpenWrt but transfers are slower (LTE =>3.5G=>3G=>2G=>56.6kbs ;-p )

Ad. 3 - Main factors in connection stability is signal strenght - I think, the CRUICAL factor is distance from a cell tower. In my office Ive tested many configurations and few GSM companies and its always the same - the closer to CELL tower the better. The problem is that its not directly connected to signal strengts reported by modem. Its complicated problem but to make it short, during signal strenght fluctuation algorithms adapt various parameters making internet data flow to become interuppted to some extend. Anyway - Ive ended-up having an GSM operator Cell tower about 100m away and clearly visible by modem - this finally made my connection rock solid. Additionally, I dont see any fluctuations (earlier and now) caused by weather, time of day etc. Its not like WiFi where a person in a room interfere with signal.
Additionally - above information is valid for HDSPA. I dont have LTE yet so it can be different kind. From the opinion on internet, LTE by default is more solid and more reliable.

Hope Youll find a way to cut Your cost soon.

PS. - also: check if GSM provider not block certain ports,  limit some protocols or use some kind of compressing technology. Ie. There are some providers which by default compress all transfer. In some cases its ok because you save some data but its not acceptable if You use 3G for professional activities. The solution is sometime to switch to alternative APN provided by them but You need to be sure its possible.
