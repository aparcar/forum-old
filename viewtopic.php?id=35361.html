It is easy, and your case is similar to mine.

There are two methods, just depends on what you need.
However your MR3220 seems missing the vlan settings, so only two ports are usable.
just take a look here : http://wiki.openwrt.org/toh/tp-link/tl-mr3420
*what is your physical settings in WAN ?

Here is the idea of the settings.
1) use your MR3220 as an AP,  I recommend this setting, so your network will still control by your main router.
- you don't need WAN interface in this case, so you can delete it or just left it as is.
- LAN IP, use DHCP (so IP will assign from your main router), or, static IP
- in LAN physical settings, bridge your LAN to eth0 (or the physical port which connect to your main router), and the wifi interface(7amada)
- don't enable DHCP server on LAN
- Save and Apply
your MR3220 LAN and wifi should have internet access through your main router,
and main router will assign IP to your wifi clients.


2) use MR3220 as a ordinary router.
- in WAN physical settings, bridge only eth0 or the physical or vlan port connect to your main router.
you can use DHCP or assign static IP which same subnet to your main router.
- set static IP to LAN with different subnet, enable DHCP server
- in LAN physical settings, bridge the wifi interface(s) and the LAN port(if needed wired connection)
- put LAN in LAN zone, WAN in WAN zone..
- save and apply

You can assign more than one wifi ssid if needed, such as 7amada 8amada etc....
