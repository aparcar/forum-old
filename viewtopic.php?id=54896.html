First of all: I am not familiar with "SuperWrt". It seems to be a custom build with multiple preinstalled/preconfigured packages, much like some Community Builds in this forum. Be aware that it might differ from "official" OpenWrt, even if it is unlikely. You could also run an official OpenWrt port, there is no particular reason to run Chaos Calmer and/or custom enhancements in your case.

As for your question, what you are asking for is a bog-standard "access point" scenario, and it is actually the one that OpenWrt comes pre-configured with. However, the DIR-505 only has one ethernet port, and naturally it is configured as LAN. So you need to re-configure it to be the WAN port after creating your Wifi. There is no how-to in the wiki yet (there should be), but there are some on the web. I found the one at https://stuff.purdon.ca/?page_id=370 very helpful, start at "Configuring OpenWrt".

eth1 is correct. You do not have two ethernet ports, but on the DIR-505, the single ethernet port is named eth1, not eth0.

As for your other problem, I really don't know, it certainly doesn't happen with my DIR-505 that is configured to do exactly what you want. It might have to do with some minor or major (mis-)configuration that comes with SuperWrt. As I said, one only needs standard OpenWrt for this particular use case, maybe you should start from scratch with a standard OpenWrt build?
