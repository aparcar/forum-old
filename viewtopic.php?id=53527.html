Get a pogoplug or raspberry pi (or similar). They're both have much faster CPUs and more RAM than most OpenWrt routers. You'll have more flexibility too as you can run a full linux distro (e.g. Arch Linux ARM or Debian).

Even with a faster ARM CPU in the pogoplug/raspberry pi, I'm not sure you'll really be able to take advantage of USB 3.0 speeds.

I'd even recommend getting a NAS or a NAS enclosure if you want to re-use drives, for two reasons:

a) Even cheaper NAS hardware is far better optimized towards this purpose.
b) I would strongly suggest to keep storage hardware separated from infrastructure hardware. You really don't want a single point of failure for both your network and your storage.

Router hardware is really only suitable if you don't really mind about throughput. That's why I usually don't get the requests for USB3 ports in routers when the rest of the hardware can't keep up.

If you must get a PogPlug, make sure it is at least a V4-A3-01 (with one SATA and two USB3 ports). If you peruse this discussion thread, there was such a PogoPlug V4-A3-01 for under US $15 with a free S/H to US from an e-bay seller. Since you have three. BTW, the PogoPlug is ONLY clocked at 700 MHz (IIRC) with 128 MB RAM. If you can still get some Seagate GoFLEX NET (CPU clocked @1.2 GHz with 128 MB RAM), it is a better choice because it comes with two SATA ports + 1 USB2 port. There may be a power issue with the 2nd SATA port on the Seagate GoFLEX NET.
