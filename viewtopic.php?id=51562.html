I don't 100% remember what happened with mine, but I remember I just let it sit long enough and it finally booted into the stock firmware lol.  What I would suggest is trying to ssh into it using DDWRT's failsafe mode (I'm not sure how in DDWRT, but OpenWRT's is here http://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/howto/generic.failsafe), and then either attempting to get back into DDWRT to try again, or flash the firmware from there.  If you can get back into stock, flashing the firmware is as simple as going to the update page and selecting OpenWRT's firmware as the one to update to.
