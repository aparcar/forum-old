codel is the innovative new queuing and drop strategy. fq_codel is generally what you want however, which does fair queueing on top of codel. It is a successor to sfqred, with multiple nice enhancements over sfq, red, and drr that are looking pretty wonderful.

It is still early days though! It's not every decade where a networking paper gets published and the code written in 7 days and slammed into the mainline kernel!
We are working out some thorny details on the codel mailing list and you are welcome to join us there.

There are caveats as yet, I listed a few of them here: http://lwn.net/Articles/503777/

The codel sub-wiki has pointers to the paper, sims, and code for linux 3.3-3.5, as well as binary packages for cerowrt, fedora, and ubuntu.

openwrt presently uses fq_codel in the qos-scripts, turning it on by default for ethernet is under discussion.

http://www.bufferbloat.net/projects/codel/wiki
