the jffs2-partition needs to be at least one eraseblock big (normally 64KBytes), so your readonly-rootfs can be <freespace>-64K. I'm not sure why you need this extra 64kB, especially when you are using an external overlay. In this case the internal flash only need to halt the kernel and some basic packages.
Check http://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/howto/extroot on newer revision of openwrt you can place the whole rootfs on the external disc not only the overlay. So I don't think it's worth trying what you are trying to do, unless you have a really good reason for that.
