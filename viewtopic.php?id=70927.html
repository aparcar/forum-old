Hi,
I recently acquired a single TP-LINK TL-MR3040.  My needs are as follows:
My job requires me to connect to small subnets (in a cabinet) while I am out in the field.  These subnets do not have a DHCPD server.  While they are not isolated subnets, I don't typically have prior knowledge of IP ranges or GW.
In order for me to manage the devices in the cabinet, I have to locate a sticker in the cabinet with IP information, assign an unused IP to my PC, and run a cable to a switch in cabinet before I can manage any of the field devices.  In fair weather, this is not a problem.  However, when it is raining, I don't want to stand next to cabinet with an exposed labptop.  I have a long cat5 cable I carry with me which allows me to sit in my truck.  However, I would rather do this wirelessly. I am currently using stock firmware on my TP-LINK.  However, this requires that I reconfigure the TP-LINK differently for every subnet.  I am hoping I can get away without doing this.  Remember, these small subnets do not have a DHCP server.

What I would like, is true ethernet bridging functionality.  If I must, I have no problem acquiring a second TP-LINK device. I just want to arrive at cabinet, connect TP-LINK to switch.  Connect wirelessly to it (via onboard wireless NIC or via a second TP-LINK), assign IP address to my PC and have connectivity to field devices without having to configure TP-LINK every time as the subnet will be different every time.
Any recommendations on how to go about this?  Do I need to purchase a second TP-LINK?
Regards,

Use AP(WDS) mode in the cabinet, and another router as Client(WDS).  Once you have the pair configured you can just plug them in and use like a long Ethernet cable.  Any IP address will pass through since they are bridged at level 2.  Make sure that the IPs you assign to the routers for configuration are not the same as any you might encounter in the field.

If you run Linux on your laptop you may be able to set its built in wifi to WDS client.

Sounds like you want to:
1) turn off the DHCP server on the TP-LInk,
2) plug the cabinet network into a LAN port on your TP-link router
3) take a photo of the cabinet IP address sticker with your phone
4) connect to the TP-Link with your laptop
5) manually address your laptop, based on the cabinet sticker info

Enjoy working from your warm dry truck.
