So you have DSL.

Your original router must have had a DSL modem built in.  It is common for the phone company to provide a combination unit.  These boxes are simple and cheap but have limited configuration options and usually low wifi performance, so it is natural to want to upgrade it.

The TL-WR841ND is only a router.  It does not have the hardware needed to decode DSL signals from the phone company.  Thus it has no RJ-11 socket to plug in the DSL line.  All the sockets on the back of the TL-WR841ND are for Ethernet.  Notice that they are physically wider than the RJ-11.  They are also electrically entirely different.

The solution is to continue to use your original box but only as a DSL modem.  It will convert DSL to Ethernet.  The Ethernet then goes to your OpenWrt router.  You will have two boxes in series instead of just one.

The best way to do this is to configure the original box in "bridge" or "tunnel" mode.  This turns off all the internal routing and wifi functions and makes it as close as possible to a direct DSL to Ethernet converter.  It accepts a PPPoE connection from your router.  You will need to know your DSL user name and password from the phone company and enter them on the PPPoE WAN configuration page in LuCI.
