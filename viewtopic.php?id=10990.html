Short Description:
I am experiencing an enormous difficulty entering failsafe mode with this router (BELKIN F5D7230-4 v1000).
I have setup a static IP lan connection, having my card the suggested 192.168.1.2 IP, 255.255.255.0 Subnet and 192.168.1.1 Gateway (already tried with blank gateway). I can see with recvudp the packet that asks to press reset to enter failsafe mode, but no matter how hard I try or the timing I choose it never shows the message of "entering failsafe".. it just keeps asking to press reset to enter failsafe mode. Is there any particular timing for this router?

Long Description:
I had my BELKIN F5D7230-4 v1000 running on DD-WRT v23SP2, though I decided to switch to OpenWRT. Given the similarities both in hardware and firmware with Linksys WRT54G (v1.0) I have chosen to upgrade the firmware with the "openwrt-wrt54g-squashfs.bin" image, using the built-in DD-WRT firmware upgrade feature, choosing squashfs to have failsafe mode in case anything went wrong.

In the end of the process things did go wrong. The router wasn't functional, didn't have DHCP or telnet, but the leds were showing life. I decided to proceed according to the failsafe instructions in order to get this router back on track, but I simply can't get pass the "press reset to enter failsafe" stage. I have tried to ping both 192.168.1.1 and 192.168.1.255 addresses without success.

Given the lack of success with failsafe mode, I tried to TFTP back the DD-WRT firmware, also without success. I just couldn't get the router to flash back the firmware.
I need the community expertise to acknowledge me if there is any particular timing for this router and/or if JTAG is the best solution from now.

Thanks for your time!
