flyashi

Since it's a USB converter cable, it probably draws power from the USB port. You most likely don't need "battery".

Open up the router. On the wiki page for the WGT634U, and also at http://openwrt.pbwiki.com/Hardware , there's a pinout of the serial port.

Connect GND to GND, RX to RX and TX to TX. You may have to cross (i.e. RX to TX and TX to RX, but try straight through first).

You can use a CDROM audio cable, a floppy drive power cable, an R/C servo ... there are plenty of things that have 0.1" female headers.

Anyway, cut 'em together. Set the serial port on your PC to 115200-N-8-1, no flow control. Boot the router holding CTRL+C on your terminal. You'll get a

CFE>

prompt. Follow the Howto at the WGT's wiki page for uploading the firmware via TFTP.

Good luck!

flyashi
18 Dec 2006, 19:28

It's a USB to Serial converter.

Since you're using HyperTerminal, I'm assuming your PC OS is Windows. Right-click on My Computer and select Properties. Choose Hardware and click Device Manager. Under Ports, you'll find your adapter. Check which port it's on - it'll be a high number (over 3 usually), but maybe COM1, as many computers these days don't have hardware COM ports.

In HyperTerminal, chose this port. You should see messages flashing on your screen as the router boots.

Good luck. Post back if you still can't get it.

- Yasha
