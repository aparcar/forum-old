I see in the other thread that your problem has at least been partially resolved, but:

dreak wrote:

1. EMERGENCY MODE is not working (not possible) anymore after OpenWRT is flashed.

Please stop repeating that, it is simply not true. Flashing any firmware -- whether it is WD's own, OpenWrt, or a broken one -- does not touch the area where the bootloader sits. This bootloader is booting the firmware, and it is also providing the Emergency Recovery mode. If you wanted to overwrite the bootloader you'd have to consciously go through a lengthy process including a custom-built firmware -- OpenWrt itself doesn't even allow overwriting the bootloader area, for exactly that reason.

And, to state the obvious, your bootloader must be fine. Because, well, your router still boots.

I strongly suspect that you are still doing something wrong with accessing the Emergency Flash. Some minute detail, maybe something as trivial as the wrong port on the router (I actually don't remember if it cares about whether you're connected to WAN or LAN, I believe it should be a LAN port) or something with your browser's cache (maybe it cached OpenWrt's redirect on 192.168.1.1) or its network settings.

2. LAN is still turned off after EMERGENCY reset. (...) Power led started to blink like it should, maybe it already reseted all setings

The Emergency Recovery procedure mode does not reset anything. It actually has no concept of how the firmware itself works and cannot make any adjustments to its configuration. The only thing it cand o is flash a new firmware, effectively ironing it over a part of the flash memory.

Anyone tried to go to EMERGENCY mode from OpenWRT?

Several times, yes.
