Put the jumper on your adapter to 3.3 volts not 5.  Do not connect the 3.3 volt pin on the router.

Try 57600 baud.  In very rare cases it may be even slower than that.

You need a TXD wire hooked up to send anything to the router.

The advice you've got is correct you only need to connect RX, TX and GND. Usually the presence of strange characters means issues with the ground (remember you can get ground almost anywhere e.g. metal casing of USB ports or antenna so no need to soldier it). Without trying a different USB adapter it is hard to say whether you might have burned something on the board or the USB adapter.
The problem is you have connected 3.3V on the board to VCC on the USB adapter (which is 5V since the USB power coming from the computer is 5V) So you could have burned things on the board as it was powered 5V instead of the normal 3.3V.
