If you enable TCP congestion controls in the Networking configuration of the kernel, you can generate modules for different congestion controls (cubic, hybla, illinois, ...). Could someone make and submit a package with at least cubic, westwood, and illinois? Kamikaze's currently only providing vegas and reno, both of which are considered obsolete in the face of the newer controls, or Vista/7's Compound-TCP.

btw, my favorite of late is Illinois, seeing that it handles my company's AT&T T-1 lines pretty well (they drop packets with excessive traffic).

http://linuxgazette.net/135/pfeiffer.html

http://www.ews.uiuc.edu/~shaoliu/tcpillinois/index.html

* Edit: the routers I've been deploying are primarily ASUS units that use the Broadcom-47xxx 2.6 software.
