slh
5 Apr 2016, 23:36

Dear Mr -1, I hope you do realize that the router in question doesn't have USB ports...

...and even if it had a USB port, did you ever look at the prices for USB FXS adaptors?
You can get a standalone VoIP ATA with 4 FXS ports for less than the price of one such mystical USB FXS adaptor
or a 2-FXS ports VoIP ATA for less than half the price
or a complete router with FXS ports for just around that price.

This is all nice and dandy, if you have enough spare parts sitting on a shelf and don't have to pay for them, but retrofitting an underpowered router (a 320 MHz Broadcom SOC, BCM4313 wlan (does brcmsmac work sufficiently?), 32 MB RAM, 100 MBit/ ethernet only, no internal FXS support, no USB support, unclear status for the ADSL2 modem, OpenWrt support for this device seems to be rather basic in the first place) with all that? c'mon.

So without FXS- or USB ports, we're back to duct tape and wishful thinking.
