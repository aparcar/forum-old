Most supported routers do have Wireless capability, but you don't have to activate the Wirelessâ€¦ in fact, if I remember correctly, Wireless is switched off by default in OpenWRT, so just leave it off. (Also, you don't need to attach the antennas, and you can probably also open the router and disconnect the wireless card on some routers).

I think it is safe to assume that all OpenWRT routers will work perfectly without Wireless enabled (as long as they have more than one Ethernet port, of course).

Without Binary Blobs? I guess you mean that you want it to be 100% open source, and no binaries produced by companies that you don't completely trust. Well, the good news is that the binary blobs are usually the Wireless drivers and you don't want those anyway. I don't know of any other non-open blobs that are in used, but I suggest you do more research or get information from someone who knows this better than I do.

Note that you can use the Image Builder/Image Generator to produce your own OpenWRT image, and you can choose specifically to not include the Wireless drivers (which will make sure the blobs are not even on your router).

If you just want a router - no advanced features - even an old proven WRT54GL will work with the latest Barrier Breaker for you (as long as you keep Wireless disabled: it has too little memory to successfully run Wireless). Perhaps a really old and proven thing feels more "trusted" for you, than something that was manufactured last month in China. You can probably get a used one for a very good price.

I hope this was helpful.

I only meant that if you are afraid some government has planted hidden software in a router to spy on people and the Internet, then a brand new router from China could seem like a bigger threat than a much older router.

Consider buying a Raspberry Pi + USB-Ethernet-Adapter + Ethernet Switch. Then you have a quite powerful router, and definitely no WiFi capability. (I have no personal experience with OpenWRT on Raspberry Pi).

There are other supported boards that could work too but they are probably more expensive. What you would really want is a board with 2Ethernet ports (so you wouldnt need the USB-Ethernet-Adapter), but I don't find any such boards on the list of supported hardware.

I suggested Raspberry Pi because it is both cheap and supposed to work with OpenWRT.

Any Linux machine with two ethernet ports and a switch can be configured to work as a router.

The Raspberry Pi has some video hardware that is not 100% supported by Open source software. Otherwise it works fine. I dont know about the license for the hardware design itself.

Since Japan is a democracy with a fair legal system, I think it sounds quite safe to buy a wireless router and leave the antennas off and the wireless disabled (or even not installed). It will be nice and cheap and work well with OpenWRT.
