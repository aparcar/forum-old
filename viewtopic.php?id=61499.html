Tinc is what most closely resembles Hamachi, from what I know. It is available as an OpenWrt package. Their website also has lots of examples for various scenarios.

I think you will need to create a Tinc node on a node you can access from the Internet (e.g. a VPS). To form a connection, some publicly accessible node must be available. If none of two nodes can receive incoming connections, those two nodes cannot form any kind of connection - they need a middle man to link them together, once that is done the two nodes can communicate directly. With Hamachi, Teamviewer and other platforms that offer services that bypass NAT, these services function as the middle man - they receive connections from both nodes, point them to each other and leave them to their business. You need to set up a similar middle man service (in practice a Tinc node, OpenVPN server or similar on a host that can receive incoming connections).

lord_of_metal wrote:

-I think about OpenVPN

I think that would be the easiest route to go about it. Create an OpenVPN server in your own infrastructure (ideally also an OpenWrt router on the WAN side), have your mobile OpenWrt router be the client. No need to worry about incoming traffic through the hotel's (or similar) WAN router, once the tunnel is established incoming traffic goes through the tunnel.

Also, it would serve as an additional layer of privacy, you never know about a public infrastructure and who might be listening in. Personally, I prefer to have all the traffic go through that tunnel when I'm out and about.
