I'm looking at the following use-case:

drop a bunch of me-controlled powered OpenWrt across a landscape.  At one end of the landscape is a  node that is not-me-controlled but connected to the Internet and offers a secured WPA2 node.

All the OpenWrt nodes connect (mesh? what is the correct name?) to each other and at the far end I connect to any one of the me-controlled nodes and enter the correct WPA2 password for the not-me-controlled Wifi node.

Think of it as camp:  The camp office has a wifi node and a password and it's known.  I'm way out of range for that node.  I want to drop wifi nodes in cabins along the way, and I want them to automagically connect together and, even when I I'm on the far end, I want to enter the camp password and have that pass along the mesh, and now I've got (repeater, repeater, repeater, kinda slow) internet.

The wifi units I'm using are Western Digital MyNet 600.  Maybe slow, but both 2.5 and 5 GHz radios.

I've got a few months to figure this out - any suggestions?
