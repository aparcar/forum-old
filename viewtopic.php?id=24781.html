hi Matco,
First thing to ask you.
Are you using any kind of voltage level converter ?
160NL is a 3.3 v device
Your old pc spits out 12+- volts..
you could possibly fry your router.

buy a CA-42 cable thats the cheapest and easiest way.

if you have already bought then there is a thread on this, topic
https://forum.openwrt.org/viewtopic.php … 04#p107904, there are loads of posts which all describe how to do a serial connection.
try this wiki too, http://wiki.openwrt.org/toh/linksys/wrt160nl.
if you still need help somebody will reply back to you on the forums.
If you have followed all of the above said things
that is like..,
1. using 3.3 v CA42 or some convertor
2. your speed settings are like
speed  115200, parity none, bits 8, stop bits 1, flow control none,

and you are still getting garbage, it could mean
try making the length of ur 3.3 volt cable shorter, bcos its noise then,
trying changing your power supply to the OLD WRT54 GLv1.1 one.. bcos the newer switched mode power supplies have more noise.
and do not keep anything near these cable along the 3.3 v length part of it, specially things like cellphone.
again  to avoid noise.... these things of point 3 seem to not make sense... but they do work. buts its noise (by definition unwanted signals)
4. try soldering the wires on ur board. bcos a loose connection is like a no connection but rather a bad connection. but this would void ur warranty for opening up the router.
