I'm sorry, I missposted this in the WhiteRussian forum first time round so apologies to those who've seen it twice.

Versions of siproxd prior to 0.6.0 don't proxy RTCP data and WhiteRussian uses version 0.5.11 with 0.5.13 in kamikaze and backports (I think)
http://www.3cx.com/PBX/RTCP.html

I used siproxd 0.5.13 on my Debian etch and Smoothwall firewalls, the result was that data streams that utilise RTCP (usually Video) operate at a much lower quality through siproxd than they would otherwise.

try ekiga or x-lite video calls, they look terrible with the mosaic effect mentioned in the siproxd forums
http://sourceforge.net/forum/message.php?msg_id=3025301
http://sourceforge.net/forum/message.php?msg_id=4893984

Conversely I just put lenny and siproxd 0.7.0 on a spare box inside my network and video calls look fantastic, plus my other SIP clients all connect simultaneously with no random disconnects or any of the other weirdness I usually associate with multiple SIP clients using STUN & NAT rather than a proxy.

Although I don't currently run OpenWrt I have in the past and I would like to use in on 2 boxes with WiFi disabled to route SIP traffic from / to my high-security network and the intermediate guest and WiFi network, without having to open up vast numbers of ports between multiple host.  I'm also trying to setup firewalls using OpenWrt / X-wrt (or DD-WRT or another) for low throughput situations that don't warrant a full PC with either Debian or Smoothwall.

If no one is interested in this I'll have a look at doing it myself in a few of months time but I'm working flat out at the moment on other projects and this is well outside my area of expertise

Thanks everyone for your time and effort
