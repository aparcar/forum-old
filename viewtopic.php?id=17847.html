The Colour Of Heartache wrote:

[edit]Now I think of it, what would happen if I get a wire with an RJ45 on one end and a RJ11 on the other? would that work?

You better refrain from doing this! That's the purpose of the different connectors - to physically isolate different hardware interfaces. RJ45 interconnects Ethernet networks (which run 12V DC) and RJ11 connects consumer devices (phones, fax machines, modems etc.) to PSTN (POTS) networks (operating on about 48V). So that's the purpose of the modem (MOdulator/DEModulator) - to "translate" the Ethernet protocol (coming from the RJ45 interface of the modem) in form suitable to be distributed over the telephone network (ADSL for example) via the RJ11 interface.
