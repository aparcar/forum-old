Basic first steps are to open it up, check what kind of CPU, RAM, flash, etc. it has. Then one should find the serial port pins/pads and try to interface with the device using a TTL-cable in order to get a bootlog, and possibly even console access.

harayz wrote:

is there a hardware list or something i can refer to, ...

OpenWRT Table of Hardware. Once you have identified the supported platform, the best approach is to see how much of RAM/Flash does your device have. The rule of thumb when dealing with the size of RAM/Flash is the more the merrier. The other two important factors I will also take into the consideration is the USB(2/3) port and the CPU clock. If the CPU clock is 1GHz or higher, your hardware can be used to host more services, i.e. FreeSWITCH PBX system, etc.
