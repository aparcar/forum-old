Ditto.  If you think it's fun to use it, you should try hacking on it.  I work for one of those horrid "tools of management opression" filtering companies, and we of course continually quest to oppress employees of ever-smaller customers.  For the past 6 months I've been studying (part time) how to squeeze services comparable to what runs on our big iron into an AP.  Two WRTSL54GS dev boxes, a Ubuntu VM on my MacBook (yay Parallels!) and a lot of head-scratching later, I have web and IM filtering working in the box.  Yeah, it's GNU based, so the underpinnings will get release before we ship it (if we ship it) but working with OpenWRT has been a blast.  Great job, team.

I should probably jump to buildroot-ng one of these days, but WhiteRussian has been meeting all of my needs.  My home network runs on one of the development boxes now, it's been stable and reliable.  One of the programmers who's been helping out has been running at home  as well, he has a 300gb USB drive attached to his WRTSL and runs SAMBA inside the house and serves web pages internally and externally.

Rock on OpenWRT.
