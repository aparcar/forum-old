AFAICT, any SIPURA device is a stand-alone device that works just fine connected behind a non-symmetrical NAT/Firewall router. What you described on a DD-WRT with MilkFish is an OpenSER (or OpenSIPS) package. Here on OpenWRT site, we have either packages and other VoIP packages, i.e. Kamailio, Asterisk, FreeSWITCH, and Y.A.T.E, to choose from.

I have a SPA2102 which I use with OpenWRT.  My testing indicates that as long as you have only one SIP device behind your NAT you do not need to do anything for it to work.  That is no port forwards or special software needs to be loaded.  This is my result using Backfire anyway.

On my SPA2102 I put the VoIP server from my provider under "Proxy" and I have disabled "Outbound Proxy".

If you need more than one SIP device then special sofware like siproxd is needed.

I used voip.ms as my provider (which I recommend).  Not sure about other providers.
