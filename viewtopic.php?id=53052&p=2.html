richbhanover
11 Mar 2016, 17:03

Does anyone else think that the OpenWrt home page is in a bad state? Here's the situation from my viewpoint:

The home page of OpenWrt - one of the premier open-source projects - fails to offer good information about the project. Despite the fact that thousands of people regularly use and contribute to the project, the home page has major failings:

* There's precious little introductory material that's welcoming to newcomers.
* Much of the information presented is of marginal interest to other readers.
* The formatting is bad - it looks hastily thrown together.
* Updates only occur when one of the heavily-overburdened core developers has a moment to post a notice.
* And because those updates are so infrequent, much of the "news" on the site is many, many years old.

I realize that team that controls the home page and top-level pages of the wiki might not want those pages changed by any random poster. But I would encourage them to consider handing off responsibility to a small team of volunteers. There are plenty of us who would take on those duties. Thanks.

Update: I just posted a note to the developer ticket database that contains the requests on this subject that we made in an email back in July 2015. https://dev.openwrt.org/ticket/22017
