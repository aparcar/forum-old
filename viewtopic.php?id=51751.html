Yesterday I was setting up the local iptables in my home server:
I setup a NAT on my openwrt router.
Then I setup a MAC whitelist on the local iptables of the server writing the MAC's from the devices I want to connect to the server (I've access to that devices).
But the connections were dropped displaying a unknown MAC...
Looking in the openwrt router I saw that that "unknown" MAC address was the  the route!

So the openwrt firewall is masquerading/spoofing/overwriting/replacing (whatever) the incoming mac addresses by its own MAC...
I don't know if there's the normal working method in openwrt but, this is not as the normal routers works ¬¬'

One additional note, the IP address is NOT being masquerading!

Someone can tell me if this is normal and how can I setup the router to not overwrite the incoming MAC addresses?

MAC addresses are always just link local. As soon as a router forwards (as in layer 3 routing) traffic it will re-send the received etherent frames with its own MAC as source, completely normal procedure for ethernet routers.

Sounds to me like you want a bridge, that operates on layer 2 and "preserves" the mac address. Note however that MACs do not "travel over the internet", all you'd get then would be the MAC addresses of your upstream ISP routers and switches.
