Hey,
I am rather new to OpenWRT and have steered myself into a corner I can't get out of.
What I WANTED to do is change my router's firmware in order to have it establish a VPN connection, either via OpenVPN or PPTP.

What happened is, that first I flashed Gargoyle, but the GUI would never accept my OpenVPN settings and eventually I installed pure OpenWrt 12.09 and LUCI.
Since some Gargoyle stuff was still messing with my settings I installed again, this time without preserving the settings.

Afterwards I was successfull in reacing the Netgear via 192.168.1.1. And THEN I did something stupid. My Modemrouter is configured as 192.168.0.1 and instead of bridging the connection and setting manually a firm IP like 192.168.0.2 in the Netgear router I selected DHCP as connection method.

Since then I can't reach the Netgear at all. I've tried everything I could possibly think of, but I simple cannot reach the router.....

Here's what I know:
When connected to the Modem, the Netgear receives the IP 192.168.0.20
When I try via LAN or WLAN to open that IP-Adress it's not reachable.
When I connect via LAN to the Netgear the LAN LED is orange and I don't receive
and IP on my laptop.
When I try to connect via WLAN to the Netgear (SSID is visible when scanning!) I can't establish a connection, even when I set a fixed IP like 192.168.0.21 and gateway 0.1. or 0.20
When I unplug everything an connect only Laptop to Netgear via LAN the LAN LED is
green, but I also don't receive an IP and setting one manually doesn't help either.
Last but not least, resetting the Netgear with the 30/30/30 method results in a bliking Power LED (software corrupt) switching off and on again results in a normal boot and the Netgear
again receives 192.168.0.20 from my Modem.

Anyone have any ideas how I can gain access to the Netgear Router again?
The Model is WMDRMACV2, but it is almost identical to the WMDR3800.
