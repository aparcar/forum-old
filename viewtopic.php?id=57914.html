Isn't the second one from the left the one that is blinking for a few seconds during bootup to indicate that OpenWrt can be put in failsafe mode by pressing the reset/wps button before it stops blinking, then blinks really fast if you actually do this?
If so, you may find a reference to it in the C5/C7-specific implementation of the preinit shell function set_state or in a similar specific version of diag.sh .

As for the LAN and WAN LEDs, on the C5/C7, all 5 Ethernet sockets are connected to the switch IC, so I guess those LEDs are mostly controlled by the switch IC, though there may be I/O options (via GPIO or switch control ports) to adjust their use.
