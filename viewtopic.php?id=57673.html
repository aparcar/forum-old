You should be fine with the sysupgrade image: http://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/howto/generic.sysupgrade

WiFi range is complicated... I don't know exactly how your walls are located, what they are made of, where the router is located, and so on. It is surprising that the WDR3600 is significantly worse than the WRT54GL over 2.4Ghz.

I am bridging a network about 75m using two WDR3600. I have noticed that:
- rotating one router 45/90 degrees affect the signal a lot (best is when they are equally far from target)
- tilting the antennas 10% downwards (towards the target) was very bad, even though the target was lower
- connecting one antenna via a cable/foot, to get through a window instead of a concrete wall, was a big mistake

And then there is interference with other networks.

Conclusion... WiFi range and signal strange is very tricky.

Since you write "corner" and "other room" it sounds like you ended up in a signal shadow somehow. Or above/below the signal?
What range do you get in clear sight? (if possible to test)

Of course, I can not rule out the possibility that there is something defect with your device either.

Edit: the WDR3600 is listed "supported" from 14.07.
  http://wiki.openwrt.org/toh/tp-link/tl-wdr3600
If you got the 1.5 version it might not perform optimally with 12.09 (it says something about wifi patch).

Additional tx power doesn't always help real-world range. Your devices tx power won't be increased, so your router may not "hear" the devices. In addition, you are amplifying noise.

Make sure your antennas are attached properly and facing straight up.
