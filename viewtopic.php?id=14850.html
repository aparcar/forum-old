Post #5

23 Mar 2008, 16:09

Because we can :-). No, seriously, there could be many reasons to do that. One could be because the original broadcom mini-pci range sucks (later has been discovered that there was a manufacturing problem in those routers manufactured before September 2007). Other could be that even with a full working broadcom card, atheros are better (in my experience) and could offer you additional bands (in my case the one I'm using is a/b/g). Also, if you want to use a 2.6 kernel and you want wifi, broadcom is not yet an option. Choose yours ;-)
