- The original power adapter delivers 5V, 1A
- MR3020 draws around 150mA
- passive hub draws around 50mA
- Webcam draws around 150...200mA (I don't have the C170, but the C270)

-> Original power adapter is capable of delivering the required current


Measurements on my MR3020:
- MR3020 100mA idle // 150mA @boot
- Power adapter output 5,14...5,15V
- MR3020 USB output 5,11...5,12V @110mA output-load


It's a good idea to get one of those little USB meters (see link below). They come handy in case of USB power problems.

http://www.aliexpress.com/item/4-bit-wh â€¦ 27741.html

Keywords for search: USB + OLED
Pay attention: There are models out there with 2 digits after the comma and some with 3 digits after the comma.

-> Measure some voltages (ground floor + 2nd floor; preferrably with some load):
- Output of the power adapter
- Output of the MR3020
- Output of the passive USB hub

- Are there any other USB devices attached to the external USB hub or is the cam the only device?
- In all cases reported by you: Did you use the same USB power cable? I just experienced that there are differences: Cable A (=original, data + power) charges my phone with 900mA; Cable B (fake, data + power) charges my phone with 475mA -> less copper in the cable -> more voltage drop, less current
- Have you tried a powered hub (i.e. external power supply for the hub)?
- Do you have any known interference sources on the ground floor, e.g. powerline-LAN, baby monitor (audio / video / wireless), electronic devices with swith mode power supply, ...?
Pull out the mains plugs of all electrical devices on the ground floor. ALL! Then check if the camera gets detected.
If yes, plug in one more device, check again if camera gets detected. Repeat until camera isn't recognized any more -> You found the interfering device.
If no: At least you know now that it's not an electrical device on the ground floor.
- What happens if you unplug/replug the USB cam? Any signs in dmesg?
- How do you determine "USB cam not detected" status? Only by trying with mjpeg-streamer? What does 'ls -l /dev/video*' show?
- Last but not least: Try BB 14.07. USB Performance of the AR9331 has been improved in 14.07.
