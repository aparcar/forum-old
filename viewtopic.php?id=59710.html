I think what RangerZ is trying to say is this:

It woulld be good to see where and how many radio's are on a channel.  No need to see the SSID so that stay's hidden as it should.   It is no secret that a radio is transmitting on channel xNo need to hide that info, and no need to create a illusion that it is hidden either. 

If everyone just used channel 1, 6 ,11 that would probably be better then clogging up the bandwidth by using the channels inbetween.   When someone uses channel 3 then Channel 1 and 6 have to deal with interferance.

when you know where the hidden SSID's are transmitting then at least you can choose a channel with the least interferance.

So the hidden SSID's stay hidden but are unknown SSID's on a known channel. 

@makarel: the option hide SSID only is there to hide the SSID Not to hide any other info. A transmitter that broadcasts radiowaves is not hidden in any way. I simply does not broadcast the name of the station, but 'the music' is there for all to hear.
