Over the last year I have gone from a Linksys WRT-54GS to Buffalo WZR-HP-G300H to a PC-Engines ALIX for my home OpenVPN server.  I had started with DD-WRT and eventually moved to OpenWRT.  Performance more or less doubled each time to somewhere around 10 Mbps with 128-AES encryption. 

Bottom line is you want as much CPU and memory as you can get. 

I am running a HooToo TM02 travel router as a client and get about 3-4Mbps down and 1-2 up.   I think this would make a better solution http://wiki.openwrt.org/toh/gl-inet/gl-inet (6416a) as the CPU is 1.5 times as fast and RAM/Flash double the HooToo.  About $25 US from China. There are at least 2 others who have recently ordered this and you can search the forum for same.  I do not think either has received them, and not sure if they plan to use this for VPN.  It is intended as a travel router, but has 2 ethernet.  If you need it for business security and not for media streaming it might be fine.  The HooToo is marginal with Youtube at high quality.

It you need a robust VPN you want a CPU with AES built in the chip.  There are almost no consumer routers with this, and you can repurpose a PC with x86 or x86/64 but may need to build your own firmware.
