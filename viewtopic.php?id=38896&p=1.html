TP-Link has a new version V2 of the MR3220. There is a ticket at https://dev.openwrt.org/ticket/12026 and page in the Wiki http://wiki.openwrt.org/toh/tp-link/tl-ap121

Current Firmware from TP-LINK
http://www.tp-link.com.au/Resources/sof … 120703.zip

GPL Sources
http://www.tp-link.com/resources/gpl/150Router.tar

The serial port on the board is not connected. It has a new hardware base, AP121, and the compression of the kernel is LZMA.

Update:
The serial signal is on the two small pads on the back side.
TP18 is TX.
TP23 is RX.

Update:
OpenWrt works on the MR3220V2 with the changes mentioned in the ticket.
USB is detected, but doesn't connect to low-speed devices. Other devices work when ledtrig-usbdev is not in the image.
WLAN is not detected.

RalfFriedl

matteo wrote:

what to you mean for low speed usb devices? do USB pendrives and hard disks work? bluetooth dongles?

Low speed devices are mainly used for keyboard and so on, but also for control devices with a small number of I/O pins.
USB storage devices and Bluetooth adapters are not low speed.
