Article on Arstechnica:
http://arstechnica.com/information-tech … n-routers/

As an associated note, TP Link's stance on this issue (against tinkerers and OpenWRT) will have an effect on sales. To what quantity, I can't say.

I recently bought a new router. Historically, I've favored TP-Link, but not anymore. I bought a TRENDnet TEW-827DRU and I'm actively working on porting LEDE/OpenWRT to it, which you can read about here: https://forum.openwrt.org/viewtopic.php?id=65956

I should have a working build in the next few days.

I could have just bought a TP-Link Archer C2600 instead, which has already been ported, but I didn't want to buy TP-Link anymore.

That turned out to be a good move for me, because the TP-Link purposefully made tinkering more difficult by removing the serial port level shifter, requiring difficult soldiering and modding if you want to get a console on their router. Meanwhile, the TRENDnet not only has a super-easy-to-access console port, but a JTAG interface and 4x more flash space (granted it's SLC NAND instead of NOR).

I hope to make the TRENDnet a prefered option over the TP-Link to OpenWRT buyers.
