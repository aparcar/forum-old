    Serial port to access the OS terminal
    JTAG port to upload the firmware
    Serial port to connect to devices (not connection to the Linux terminal)
    RS232 port to connect to devices (not connection to the Linux terminal)
    RS485 port to connect to devices
    SPI port to connect to devices
    I2C port to connect to devices
    Led control via software

mazilo
4 Sep 2012, 16:48

I have yet to see any inexpensive SOHO WiFi router with 128M+ RAM, let alone CPU clock @1GHz+. If your project is meant to be an end product, any inexpensive (chances are discontinued) Linux embedded devices powered by an ARM (single or multi) processor comes with at least 128M+ RAM, CPU clock @1GHz+, 1 RJ45 10/100/1000Mbps port. and 1+ USB2 ports. Some of these devices come with a built-in WiFi + Bluetooth supports. Such inexpensive devices are mostly discontinued and are still freely available @ Amazon, e-Bay, etc. BTW, if your budget is around $70, you may even be able to get an old/discontinued 7" Tablet PC powered by a dual-core ARM processor, i.e. eLocity 7" Android Tablet w/ 1.2GHz Tegra II Processor, 4GB Flash Memory, Capacitive Multi-Touch Screen + Case (Today - 09/04/2012 - ONLY). Currently, this device is NOT supported by OpenWRT, but doesn't mean OpenWRT can't be ported to this device. If you ported OpenWRT to this device, you can even turn it into a 4G/WiFi router with an external USB 4G/WiFi dongle.
