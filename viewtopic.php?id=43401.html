mobilephones, tablets etc. have most likely only 1 Antenna, so MIMO isn't possible.

check out the data-rates:
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IEEE_802.11n-2009

so with one spartial stream the best possible coding scheme is MCS7 (150Mbit/s at 40MHz or 72,2Mbit/s at 20MHz) so get clientdevices which will support multiple spartial streams, or some which support 5GHz to get at least 150Mbit/s.

your laptop should get more than 72,2Mbit/s, it will most likely have 2 Antennas, and maybe supports 5GHz so a theoretical bandwith of 300Mbit should be possible.

the rates you see when running iw station dump, aren't the maximum rates, they are the actual rates at this moment. You only get high rates is you produce massive traffic, otherwise the connection-rate will be lower to save power.
So run massive down- or/and uploads to get more accurate rates, und run iw station dump multiple times to get an impression.
But if your clients support only one spartial stream, and you want to respect regulations, you will not get higher rates on 2,4GHz.

brontide

cobra98 wrote:

So both only connect to 2,4 GHZ right? :-(

Apparently, yes, both devices are 2.4ghz only.  If they both only showed 72mbps before then they are also both single stream N devices ( pretty common phones and inexpensive mobile chipsets ).  72mpbs is also the speed of that particular device and if you do have slower G devices banging around they will knock down the effective bandwidth significantly.  The Galapad 7 specs are clearly wrong while it does support 'N" and "N" supports 600 mbps, it is not true that the galapd 7 supports 600 mhz, the reality is that a single stream 2.4 only N device will be lucky to break 40 mbps in effective throughput and will have a 150mbps theoretical maximum in lab conditions.

Depending on congestion in your local 2.4, using 40mhz channels may result in a higher channel coding, but could actually hurt performance as the larger signal conflicts with other nearby 2.4ghz devices causing dropouts.  It should be used only after testing both 20mhz and 40mhz channels at the location you intend to use it. 

Swapping out the router will not improve the bandwidth unless your clients have better wireless adapters.
