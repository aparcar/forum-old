I am interested in this device now.

I use the TP-Link TL-WR710N extensively:
https://forum.openwrt.org/viewtopic.php?pid=238822

Previously the pricing on the PR2000 was out of whack, but now it looks like it's priced appropriately in the US market.

This Netgear PR2000 is very similar in that it has a built-in power plug, a USB port, and two 100BASE-T ports.

Internally, the PR2000 has twice the RAM and flash storage as the TL-WR710N. That's a big plus. The PR2000 also has a switch and some additional LEDs. It also looks like it can take power in from USB, though that's not interesting to me. CPU is slightly faster on the Mhz, but that's a poor comparison.

On the downside, the swivel antenna looks gimmicky and I'd be worried about that getting broken off out in the field. Also, the MediaTek chip is an issue due to their exceptionally bad Linux/Android reputation. Netgear's reputation isn't very good either.

I will probably buy one and give it a try.

One word of caution with the PR2000.

It appears to have a known failure mode when plugged into any AC outlet with a surge suppressor.

In our office, it works great when plugged into a normal wall outlet.

When I plug it into a power strip with a surge suppressor inside, it drops Wi-Fi or reboots every couple of minutes, making it impossible to maintain connectivity.

I recently plugged it into a normal wall outlet inside a fire department EOC, and it behaved the same way. When I asked "there's a whole-building surge suppressor in the electrical panel, right", the answer was affirmative, and I put the Trek aside.

I found one reference to this as being the cause, and lots of threads complaining about the problem, without a good solution.

If it weren't for that issue, I'd give it five stars.
