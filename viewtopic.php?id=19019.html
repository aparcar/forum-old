Not sure why you are quoting Atheros chips on a Linksys that is Broadcom based...

but it isn't just a matter if it is possible.  YES, it is possible.  dd-wrt has demonstrated this (2MB of FLASH).

It is what is your time worth?  When more modern 802.11n routers are now down to USD $35 or less, how much effort can be put into a router where the vendor cut the legs off it and sold the WRT54GL (still rather limited, why didn't they put 32MB of RAM like earlier models)?

if you value your time at very low... then yes, you can do it.  But for most people just easier to sell the router used and go get a better model.  What is your net cost, an extra $15 to $20?

yes, I know that all Linux ideals aren't driven by economic practicality - but there is a point where most people agree the results are not worth the effort.
