Take a look at http://nuwiki.openwrt.org/inbox/wrt54gl.recovery

I used 'Last Resort Recovery' the other day to fix my system. Note that you must use .bin files and not .trx files. I would also use Tomato or the original Linksys firmware just to get the system up.

I have Vista with a (Oracle VirtualBox) virtual machine of Debian. I also am connected through another router such that my link doesn't go down (to solve that problem you may have to look at some other forums). I used the instructions from the recovery page in my debian vm to do the tftp.

The Tomato firmware was then loaded and running at 192.168.1.1. I then flashed the Tomato firmware to the router. I also chose to do a thorough erase (just to get rid of any old OpenWRT settings). I can now use the router again, and I will soon start to re-attempt using OpenWRT...
