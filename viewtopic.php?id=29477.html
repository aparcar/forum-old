The most important thing for you to do is attach the serial port...

These ports use 3.3v signalling. attempting to connect to your computer's serial port will most likely fry your router. go on amazon and see if you can get a Nokia DKU-5 cable for this job. ...It's easier, and probably cheaper, than making your own serial cable. You'll also need wire strippers, a soldering iron, and some solder.

If you have this connected, you can view the boot process thru the serial interface... that way you'll know whether you softbricked it or hardbricked it, and be able to fix it. I can tell you this: with the TFTP method alone, I was NOT able to restore my E3200 from a softbrick, had to enter some commands through CFE's serial console interface. Keep that in mind.

That said, you CAN experiment with OpenWRT on your E3000, just make sure you have a way to debrick it... and a serial interface. ...that's what I'm doing with my E3200.
