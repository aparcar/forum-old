I have a problem with DHCP on my WRT54G running OpenWRT Backfire.

In my setup, I have a WRT54G as main router, DHCP server and AP, connected to the DSL modem. A second AP (TP-Link 1043ND running stock firmware) is connected to the WRT54G by a LAN cable. Both APs have the same SSID and security settings.
I can connect my Android phone to the TP-Link AP. It receives an IP and everything works as expected. I can also connect my phone to the WRT54G, also this way works. But when my phone was connected to the WRT54G, and I move to the range of the TP-Link, it doesn't receive an IP address anymore. Bringing the phone back to the range of the WRT54G works, the phone can get an IP then.

Using wireshark on a PC connected to the wired switch of the WRT54G, I can see the phone sending DHCP requests, but OpenWRT does not answer...
Even a reboot of the router does not help. Only after a power cycle, the WRT54G starts to respond on the DHCP requests again.
Somehow it seems that if a device received a DHCP lease over WLAN, it can not get a (new or renewed) lease over the LAN ports anymore.

Has anybody an idea how to solve this?
