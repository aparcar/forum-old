Hi,

Here's my situation: I've recently added a WGA600N Linksys Dual-Band Wireless-N "Gaming" Adapter to my network (network A). I'm using the WGA600N as a wireless bridge to my WRT54GS v2 w/ Kamikaze to connect a desktop without wireless that's too unfortunately placed for Ethernet. This mostly works but sometimes the connection just dies. Restarting the WGA600N solves the problem for a little while. I get a good connection anywhere from a few minutes to 2 days. But inevitably it doesn't last.
I started thinking maybe the WGA600N was bad. So I used the WGA600N on another network, call it network B, with a different router and stock Linksys firmware. The connection lasted about 3 days the first time it was configured. This is typical of network B as the AP is really too far away.

I also have two other laptops on this wireless network A that don't seem to have a problem staying connected.

Now I'm thinking I can try adjusting the tx power on the router, maybe lowering it from it's default. I tried lowering the power on the WGA600N from "high" to "medium". That did seem to help a bit.

Anyone have experience with these wireless bridges and Kamikaze on a WRT54GS? Or any otherwise useful help?
Thanks.
