wndr3800 will be a good choice.

I got a wndr3700 v1 it's allmost the some just less ram and flash as the wndr3800.

it's the fastest cpu you can get for less than 100 euros. the bootloader has a tftp-daemon running so you will be able to recover from flashing bad firmware, without the need of a serial-console.

the only bottleneck might be encryption (for vpn, sftp or ftps) it will be done software-crypto as the cpu lacks of any hardware-acceleration so don't expect more than 25-30Mbit/s when using a decent cipher.

But wndr3800 should meet all your requirements.  It supports VLANs, has simultanus dualband 11n, the cpu is fast enough.
So you will be just fine.

Post #7
eleon216
28 Jun 2012, 08:36

8Mbit isn't a problem at all, blowfish performs even better than AES, so you have nothing to worry about.
Regarding latency and qos you should know that in the default configuration the qos-script adds a delay for the normal and bulk buckets.
So your low-latency traffic has to be priority or express, or you have to change configuration of the classes, but I'm not sure how this will influence qos.

But the CPU will be fast enough for all of this. And a router with hardware crypto-acceleration won't help you either with blowfish, only with AES. You won't get anything faster router with good openwrt-support, unless you will use a PC, or something really expensive.
