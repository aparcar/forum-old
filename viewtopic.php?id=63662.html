I am looking to buy a new 802.11ac router/AP for my home and was looking at the TP-Link AC2600. However, TP-Link's recent statement of intent to lock out OpenWRT use combined with the pricing+flash difference with the Amped AC2600 had me looking at it as an alternative.

The TP-Link AC2600, Amped AC2600, and Linksys EA8500 are all pretty much identical as far as SoC and radios go. However, the TP-Link only has 32MB of flash while the other two have 128MB. On the downside, the Amped has 1 USB3 port and 1 USB2 port instead of 2 USB3 ports, but this isn't a problem for me.

SmallNetBuiler has some reviews on all three with interior pictures:

http://www.smallnetbuilder.com/wireless … o-reviewed

http://www.smallnetbuilder.com/wireless … r-reviewed

As I write this, work is still being done on the TP-Link AC2600 to get it operating and stable. It's not ready for use with OpenWRT as far as I am concerned, but it looks like that's just a matter of working out bugs in the new hardware:

https://forum.openwrt.org/viewtopic.php?id=54973
