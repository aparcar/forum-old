I need 2nd ethernet adapter for raspbery PI.
Tried D-LINK DUB-E100. Found it uncapable of real full duplex although ethtool reports its in full duplex mode and vendor advertises its full duplex functionality. Adapter behaves in windows even worse than linux. Tested on 2 powerful PCs with core i7 connected with gbit network and giving 960 mbit /sec on their built in NICs.
What dlink shows : one direction - 96 mbit, other direction - 6 mbit.
I know USB 2.0 is half duplex itself but its capable of transferring data with 37 mbyte/sec speed. This is what my usb 3.0 flash drive gives me when connected to 2.0.
100 mbit is about 12 mbyte/sec. Thats why I suppose good usb 2.0 to ethernet adapter can use time slicing for bidirectional transfer of 24 mbyte/sec.
So i wonder if such adapters exist.
What about gigabit usb 3.0 ethernet adapter connected to usb 2.0 port ? Will it be able to do full duplex at 100 mbit ?

I did simple experiment. USB flash drive to usb 2.0 slot.

# write
cat /dev/zero | pv -L 13m >testfile
660MB 0:01:04 [12.9MB/s]

# read
cat file_on_flash.bin | pv -L 13m >/dev/null
822MB 0:01:14 [11,8MB/s]

With short time falling to 2-3 mb/s which is related to system cache.
So I can conclude it is possible to do bidirectional transfer of 12 mbyte / sec  with USB 2.0
