orangensaft wrote:

Of course there is the option to buy another router in case somebody here thinks it would fit my needs better.

AFAICT the WNDR3700 is the most powerful, affordable router that can run OpenWrt out of the box. A possible alternative is the D-Link DIR-825 which is almost its twin brother.

If you want something more for torrenting, then you may take a look at Plug computers. They all have a 1.0+ GHz Marvell Feroceon/kirkwood ARM processor, no less than 128 MB RAM and run OpenWRT (other Linux distros as well). The most affordable option is the Seagate Dockstar; more expensive models offer more RAM, more flash, eSATA ports, integrated wireless and whatnot.

Depending on where you live, you might be able to get one of the better plugs for about the same money as a WNDR3700, an interesting option if you are satisfied by your current router and only looking for some low-power fun. If not, you might still get a Dockstar + a Tp-Link WR1043ND for less than one WNDR3700.
