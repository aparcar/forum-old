The Buffalo WZR-HP-AG300H is not a modem. This is the router I bought for my home network. Here's my experience:

1) I used the tftp method to upload the initial openwrt firmware, using an intermediate switch. (trunk r30556) Once I started using the correct .bin file I didn't have much issue uploading. (The tftp method worked roughly ~10-15% of attempts, so you have to try a bunch of times.) My unit had dd-wrt when I bought it, but I'm not sure if it originally shipped that way as it was an open box special at Fry's.
2) I had some initial issue with the radio when I had a) both radios on and b) WPA with TKIP. Wife's iPhone and our galaxy tab had terrible connections (looked like the connections might have been resetting themselves.) I turned off the 5GHz radio, switched to AES with WPA/WPA2 blend on radio 0, and turned the 5GHz radio back on with WPA2/AES and that seemed to fix everything.
3) I don't have any issue getting good signal strength around my house as others have reported. I generally get in the range of 60-80 Mb/s net throughput over wireless.
4) Using LuCI on this device is very responsive, presumably due to the able CPU. The flash and RAM are extremely ample, and even now I'm only using a small portion of these.

Personally I'd recommend this device, with the possible exception that I haven't actually tested if I'm getting the proper full TX power out of this device. I was comparing this one to more expensive alternatives and I'm happy with my choice. YMMV
