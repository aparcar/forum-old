Unlikely as this is potentially blocked on a number of levels both hardware and software. Not least the USB hub itself should limit the total current unless its powered.

You have to bear in mind that all active USB devices report to the system how much power they want and will not work if its past the limits.

In my experience even keeping within those limits can be hit and miss.

I have seen a lot of chinese unpowered usb hubs report themselves as powered hubs and connect the +5V pin of the USB cable directly to all output ports without any overload protection.

Many (non compliant) USB devices nowadays draw more than 500mA anyway. Examples are external hard disks or laptop hard disks adapted inside an USB case and cheap devices that recharges their internal batteries from USB power without conforming to standards.

Nevertheless, these devices work because the host's hardware designers often omit the overcurrent protection circuitry and use power supplies that can handle higher power.

Summary:
Should you try to draw more than 500mA from your router's USB port ? A: No.
If you try to do so, will it work ? A: Probably yes, up to some unknown limit.
Could it eventually damage your router ? A: You're on your luck.
