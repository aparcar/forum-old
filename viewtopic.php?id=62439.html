AV products search strings of data for known and suspicious patterns. IP traffic is comprised of packets which are, normally said, string fragments. An AV product would need to be a MitM to amass the packets, inspect the reconstituted string and re-transmit the strings to the endpoint recipient. The location of the AV assembler-inspector-transmitter would best be placed in a firewall appliance. These devices exist today.

I do not think a router is the place for an anti-virus software: network traffic seems too low-level to detect malware.

What you can do is use the DNS in your router to block access to known malware sites; maybe even alert you when one of your computers tries to access one of such sites. Search for "OpenWRT adblock dnsmasq" in this forum, I think there are at least a couple of relevant posts around here.
