metai
13 Feb 2015, 20:43

Short answer: If you're getting your equipment now with the plan to have it run OpenWrt, don't go for a modem/router combination. Even if some very select integrated (mostly Lantiq-based) devices are supported by OpenWrt, they are still the exception and oftentimes very outdated hardware -- for example, the DGN3500 you mention doesn't even have 5 GHz wifi, and its 2.4 GHz is quite limited (I speak from experience).

My advice would be: Get a somewhat modern router supported by OpenWrt, something that fits your requirements and your budget. You can get a lot of routers that offer quite a lot more than a DGN3500 for much less money, second hand. And then get a separate modem for a few bucks on ebay.

The DGN3500 seems to have turned out pretty fine in the time since my last reply in this thread. Keep in mind that it is restricted to the 2.4GHz Wifi band. If that's not an issue I don't see anything speaking against it. In fact, having owned a DGN3500B myself, they are pretty decent devices, even on stock firmware.

And that's another point to consider. I'm an evangelist for OpenWrt but I still have to say, unless you are looking for really advanced features or custom packages on the router contemporary modem-router devices deliver solid performance even on stock firmware. If your family member does not have a need beyond basic routing and access point, does want an easy to use configuration interface, and device integration is higher on the priority list, maybe going for a current TP-Link or Netgear device on stock firmware might actually be preferrable?

Note that an BQL equivalent in the DSL-driver might allow you to get away from the need to apply traffic shaping on the egress, but you still need to tackle downstream buffer bloat, so most likely you still want to set up qos-scripts or sqm-scripts...
