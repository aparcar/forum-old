If you are looking at using openWRT on the C7, make sure you get the V2 version. OpenWRT does not work on the V1 of the Archer C7.

There is a good and simple comparison between the C7 and C8 at http://jamescausian.hubpages.com/hub/TP … r-C7-vs-C8. Check it out.

Post #10
SCF
4 Apr 2015, 17:49

To be more precise, OpenWRT does work on the C7 V1 but the 5GHz WiFi is not supported, the rest works fine.

The C5 used to be the safe choice because v1.20 was the only one on sale. However according to the wiki there is a v2.0 that is Broadcom based. I recently got a C5 and got the v1.20 so I don't know how widespread the newer revision is.

see also http://wiki.openwrt.org/toh/tp-link/tl-wdr7500

Of course if you have more modest needs you can get one of the non-ac models you mentioned. I'd also check prices on other models like WDR4300 and then pick what best fits your needs.
