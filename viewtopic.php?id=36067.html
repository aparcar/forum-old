Post #7
jow
10 Apr 2012, 13:03

rhlee, sure it is possible, you just have to configure the wds client properly.
For example if your WDS-AP hands out DHCP in the 10.20.0.0/24 subnet you want to change the WDS-Clients LAN-IP to e.g. 10.20.0.2 to be able to reach it while you got DHCP from the WDS-AP. If the Client still has its original IP address you can declare an interface alias on your local Linux machine and use that to connect to it, e.g. "sudo ifconfig eth0:1 192.168.1.2 up; ssh root@192.168.1.1" .

Finally you can also trigger failsafe in the WDS Client, telnet in, run "mount_root" and change the LAN IP to something reachable.
Of course you also have to ensure that WDS-AP and WDS-Client use different LAN IPs etc.
