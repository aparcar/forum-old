The routers boot in stages, first booting to a bootloader like CFE and then booting into the firmware. CFE allows you to reflash via tftp, other bootloaders have similar mechanisms to reflash. In other words, even if the firmware doesn't boot, you can still boot enough to recover without jtag.

If you manage to screw up or crash CFE, you will need a jtag cable. There is no jtag over ethernet; inventing terminology for concepts not understood does not make it correct.
