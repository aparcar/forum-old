In any way you extend a signal by using a single radio interface, you will half the bandwidth.
You can do as much repeater bridges as you want without having troubles, just ensure a good signal of about between them, and you will have a nice network.
Just, if the band allows 300Mbits(for example) , all the repeater will be working at the half of it(150Mbits each side).
Its not a drama! Check my post here,https://forum.openwrt.org/viewtopic.php?id=39077 you need the config #1.
For me, a good signal than ensure a high data rate and long range between routers is about -63dB or more, but do the test yourself for checking the performance at different levels.

For doing just bridged wireless client, without repeater, delete the second interface in the wireless config, in "ap" mode.
That way the wireless will use its full bandwidth as client.
In this case, in your picture, the right devices will work at full bandwidth, while the left ones would do at half.

But, unless you have a 100Mbit internet connection, I thing the loss would be small.
Considering that the cheapest routers run at 150Mbit, leaving half each side, could give 50-60Mbit of real bandwidth, which is still very good.
And you have better ones, capable of 300 and 600Mbit...

What router will you get? I recommend and atheros based, as OpenWrt has excellent support for it.
I could help a bit with the configs also.

In case you want 100% of bandwith in repeater mode, you could use a router with two wireless card, or buy a router with USB and plug an USB wireless card working as client, while the router works as AP. Of course you need a wireless card that is supported by OpenWrt.
