Is this the right conclusion?

When you have a telnet connection to that router, you are executing the commands locally on the router. The OS which hosts the telnet client doesn't matter.

Exactly.  When you telnet in and type for example 'mtd' or 'snarf', the commands run on the router.  Windows is operating simply as a keyboard and screen dumb terminal.

As a fellow owner of one, there is a big thread about the WNR2000v1 in this forum.  It is a little tricky due to the small flash and unusual partitioning done by Netgear.  I suggest you go right to the serial port (pins are already installed) rather than trying to enable the telnet on stock firmware.
