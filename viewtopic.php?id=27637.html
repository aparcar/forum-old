Without seeing more of the protocol trace it is hard to tell what is happening exactly.

There are two independent parts to this message:

1. "TCP Retransmission"
2. "Continuation or non-HTP Traffic"

TCP Retransmission means that TCP detected that a packet was lost somewhere in the network and it is retransmitting the lost packet.
Lost packets can be due to a number of reasons including errors on the line, congestion in the network or excessive delay (an ACK is delayed longer than the retransmission timeout).  It isn't unusual on the Internet.  I usually don't worry about them unless I get a lot.  When looking at Wireshark traces you are likely to see other anomalies such as "TCP Previous segment lost",  "TCP Dup Ack", "Out of Order", etc.

The packets could be dropped anywhere on the path between your client and the server so it might not have anything to do with your wireless LAN or router.  Of course, if both ends are on your local network, then it could indicate a local problem.  But retransmissions happen occasionally so unless they are excessive I don't worry about this.  If you are getting excessive retransmissions it will slow down your downloads and you will probably need to troubleshoot.

The wireshark website has some good tutorials on how to use wireshark to troubleshoot problems.
http://www.wireshark.org.

The second item isn't necessarily an error.  It usually means that Wireshark got a packet on port 80 that it could not decode as HTTP.
