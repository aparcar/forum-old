I've got the same problem and I can't help you FIX it but I've got a workaround.

1) It seems to be OpenWrt that is the problem, not the router. 
With the stock tp-link firmware it doesn't do this.

2) I've plugged the WAN port of the router and the LAN port of my cable modem into a small switch.  Those are the only 2 devices connected to the switch.
For some reason it works in this configuration.  That is to say, it will get a DHCP address w/o having to unplug/replug the WAN cable.
