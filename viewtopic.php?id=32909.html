Hello all,

I have a WR1043ND, using Alfa AWUS036H with 5dbi omni-directional antenna to connect as a station to a WPA2-Enterprise wireless (in this case, the 'eduroam' wireless service). I am, I suspect, approximately 400 feet from where the access point i'm connecting to is located (but I really don't know for sure - it's close, anyway). I'm currently running Backfire 10.03.1-RC5.

The connection is stable:

Link and Signal levels fluctuate between the -50s, but it's stable. Bit-rate can go up and down, but largely it reports that its at 48 or 54 Mb/s.

Here is where the problem begins: the internet is dog slow. I get, at average, around 1 Mb/s download and about 2-3 Mb/s upload (tested using speedtest.net). I was scratching my head to figure out why, when I noticed the noise level on the Luci web interface was reporting a noise level of -36db... which, I suspect, is unusually 'high'.

The wifi traffic in this area is quite busy - I can pick up anywhere between 20-40 aps. I also live in (and around) student blocks with various, poorly maintained (ie. plenty of BT Openzones being thrown out to crowd traffic) wireless routers (and, knowing from my own building, a fair few microwaves!) The adapter is attached to my window. I also live on a busy seafront, so traffic can be fairly busy (and I have no idea on the effects of ferious welsh sea winds on wireless signals, tongue)

Am I right in assuming that the noise level is what's behind my slow speed? Eduroam is *fast*, I know I should be getting, at least, somewhat better speeds based on my signal level. If the noise level is the case, any ideas on what I can do to solve this? I assume a directional antenna might be of some worth (and something I'll definitely consider if it'll help) but is there anything else I can do to 'lower' the noise level and get a better connection?

Any suggestions?

I think at best you can do is to get a bigger directional antenna. 5dBi is too small for such distance.

BTW, have you tried fixed 802.11b mode instead of 802.11bg mode?

Secondly, next time you shouldn't get such a 1W high power WIFI adapter which enhances both transmission and also noise. Instead, you should get a mid-power, 100-200mW or 20-23dBm, WIFI adapter plus a high gain directional antenna which is the right way to extend range.
