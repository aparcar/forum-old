Buy a Buffalo WBMR-HP-G300H

It's not a very cheap device, but it's the most reliable choice when it comes to ADSL@OpenWrt.
It has 4 gigabit ports, 32MB of Flash Memory (that's important, because you probably won't need to extroot it), 64MB of RAM and 1 USB 2.0 port.

Migration from stock firmware to OpenWrt is pretty easy, you first flash dd-wrt and then flash OpenWrt. There are instructions on the Wiki.

There also some TP-Link devices like TD-W8970 (V1 only, not V3) and TD-W8980 but they have 8MB of Flash Memory,so extroot is inevitable, especially if you want to setup things like VPN, traffic stats etc.

Another important thing about these TP-Link modem-routers is that they will probably come with a firmware that won't allow an easy migration from stock firmware to OpenWrt and you will have to solder pins to the board and use a USB to TTL serial cable to flash OpenWrt. So... in few words, I don't recommend them.

Hope you find the above info uselful.
Cheers!

angelos wrote:

There also some TP-Link devices like TD-W8970 (V1 only, not V3) and TD-W8980 but they have 8MB of Flash Memory,so extroot is inevitable, especially if you want to setup things like VPN, traffic stats etc.

I have to disagree. 8 MB is plenty. 4 MB, now that's another story, you'd be hard pressed to fit much of anything beyond the base system in there. But 8 MB should be just fine for anything smaller than what shouldn't realistically be run on router hardware anyway.

By the way, I still would have to advise against the W8970. It takes a good amount of effort to get it running, as you say. And if it's running, it's not completely "smooth", as in "sometimes the logfile yells around a lot about errors". Maybe that will get better, or maybe it has already, I haven't tried anything beyond CC in the last months on my W8970.

My general advice would be to get a router, and get a modem. It's just much less hassle that way. You're very limited if you're looking for an all-in-one solution, and you'd miss out on otherwise great hardware just because it doesn't come with a modem built in.

If you don't mind using a second hand device, the Netgear DGN3500/DGN3500B (depending on whether you need Annex A or B DSL support), will give you all you need.

My experience with the 3500 (Annex A) on the last three OpenWRT releases has been solid, as long as the ADSL modem firmware is updated to a newer version than that shipped. Details in the discussion thread.

Interestingly, although early days into testing, the packaged ADSL firmware is solid using the LEDE trunk builds, which implies the problem with ADSL disconnections in OpenWRT lies elsewhere.

DGN3500 info: https://wiki.openwrt.org/toh/netgear/dgn3500b

DGN3500 discussion thread: https://forum.openwrt.org/viewtopic.php?id=24195

Regards,

Richard
