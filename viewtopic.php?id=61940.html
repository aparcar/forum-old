OpenVPn is not easy, but start with this as a guide. https://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/howto/open … rver-setup
You can find other VPN guides by entering Openvpn in the upper right search box.

I do not suggest trying to configure with LuCi, but get and install WINScp.  Its a file manager for your router.  You login like you would with putty, your IP address, user = root, and your password, file protocol SCP and port 22.  You will find the files you need in the folders \etc\config and your keys go in \etc\openVPN.

I am assuming you want to connect to an OpenVPN service as a client, but you have not stated your use case.  Many services will have a script for you to use.  Backup or rename the delivered openVPN files (NOTE: there is NO opvn extension on the files) and create a new blank openvpn file for you to edit.  WinSCP has a text editor built in.  Use it to open the delivered file and your new blank one and cut and paste, and\or reformat your services file to the correct format.

Don't forget to create the key files (your service will give them to you) and put them in the etc\openvpn folder.  There is more than that, but this should keep you busy for a while (interface and firewall config).

Much more complicated.  Still a good, maybe the best article.
You do NOT need to use OpenWRT to generate your keys.  I suggest doing if from a local PC and use the OpenVPN tools. all gui driven.  Also suggest you do not use your email or real name in the key creation.  Just will make your life easy when you post for help, no other real reason.

Read this page https://openvpn.net/index.php/open-sour … howto.html
Decide on TUN or TAP.  I choose TAP as it's easier to get the local resources, but I think you can use TUN with the right firewall rules.  Leave out any cipher, encryption, mtu etc until you get the 2 sides talking, then tweak 1 feature at a time.

It honestly took me months to get this working reliably.
