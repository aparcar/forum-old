I'd like to make use of an openwrt-running router to act as a 4 (or 5) port gigE switch and be able to monitor a USB-connected APC UPS. 

We've a generator here, so I don't need much in the way of capacity, just enough to keep the gear alive until the genset fires up.  So I've got a number of APC 350 and 550 units.  They've been going unmonitored for a while and eventually they need new batteries.  I'd like to have a heads-up on their battery condition before their little piezos start bleating like stuck pigs... in the middle of the night...

The idea occurred to me that since more consumer routers have a switch, what about combining the two?  Monitor the UPS and act as a 4 port switch to local devices.  Or 5 ports if the WAN port can be bridged.   

Most of these are to connect a TV, so there's a DVR, possibly a streaming player or a receiver, and an iTach network IR emitter.  Funny how the number of ports needed adds up these days.

Don't need wireless at all.  I'd just disable that in software if it was present.

I'd be willing to spend more if it meant having better gigE switch throughput.  Especially when it comes to blocking certain traffic, as their as some implementations of DLNA features that don't work and I wouldn't mind preventing them from being seen on the network.  As in, you can't get network control without also enabling DLNA, but then anything on the subnet can see them (and send to them).  It makes for messy lists on various tablets and phones as all sorts of devices are dynamically discovered.  Being able to block that would be sweet, but it's not critical.  If it meant spending a little more to get a router that was 'less slow' at this I'd entertain the upgrade.

Any suggestions on a stable solution to handle this?  4/5 ports gigE and USB to run NUT?
