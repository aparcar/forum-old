Hello to everyone! As you can see, I'm new and I'm sure I would learn a lot here.
I'm experiencing a problem with a friend's G604T. After it crashed to the ground, the wireless connection stopped working. He tried to get it work again with no success then gave it to me.
I opened it, desoldered the plate over the wireless card and found out that the mini-pci card (an Alpha Network WMP-G04 with TI TNETW1130GVF chipset) was a little dislocated. I put it in place and resoldered the plate but it didn't work: the wireless couldn't be started.
I'm quite sure that the card could be damaged. Do you agree with me? Is there a way of interrogating the card by the Linux subsistem accessible by telnet? If I have to change the card, is it possible to replace it with a different card? I fear not because I think that somewhere in the router mtd3 there might be some drivers for it and this will lead to a lot of errors.
Alpha one isn't to be found anywhere, while I've found a couple of TNETW1130GVF based cards. What do you think I should do?
Thanks for your replies!
