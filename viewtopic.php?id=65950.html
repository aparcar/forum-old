I have been running the WRT1900ACS for about a month now. Tried a variety of  Firmware , Native Linksys, DD-Wrt and OpenWRT. None of these  stretch the hardware and never have seen the CUP's go over 1%. I suspect my next F/W will be LEDE based. I use this in a domestic environment with a connection to the internet using PPPoE. As yet there is nothing really to choose from although my aim is to make my connection is a secure as possible. I believe this means either OpenWRT or LEDE.  I have spent a fair amount of playing as its over 20 years since I played with Unix. To sum up the hardware is good but as yet I have seen no firmware that stretches the hardware's capability .ie apps for Adblock, IDS&IPS, malware etc.  I suspect this is down to my basic capability with the unix style f/w

FurryNutz
1 Jul 2016, 21:24

The only saving grace to Linksys WRT series is the users have a way to get away from OEM buggy FW and use OpenWRT, LEDE or DDWRT. Otherwise I'd recommend going with a different router mfr. Linksys OEM FW development is lacking, buggy and seamless support is very bad. Last ACS update took 6 months and now probably no on track for any more updates as what happened with the v1 and v2. There still bugs in OEM FW that hasn't been addressed by Linksys and probably won't be. It's been a year now and nothing since then for them. It's a shame that Linksys has fallen so far down the well since they use to be on top. No sure if that was due to Belkin buy or just poor management.

I've installed OpenWRT on my v1 and all is good. I was advised by a forum user I know to migrate to LEDE fork repo as they seem to have more up to date code. Still compatible and similar to OpenWRT.

Thank you for 3rd party code.

Apart from some clients not getting n or ac speeds wireless is fine, one could argue that some features are missing, thou not essential intel would have implemented them Make sure to update to the latest driver. Apart from that the hardware is well supported by linux.

About not needing the performance, the v1 didn't made the cut for me so I waited patiently for the acs. Getting the cpu to 100% is as easy as downloading at Gbit speed (nat) and there are many other creative ways to max it out.

If the open support for thirdparty firmware carries value for you go for it. For me the looks and hacker friendliness are other valuable aspects.
