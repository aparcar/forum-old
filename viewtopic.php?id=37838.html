Hi,

the wifi is disabled because this is the only secure way to do it.
What would be the alternative? setup an open wifi-network? An encrypted network with an known key? At home I get 4 wifi-networks from neigbours (Thomson/Speedlink-Modems) which use the preconfigured wifi-setup by the vendor, and I can connect to all of them. And they have no password-protection on the webinterface either, so yeah this would be a really good approach for openwrt, too. So after you flash openwrt everyone can connect to your router and set a password, that is really a great idea.

and now to your problem:

Why are you using kamikaze? it's from 2009! get backfire 10.03.1
for configuring wifi check this out:
http://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/howto/wireless.essentials
http://wiki.openwrt.org/toh/linksys/wrt54g

and for your hardware there are two options the properitary driver from broadcom, or the opensource driver (b43). I'm not sure about the situation in backfire because I don't use broadcom-chip anymore, and broadcom has released some opensource-drivers itself. But in kamikaze you have to decide if you get bcm-2.4-target with kernel 2.4.x and the broadcom-driver, or the brcm47xx-target with kernel 2.6.x (or 3.x) and the opensource driver (b43). So get a firmware for the right target-plattform, install the right driver, run wifi-detect  and then configure your wifi-network.
