mount -o umask=777,uid=0,gid=0 /dev/discs/disc0/part1 /mnt/test

A umask contains bits that are masked out: turned off.  It sounds like you want mask=0, to not turn any bits off, letting files show up with mode 777.

After mounting the rights changed to 755 and owner is root again. and after umounting test bla is the owner again.

You're confusing the mount point with the root of a mounted filesystem.  A mount point is a (usually empty) directory somewhere in the directory hierarchy that your filesystem gets mounted on.  Because it's a directory, you can set the owner and group and mode, but once you mount a filesystem on it, it's hidden from your view by the root directory of the filesystem you mounted.  When you unmount the filesystem, the underlying directory becomes accessible again.
