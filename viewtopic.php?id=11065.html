See the Hardware Wiki here:
http://wiki.openwrt.org/OpenWrtDocs/Har … ardware%29

Some people just cut open a Nokia DKU-5 cable if they want a quick hookup for serial, it's got the chip for converting the 3.3V TTL signals of the router to what you need for PC.

Recommend you READ THE WIKI for documents like this one:

http://wiki.openwrt.org/OpenWrtDocs/Ins … WrtViaTftp

There are "SEARCH" buttons on both the Wiki and Forums where this ground has been covered many times.

Do not rush around erasing things and shorting out pins without knowledge of what you are doing, that is not smart.

9 times out of 10, even before learning to add serial console to routers, I have rescued bricked routers SIMPLY BY PERSISTENCE.

Put a switch between PC and router to keep link up.  Keep trying different timing of power-up, send TFTP file.  Sometimes it goes very quickly other times it takes many tries.

With serial console though it is usually much easier and quicker. And is only 3 (or 4) wires to solder on router, no big deal.  The variant of serial port design I use is so simple I just add the jack for it, to every router that passes through my hands now.  It was originated from JimWright and netprince, and is documented in section 3.1.2 of this page:
http://wiki.openwrt.org/OpenWrtDocs/Har … /WRTSL54GS

Here endeth the lesson.
