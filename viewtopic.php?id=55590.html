Found a link to this and it sounds pretty interesting. There's a reddit discussion where one of the firmware developers claims that OpenWrt builds will be available for those that don't want to use their cloud-based firmware. That makes me think their own firmware probably is also OpenWrt-based. Hardware sounds pretty powerful too compared to most of the current market. Preorder price is $125, which is really not bad. Retail will be $199.

Website: http://www.eero.com
Specs:
- 1.0 GHz dual core processor (ARM, probably according to Wired)
- 512MB RAM
- 1GB Flash
- 2x Gigabit ethernet
- Dual 802.11a/b/g/n/ac radios (confirmed: two separate 5GHz radios, not just 2.4GHz+5GHz)
- Bluetooth 4.0/Low Energy

I'm not affiliated with this company in any way, but thought there might be some interest here. It really is aimed at simplifying things, while being a lot more stable and reliable than most manufacturer stock firmware.
