this is an directional antenna and 21dB is  also a really high gain, so it will transmit a quite narrow beam (probably around 10 degrees horizontally and vertially), and it will receive only in this narrow angle. So only networks the antenna is directly directed to will get a stronger signal, all networks outside his narrow angle will be much worse.
So that's why you see less networks. And that's actually a good thing, because that means that less networks will interfere with your connection.
But directional Antennas are used to connect to other directional antennas (or at least highgain omidirectional antenna), so you have to point directly to the other antenna.
Directional antennas aren't the right wardriving equipment!

And you should keep in mind that the allowed transmit power is limited too. You have to check for your country but normally a ERIP of 20dBm or maybe 23dBm is allowed, which mean that you have to reduce the txpower of your wificard a lot. You will only be allowed to set it to (around) 5dBm to compensate the loss of the 5M cable, connectors etc.
But sending only with a ERIP of 20dBm over a long distance will only work if there is a high-gain antenna on the other side, to get a high receive sensitivity.

@elon 21db gain minus 5db cable loss (approx 1db/m plus loss on connectors) will reduce the mean gain to 16db.
Bringing the router to 4db or less output will keep it in within 20db EIRP.

Mounting an antenna on the roof does not indicate wardriving - quite the opposite.

@Slazer: You need to set the correct antenna in /etc/config/wireless (txantenna 1, rxantenna 1) in the main section and restart the router. (or execute wifi from the console).
Keep in mind, that some routers are real mimo-devices (one antenna per stream), while others have an antenna switch, which connects two antennas to one stream.

txantenna/rxantenna values may vary. Please see iw info / iw phy phy0 info for all possible values.
0 is auto, 1 is main antenna, 2 is aux antenna in most cases, 3 is often diversity for two antenna devices.
Newer MIMO-devices may need other settings. Stick to the wiki.
