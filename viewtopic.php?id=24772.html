I was told by someone on the other forum that the wpad-midi is the drop-in replacement for both the hostapd-mini and the wpasupplicant-mini. So it should be enough just to deinstall hostapd and install wpad/wpad-mini instead. OpenWRT wifi-scripts should do the rest of the work, automatically detecting the presence of the wpad and using it instead of hostapd.

P.S. This should work with OpenWRT 10.03 or later.

Upd. In case your firmware is not self-compiled, it should be more safe to opkg uninstall all the wpa_supplicant*, hostapd* and wpad*, and then install the only parts you're going to use.

Take a look at this:

root@dir825NSAHome:~# ls -la /usr/sbin/wpa* /usr/sbin/hosta*
lrwxrwxrwx    1 root     root             4 May 17 22:44 /usr/sbin/hostapd -> wpad
lrwxrwxrwx    1 root     root             4 May 17 22:44 /usr/sbin/wpa_supplicant -> wpad
-rwxr-xr-x    1 root     root        421263 May 17 22:37 /usr/sbin/wpad

This box has got only wpad-mini installed. As you can see, hostapd and wpa_supplicant are both there, but they are the symlinks to the wpad executable.
