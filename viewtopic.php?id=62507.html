You cannot have a port open actively all the time without a service listening on it. "Opening" a port in the firewall actually just unblocks it, it will only be "open" when a service starts listening on it.

eduperez
2 Feb 2016, 15:03

I see you have port 500 open on the router, is it that? But I do not see that port being redirected to any port on your game console.

Anyway, as "jow" commented, I think you are confusing "opening a port on the firewall" (what you have) with "having a service that can be reached from outside" (what GRC is reporting that you do not have). So, right now your firewall is letting incoming connections to port 500 pass, but only to port 500 on the router. But your router is not redirecting such incoming connections to any port on your game console. And even if you do that, you must have a program on your console, listening on port 500 and ready to receive incoming connections.

Perhaps if you explained what problem do you have, and why do you want to open such port, we would be able to help you better.

Just add these to your firewall and reset dnsmasq and firewall.

config redirect
    option name 'PS4_Redirect1'
    option src 'wan'
    option src_dport '30740'
    option dest 'lan'
    option dest_ip '192.168.1.132'
    option dest_port '30740'
    option proto 'tcp udp'
    option target 'DNAT'
config redirect
    option name 'PS4_Redirect2'
    option src 'wan'
    option src_dport '9308'
    option dest 'lan'
    option dest_ip '192.168.1.132'
    option dest_port '9308'
    option proto 'tcp udp'
    option target 'DNAT'
