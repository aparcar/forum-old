Hey there.

There are plenty of registrars out there. That's the first step. Just like eduperez said, they usually come with DNS for you.
That's what I did.

Then I decided to give Cloudflare a try, basically to use their CDN service for static file delivery.
There I realized: They have a pretty nice and powerfull DNS management tool as well.
The only thing you need to do: Have a domain somehwere and point name servers to cloudflare.

Some time later I stumbled across hurricane electrics DNS service. It's not as nice as the one provided by cloudflare. But in contrast to cloudflare, they provide nice and easy "dynamic DNS".

So my current configuration looks like this:

* "mydomain.de" regiterered "somewhere" (InternetX Gmbh, the company I work for has a couple of hundrets of domains there, so I got it basically for free)
* name servers of "mydomain.de" point to cloudflare ("nslookup -type=ns mydomain.de" => ray.ns.cloudflare.com, elle.ns.cloudflare.com), configured through the DNS interface of InternetX.
* name servers of "external.mydomain.de" pointing to HE ("nslookup -type=ns external.mydomain.de" => ns2.he.net), configured through the DNS interface of Cloudflare.

My internal domain handled by OpenWRT at home is "internal.mydomain.de". There's no need to expose that to the outside world, so it's not added Cloudflare nor to HE nor to InternetX.

I really recommend to give both, Cloudflare and HE a try. Cludflare has a powerfull and shiny ajax based interface, HE adds dynamic DNS to your domains.
