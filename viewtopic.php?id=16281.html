We want to create a all in one product and need a developer to apply his time to create our product as fast as possible.

We need the following supported on any cheapy router with a onboard ADSL rj-11 annexure A port(POTS).

Product A

1. Support for PPPOE.
2. Support for L2TP tunnel.
3. Webif to quickly setup PPPOE & L2TP.
4. Possibly a way to create a script config file that can be uploaded to preconfigure a router in the least possible time.

NB The finished product must be documented.

Product B

1. Support for PPPOE.
2. Support for L2TP tunnel.
3. Working Hotspot with captive portal and walled garden running either on the Eth port or on a optional WiFi interface.
4. The Hotspot must authenticat users against a radius client and be able to send back accounting data to the radius.
5. Hotspot login page must be customizable to certain extent.
5. Custom Webif is optional.



We are offering an incentive of $1000 USD to the person or group who can implement this and deliver the goods.

The irony of it all is that the networking requirements are trivial and pretty much already exist in OWRT.  In fact, OWRT + WiFiDog would probably fulfill what you're asking for.

I would actually be interested, but have some definite concerns, not the least of which is that I've seen many projects start bounty programs that end up failing mostly due to benefactors never paying.  Come out front with an escrow or use a program like BountySource or BountyCounty and you'll probably get more serious interest.

HugoMeerkat wrote:

...on any cheapy router...

This is the second major strike against it - you can't expect a re-creation of a project like OpenWRT (which the word 'any' implies) on that budget.  One, maybe two major architectures.  This also helps clear up the 'optional wifi' and some of the open-ended customization requirements in product B.

JKoltner wrote:

At a standard full-burdened engineering rate in the ballpark of ~$100/hour in the U.S., I suspect you're at least an order of magnitude shy on your incentive.

And the 3rd strike.  Although I disagree with the 'order of magnitude' estimation (which it could still easily hit with enough customization requirements), good freelance UNIX engineering contractors in the US make a minimum of $100 per hour, and that's if they're interested in the job.  I doubt putting all of that together (both products, with documentation) is going to take <= 10 hours.
