If I were you, I'd return that router and get something like a WRT54GL from NewEgg, Amazon, etc.  They are $52US - $62US depending upon rebate.

The version 6 is only partially supported by OpenWRT and worse, only has 2MB of flash.  That's enough to run OpenWRT, but I don't think you can add much if anything at all. (I've never used one, but the FAQ says 2MB will leave you pretty cramped.)

The WRT54GL (note that the 'L' is important) has 4MB flash and 16MB ram and works very nicely.  It leaves a good amount of room to add software,

Plus, by buying the GL you are voting with your wallet for the Linux based units over the later VxWorks based units.

Whatever you do, have fun!

More info about WRT54GS support here:

http://wiki.openwrt.org/OpenWrtDocs/Har … ys/WRT54GS

-Steve

Believe me, the sheer OpenWRT coolness you will experience after you find the right router will more than make up for the initial frustration.

That said, I'd do some research in the forums search about just what it might take to do the virus scanning you are talking about.  I'm not certain that the WRT54GL would be up to it.  However, something like the WRTSL54GS, which is about 99USD, has 8MB flash and 32MB ram.  Plus a USB port so that you can have a swap partition and plenty of disk space. (From a small usb fob to a, say, a terabyte RAID array. (I think it will do RAID.  Haven't actually tried.)

I'm finding that the wireless range on my WRTSL54GS is awful, though.  However, Vincent has several of them and says the range is fine, so mine might be defective.

-Steve
