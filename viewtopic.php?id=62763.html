When you are on a mobile netwprk you are usually behind a NAT controlled by your mobile network operater... That means that they are managing ports, which are in fact closed.

As you already figured it out, you meed VPN or reverse SSH where the OpenWRT device behind the 4g modem is a client.

Reverse ssh!

I did not know that exists.
https://www.howtoforge.com/reverse-ssh-tunneling

So a battle plan sounds like:
1) Make a Dynamic DNS for computer
2) Make router repeatedly (each 30 min ?) resolve the IP of the DDSN and try to make a SSH connection.
3) Make the router log this, and sync the log to papertrailapp.com
4) From the computer, watch papertrailapp.com
5) If syslog shows connection, then reverse connect.
