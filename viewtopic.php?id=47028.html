Right. If you are in the market for a router, I'd suggest you get a router. Multiple ethernet ports, solid Wifi performance, some RAM and flash memory so you don't hit a wall as soon as you want it to do more than just the basic tasks. The WDR4300 and the WR1043ND are both equally suitable.

I don't even know why you put the WR703N and the Raspberry Pi into the mix, other than they might be "en vogue" or inexpensive. They are both niche models, and if you need the niches they excel in, you'd know why to get one. But they are not suitable as a baseline everyday router.

My recommendation is to pick up a regular router, something like the  Belkin 600 HD for $35. Dual Band, Gigabit and Dual USB Ports.

Run Tomato USB on it -
http://blog.qnology.com/2012/12/tutorial-apple-airplay-on-tomatousb.html

Do not use your router as a NAS or to download torrents. Yeah it (barely) works, but you'll not be happy with it. Router CPUs are typically too slow.

Instead consider picking up a Pogoplug and hacking it to run Arch Linux ARM. Run all the things you wish your router had to power to do on your Pogoplug instead - SAMBA/NAS, BitTorrent, etc.

http://blog.qnology.com/2013/03/tutorial-pogoplug-e02-with-arch-linux.html
