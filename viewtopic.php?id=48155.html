Do you want wlan1 and wlan2 to be separated, so that a computer from wlan1 cannot access wlan2, but both can access the adsl router (that I fail to find on your picture)?
Or do you just want them to be in the same network and have access to the adsl router and the internet? In the latter case you don't need vlans. Just creeate eth0 as bridge and add wlan1 and wlan2.

Read his post again. Neither WLAN1 or WLAN2 are "networks" and neither have any computers connected. Both are to be client mode connections to two other routers.

In which case you don't need virtual interfaces at all. Each WLAN as its own interface with DHCP will work just fine.
