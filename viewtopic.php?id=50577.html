I've followed http://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/recipes/relayclient and have a working relayd bridge now.  The bridge is much better than the one I replaced with stock firmware, but it's still not stable and seems to require a reboot every day or two.

The "head end" access point is a D-Link DIR-655 which cannot run open source firmware due to a proprietary chipset.

I'm curious how stable people have found relayd bridging to be in practice.  I need uptimes of a month or better.  I had very similar problems - the bridge just Stopped Working - with the proprietary unit I replaced, although it was much more frequent.  I'm wondering if I'm dealing with bugs in the DIR-655's firmware - if most relayd bridges are stable, that'd give me a clue.

I have a Rosewill RNX-N360RT with RP-SMA connectors.  I'm considering replacing the DIR-655 with it if OpenWRT runs properly on the RNX-N360RT (allegedly a rebranded TP-LINK TL-WR941ND v2 and the firmware UI sure looks like a crappy rebrand job).  I need RP-SMA connectors on the head end because it's a point-to-point link using directional antennas.  Just wondering if relayd is known to normally be quite stable, or not, before I put tons of work into seeing if Barrier Breaker will work right on the RNX-N360RT yet or not.

I use a TP-Link WDR4300 as a wireless bridge with relayd. I bought it in January, and built an image based on r39403, and I had an uptime well over two months, and the bridge had passed more than 1.1 TB of traffic. If I hadn't rebooted it to upload a newer trunk image I'm confident it would still have been running, it never stopped working on its own. It's currently running r40540, and seems just as stable to me.
