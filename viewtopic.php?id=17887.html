http://wiki.openwrt.org/OpenWrtDocs/Har … WHR-HP-G54

According to the above information, the brcm-2.4 version should work. There're also instructions on how to flash your router.

The 8.09 has a web interface built-in already. So you may or may not need X-Wrt. Nonetheless, 8.09 is in Release Candidate status so I would recommend 7.09 to you if this is your first time.

Post #8
loyukfai
2 Dec 2008, 07:32

If you give your Ethernet adapter a fixed IP (e.g. 192.168.1.100) and ping 192.168.11.1, 192.168.1.1 and 192.168.11.100, what happens?

If there're responses, then you may try accessing the web interface. It could be that the DHCP server is not working.

If there's no response, I suspect that the router is still bricked and you may use tftp to flash it again. I had to try several times to get it right the last time I bricked my WBR2-G54.

Speaking of that, what's the "original firmware" you used? Buffalo or DD-WRT? I think falling back to Buffalo's is the safe bet here.

BTW, if you intend to try OpenWrt again, you may like to make sure the version 7.09 works on your router before trying 8.09

Lastly, the bin files are for specific Linksys and Motorola models. For the others you should use the trx files.

http://wiki.openwrt.org/OpenWrtDocs/Ins … c2ad371791

Hope this helps.
