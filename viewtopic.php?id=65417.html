While I agree that Max tends to be a "bad actor" I have been hoping for a real solution to the (big picture) question in this post. 

This is a current and topical issue for anyone using a travel router in public hot spots.  While the OP has attempted to solve this with MAC spoofing, I do not consider this a good solution in public venues.  I do not think this is as much an issue of secure or insecure wireless connections, as it is the TOS\Splash page not being passed from the STAtion side to the AP clients (my non-technical interpretation). 

Max's referenced solution appears to be designed for a known STAtion connection, which is the OPs case; however the OP has not objected to signing in (I believe) to the portal, but is unable to get the sign in page.    Neither this nor MAC spoofing is a great solution in public venues.

Travel routers have led to new logistical issues for which the current OpenWrt tools are just not adequate.  These are now a handful of ways to address the inability to connect when the STAtion changes (Limitations of a single radio), but this issue haunts me in almost every public location with a portal.  The only work around I have been able to find is to get the address of the local portal page and save it as a web shortcut.  Even then, in my most frequent hotel, the lease time indicated in the portal page is often not respected.  I have no issues with my PC directly connected.

While the OP is not using a travel router, his issues are more like a travel router than the typical bridge.  There is a true technical solution needed, though it is beyond my technical abilities.
