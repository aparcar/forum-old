Hi

I'm using openconnect on OpenWrt. It all works great, save for DNS resolution of names only on the VPN.

After a couple of hours of looking into what's happening, it seems this is the *defined* behaviour DNSMasq. I found this thread here http://lists.thekelleys.org.uk/pipermai â€¦ 03295.html

It points out two things:

1) That there's not really such a thing a nameserver priority
2) That even if you override this with the 'strict-order' option, it'll stop at the first failure

It seems there is a concept of 'DNS splitting', which the VPN server can be configured to do. Essentially, I think that means the VPN server responds with a list of DNSs for *specific* domains. However, the VPN I connect to doesn't do this, and just sets up two new nameserver entries.

I naively assumed that the nameservers setup by the VPN connection would be tried first, but I've found using the 'log-entries' setting that they're just forwarded on upstream.

If I completely shutdown dnsmasq, I see that it's a good citizen and restores /etc/resolv.conf with the contents of /tmp/resolv.conf.auto, and all of a sudden resolution of VPN-private domains works.

It seems strange to me that dnsmasq argues its behaviour is correct, whereas when you stop it and use the default (presumably somewhere in the depths of the kernel/libc?), it works in a way that seems more intuitive.

Anyway, after all that, what's the best practice here, in the absence of DNS splitting, to get DNS to work both when VPN is up and down?
