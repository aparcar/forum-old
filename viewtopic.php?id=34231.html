Some of the default rules for the firewall seem a little strange to me.

Two examples are the DHCP and DHCPv6 traffic rules that allow DHCP traffic from the WAN to the device.

My training and experience tell me that I should accept no unsolicited traffic from the WAN unless I am offering a service (HTTP, SMTP ...). Furthermore such traffic should be directed at hardened targets that reside on on a seperate network (DMZ, server farm ...).

My question is why are these rules setup this way.

I do not particularly like the term but it does describe the desired outcome fairly well, a port that is completely non-responsive to any probe or activity.
I think I picked the term up from Gibson Research they use it in their shields up service. It categorizes the result of a port scan in term that most non-techies will understand.

In practical terms it just means no forwarding between zones except under the control RW rules and using DROP rather than REJECT as both policy and targets in iptables.
