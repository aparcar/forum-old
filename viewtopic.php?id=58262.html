Lots of manufacturers change the amount of RAM/ROM from version to version, but it's never clear exactly why because they tend to not add features and the stock firmware usually doesn't push the limit anyway. The Archer C7 did this as did the good old WRT54G/WRT54GS. I assume it has something to do with the deals the OEM makes with the RAM & flash vendors. Or in this case, since it's being marketed as a high-end fancy router, maybe they just want to be the biggest and baddest spec wise.
