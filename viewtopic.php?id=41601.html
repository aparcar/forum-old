You should be able to determine GND by measuring for a 0 Ohms resistance to one of the plenty available GND sources (like SW4 at the white triangle). Most likely Rx and Tx pins are right next to GND. If you find a spot on the pcb that says something like 3V3, you can measure the resistance of your serial pins against that point (same here, test for 0 Ohms).
You can then go with trial and error for Tx and Rx, there are just 2 possible options.

Another way to find the supply and ground is to identify the ram chip part number and look up the data sheet.
Use an ohm meter to check continuity from rs232 header to these supply and
ground pins.
Then you have only two pins to work with. With your level shifter try the remaining pins one way, if they dont work reverse as noted above and try again.
~john
