Don't use all-in-one routers for everything. Use such router only as wireless AP.
For gateway take something like Lamobo PI. It must have 2+ core arm or x86 cpu.
I use dlink dir-825 as openvpn AES128 endpoint. Only have about 10 mbps. cpu is too slow for more.
With Banana PI I have 50 mbits from one core.

Well, Q-Atheros is not FOSS friendly anymore.. their new ath10k driver is a mess and has blobs etc. MediaTek seems to be the new FOSS-friendly company but their stuff is new and I heard some issues exist with VLANs not working etc.. hope someone can chip in on this.

Anyways, the C2600 is good value. There are many other routers (Linksys EA8500 and a netgear I forgot the model) that have the same SoC but cost more..

In my opinion the C2600 is even better overall than the WRT1900AC(x) (although it has only 32MB flash).. but the linksys is just far more popular because they advertised "open source ready", and maybe their WRT54G heritage came into play since its kinda a household icon..

The WRT1900ac(x) thread in this forum is very active, while the C2600 club seems to be a handful of people... perhaps they have no problems and hence the thread is not active

Careful when buying TPLinks (if you are in US) since they started locking down.. AFAIK the C2600 is still flashable..

Anyways shop around, there are many choices.
