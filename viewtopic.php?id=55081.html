You can find that in Network -> DHCP and DNS, scroll down to the "Static Leases" section at the bottom.

Hey there.

What exactly are you trying to do?

Hint your clients to specific IP addresses?

Well, the exact wording of your question is exactly this one.
That's something the DHCP server of OpenWRT is for.
So go to "Network => DHCP and DNS".
Search for section "Static Leases".
Hit "add" and enter one line per client computer.
After pressing "Save & Apply" you're done.

Fore your routers Ip address on either the WAN or LAN interface?

Just to be complete. According to your questions wording, that might be less important for you.
Go to "Network => Interfaces".
Here you have at least one called "LAN" and one called "WAN".
Edit the one you want to adjust.
"WAN" is set to "Protocol: DHCP" by default. You can adjust that, if your internet connection is not a DHCPed one.
"LAN" is set to "Protocol: Static" by default providing 192.168.0.1/24.

Suggestion: Upgrade and reset

To be honest, the OpenWRT UI of at least the two sections I mentioned is way better then every default interface of every SoHo router I know. And having worked as "setup and repair guy" part time during my study time I came accross several of those.

If anything is not as described, the former owner of the device has most likely messed something up. It's really easy to delete interfaces or modules as a whole. That could explain why you don't get along with your device and find it not intuitive.

In general: I would make sure to have the current stable 14.07 installed. If you're not interested in cutting edge stuff (which you're clearly not) that saves you a lot of trouble.
