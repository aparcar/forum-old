While there is work to support Atheros SoC platforms in Kamikaze it isn't ready yet, and even if it was, the WRT54G v7 has 2MB flash and 8MB ram.  This makes is virtually worthless for OpenWRT.  I don't know how the size of Kamikaze images compares to White Russian images as my Kamikazes contain more than just the standard WR images, but I would imagine that Kamikaze is slightly larger (due to using the 2.6 kernel, etc) which would make it even harder to fit the image into the available flash, let alone actually do anything with OpenWRT once it is installed.

Personally I consider any router with less than 4MB of flash and 16MB of ram worthless when I am looking at candidates for OpenWRT.  I know many of the devs feel the same way.

Really, just give up on the Linksys routers, all the new ones are over priced junk in comparison to what they used to be.  Ever since Cisco bought Linksys, they have continuously neutered all the Linksys gear probably so they can continue to justify the Cisco gear to enterprise clients.   There are a lot of better choices on the current market:

Buffalo WHR-G54S <-- Possibly the best OpenWRT capable router on the market for the price.
Buffalo WHR-HP-G54 <-- May have a slightly better range with the built in radio amp, but I haven't test it. 
Asus WL-500gP <-- 8mb flash/32mb ram/266Mhz cpu/2 USB 2.0 ports/mpci radio.  This router has incredible potential with OpenWRT.
Linksys WRTSL54GS <-- The only Linksys I would consider only if for some reason I could not get the ASUS WL-500gP, but the antenna is no removable.
