sirius42 wrote:

I have connected 3 pins to serial COM1(DB9) 2,3,5(GND) and have noise output - unreadable data on original FW 2.0.2.1 and 2.0.1.9

Watch out. You might have damaged the serial port of RT-N15. You need a TTL +3.3V RS232 level shifter. Buy a Nokia CA-42 USB cable to build your own serial cable. Search "CA-42" in the forum to find out how.

Excuse me that I only have little knowledge about Linux but I've been playing with minicom and it shouldn't be difficult.

Since your laptop has equipped with COM1, you can search eBay for RS232 converter or level shifter or try the keyword "max3232 OR max3233." I suppose you know the difference between max3232/max3233 and max232. The later is for TTL +5V level shifter and had better be avoided. The pros of max3232 level shifter is that it doesn't need a driver. Check "LaFonera Hardware Serial-Cable-Port - DD-WRT Wiki" for what a max3232 level shifter kit should look like.

I still recommend Nokia CA-42 data cable to you because it's very cheap and easy to get. Check "File:CA-42-small.jpg - NAS-Central Buffalo - The Linkstation Wiki" for what it should look like.

At first, I used MAX3243 to build the serial cable and later I learned that it's much eaiser with cellular phone USB data cable. BTW, the data cable I bought is not a CA-42.
