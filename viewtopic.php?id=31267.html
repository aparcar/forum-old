Hello everyone,
First up I have to say how awesome OpenWrt is.
A short while ago I purchased a Buffalo WZR-HP-G300NH but found the stock firmware (a cut back version of DD-WRT) too limiting. After trying for ages to get the full blown version of DD-WRT to work I thought I would give OpenWrt a bash and haven't looked back since!

First some background:

At work our photographers spend quite a bit of time wrestling with windows xp computers to get the card readers to work (most often I arrive to find windows has assigned a drive letter to the card reader that is already in use by a network share) and then have to copy, sort and rename the photos before they can work on them.
It occured to me that most of this task could be automated with a dedicated card reader attached to an off the shelf router with usb (a WZR-HP-G300NH or perhaps something simpler as wireless is not needed)

- Plug the CF or SD card into the reader
- Photos on the card are processed by exiftool
   - renamed according to exif data and moved to a sub folder of a windows share
     named by the value of "copyright" in the exif data (the photographers name)
       eg: exiftool "-directory<copyright" "-filename<createdate" -d "%Y%m%d_%H&M%S%%-c.%%e" "smbdestination"


Now to my question:

Is this possible with OpenWrt?
I imagine it could be triggered to start upon attachment of a new usb device or insertion of SD or CompactFlash card into the reader and hijack the lights on the front of the unit to indicate progress/completion

Has anyone done anything similar or have any guidance on where to start?
