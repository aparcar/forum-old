I give you a feedback re dlna. Simply forget it with openwrt or any NAS which has a lot more performance than a router. I didn't get happy with NAS from Qnap and Synology and a 2GHz Marvell cpu and 512MB RAM. Generally it works as long as you do not have a lot of files and you do not change / add a file. Indexing takes for hours and this starts after _every_ reboot or change for hours and at this time, there is a big chance, that you cannot use the dlna-server. So at the end inotify is useless and it doesn't matter if it works with Qnap or it doesn't work with Synology.

I recommend to use a Asrock E350M1 or ASUS C60M1-I motherboard (which is about 60€ incl. cpu) and a LC-Power LC-1320MI case. All in all I had to spend about 130€ incl. 4 GB RAM plus a harddrive for a dlna-server with excellent performance, using Ubuntu Server 12.04 and minidlna. I have no problem with covers, but I had with the mentioned NAS.  You have to think about how you start your DLNA-Client. Probably the easiest solution is to use S5 and press the button on the case with your fingers, when you need the dlna-server again. I am very unsure, if wake on lan makes sense (for me). Check which Zacate and Ontario motherboards if they support work on lan, most mobos don't or at least it is not mentioned in the manual.

BTW the  E350M1 is very fast (100000 files in 80min, the NAS needed more than 24hrs), when minidlna creates the index, wondering why it is faster than with my 3.4GHz-Dualcore. But both machines are very different. The E350M1 system is very minimalistic, while the X2 270 has "tons" of packages installed with 3 harddrives (5TB)
