Hi,

I am running Attitude Adjustment r30849 on my WNDR3800. I have noticed today during 2 file transfers that the LAN port becomes unresponsive during a Windows 7 file copy that goes through the switch. Here is my network configuration:

Laptop <--1 Gb/s--> WNDR <--100 Mb/s--> Switch <--100 Mb/s--> Switch
                                                                      |                                 |
                                                             VOIP adapter           HTPC, PS3, Xbox

The transfer is running at 11 MB/s which should not be taxing the WNDR's Gb switch. Top is reporting a CPU load of 4 - 5% while the transfer is running. I have conntracking on SMB connections disabled.

I also ran a ping from the laptop to the WNDR:

Pinging 192.168.0.1 with 32 bytes of data:
Reply from 192.168.0.1: bytes=32 time=657ms TTL=64
Reply from 192.168.0.1: bytes=32 time=629ms TTL=64
Reply from 192.168.0.1: bytes=32 time=689ms TTL=64
Reply from 192.168.0.1: bytes=32 time=661ms TTL=64

What could be causing this?

Edit: forgot to mention that the transfer is from the laptop to the HTPC.

So it looks like it might be my AR8131 LAN port causing this. Funny that I didn't notice this when I was running my laptop wired before, but if I switch to my Intel 5300 wireless I can ping it at ~5ms while transferring files.

Edit: It was the Atheros drivers. Turning off flow control in the advanced driver settings (Windows 7) fixed it and it now pings at <1ms under load.
