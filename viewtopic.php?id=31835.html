The wiki page http://wiki.openwrt.org/toh/start is probably the place to start.

The most common devices talked about on the forum will probably be the best ones to go for, as you'll get the most help and support. I'd suggest looking at the following:

- TP-Link TL-WR1043ND
- Ubiquiti RouterStation / RouterStation Pro
- PCEngines Alix
- Buffalo WZR-HP-G300NH
- Netgear WNDR3700 (although watch out for newer versions which apparently use Broadcom chipsets - these won't work with OpenWRT)

Hope this helps,

Sam

samcrawford
13 Sep 2011, 13:59

With the Alix boards you'll need to buy all of the components separately. So you'll want:

- The board itself
- An enclosure
- A power supply
- A CompactFlash card
- (Optional) A mini-PCI WiFi card
- (Optional) One or more wireless pigtails
- (Optional) One or antennas

You can buy them directly from the PC Engines website, or through a reseller. They seem to be fairly widely available in in Europe and North America, but I can't speak for other places.

Thanks

Sam
