Around a year ago I successfully installed my first OpenWrt. Now I already migrated all my routers to OpenWrt. Also I gave second life to some of older unused  routers thus avoiding some cable installations.
Totally I have four wireless routers. Two of them are main ones that are attached to public internet and working as firewalls too. One in my home and another in office.

While they are working more stable as original firmware ( at least I do not need restart them regularly ), they still has some problems.
For office one - looks like wireless loses some network information. It does not go off, but internet is lost (no pings to DNS etc.) Wireless connections disconnect & connect on PC helps for that.
For home one - after some couple of months of successful and nice working it started to drop speeds. As it looks like bufferbloat, I installed SQM, that helped and now it is more stable. While it worked much better, I would like to be more informed what is happening in my router and network.
Also I have the question - why this problem appears only after a few monts?
Now I compared two pings to public internet - from router and from my PC within my LAN. While from router ping times was stable and small (within 3ms) my local pings was humpy-jumpy so that sometimes goes to thousands of milliseconds.

As there could be multiple reasons for the problems above (from my OpenWRT config to activities in my network), than I would like to find some ways/tools to quickly find the typical problems. Of course I would like to find out how to diagnose problems and better tune up my router - that is one part, but second is be informed about problems in my WAN (f.ex. call to provider - why so slow) connections and/or LAN (f.ex. ask kiddies why you are generating so much traffic or look for viruses etc.) connections. So I am also looking for some good monitoring and analyzing tools too.

I would appreciate some recommendations, links etc. from what to start from other users experience.
