Given the fact that there are still new routers shipping in 2013 with 32M ram and 4M flash it makes sense to develop this tiny alternatives.

From a development pov, when setting resource constraints aside, it would still take us more effort to modify dbus to do what we want than it took to write ubus, netifd and the related libraries from scratch.

Desktop distributions carry around a lot of legacy stuff (Debian........), this slows down the adoption of new software designs (systemd, Wayland, etc) and thus also the development, since development requires an active user-base.

Operating systems for Mobile devices could/should be able to deliver much faster development of code and system/software design by dropping all the legacy stuff. Sadly their closed, so they got no active user-base!

OpenWrt on the other hand, is pretty cool. It seems to be the most free of legacy stuff. So, could the efforts of Luci2 be re-used?

Yes, only someone actually working on a system for this hardware devices can decide that, but in order to do so, they would have to know about the existence of Luci2. You get my point?
