Dear all,

I'm puzzled that after several hours of research, I still cannot get all details of what I need for proper German Telekom VDSL connections. More likely than not, I will switch to a "Magenta Zuhause M" contract (Telekom Germany), despite the fact that they don't support T.38 for fax over IP. Because generally I'm a happy customer there, and we have access to slightly cheaper options as the contract is supported by my employer.
So what kind of hardware should I buy? I don't want a FritzBox if I can avoid it, and I want to restrict to as few devices as possible. Best option: One router with integrated modem, one VoIP DECT phone base station.

So far on the wishlist: The router/modem should have

  1. VDSL2 support (necessary for Telekom VDSL50 and above?) and ADSL2+ support (just in case)

  2. Annex B/J support

  3. ITU-T G.993.5 support (should be required for bandwidths > 16 Mbit/s, but I read other claims, too)

  4. preferably OpenWRT support

Also, I'm unsure about whether additional capabilities are required to ensure QoS for phone calls from a VoIP PBX (thinking about something simple like a Siemens Gigaset C430 IP) connected to the router (VLAN tagging?), and if so, if they are de-facto standard for all newer models. Things used to be so simple a few years ago...

I had a look at TP-Link, Netgear, Linksys and D-Link devices. The closest match seems to be the TP-Link TD-W9980B(DE), a.k.a. N600, which I guess will be OpenWRT-supported at some point in time, but it only has G.993.2, not G.993.5.
Also, I could not find separate VDSL2-Modems except full FritzBoxes which can be turned to pure modem mode.

So, is there really no way around FritzBoxes for the time being if I want to enjoy the 50 Mbit/s connections via Telekom VDSL?

(BTW: Our current phones are three standard analog phones plus analog fax, connected to an old ISDN PBX. AFAIU, all current routers with integrated PBX and TAE sockets or old-school a/b-connectors for plain old analog phones do not offer PBX functionality under OpenWRT? Also, are there affordable home-user router/PBX combos with more than two TAE sockets?)


Thanks a lot in advance for your advise!
Alexander

        Well the vigor 130 does support G.993.5 vectoring and has been used successfully on Deutsche Telekom lines with active vectoring so the specificition page seems a bit outdated. You will need a G.993.5 if you are on a vectoring Outdoor-MSAN, otherwise I believe DTAG will fall back to VDSL16 without vectoring, not too attractive. The TP-Link does not (yet?) support vectoring to my knowledge (which admittedly is limited). I do not know any other pure VDSL modem.

        ADSL2+ and VDSL ITU standards use sequential letters to organize their annexes:

Annex J for ADSL2+ is a bandplan/profile that reuses the complete POTS/ISDN range for upload and can offer up to 2.8 Mbps uplink on a ADSL2+ link (see http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G.992.3_Annex_J).

For VDSL2 Annex B is a collection of band plans and other technicalities in use in Region B (Europe) and Annex J is not yet assigned (as far as I know)

So for VDSL2 you need an Annex B VDSL2 modem and for decent ADSL2+ you need an Annex J ADSL2+ modem. This is a bit confusing, but also logical once one accepts that the annexes always reference a "base document".
