Devices with 16Mb RAM, as the original Linksys WRT54G, were fully supported and working fine until Backfire 10.03.1.

There is a forum Topic about running Barrier Breaker 14.07 on WRT54GL:
  https://forum.openwrt.org/viewtopic.php?id=51729
In that Topic a new feature; zram is mentioned. zram is now part of 15.05 and I decided to give it a try.

What I find (with my WRT54GL):
- The standard CC images are too big to fit on a typical (WRT54GL) flash
- The image builder can easily generate smaller images (including zram-swap, excluding LuCl, WiFi-drivers, ppp-stuff)
- zram-swap is of no significant advantage for this class of devices
- opkg fails to install packages due to lack of memory
- sysupgrade is really hard to run, due to lack of memory
- WiFi (b43/b43legacy) does not practially work (I had some success without encryption)

There are certainly good uses for a 16Mb-device that do not require WiFi.
Despite zram-swap, I find CC less suitable than BB for these devices.

There are 86 devices listed as supported with just 16Mb of RAM. Not all of them are brcm47xx. I don't know if other targets work better (atheros, ar7, brcm63xx, ramips, adm5120, mcs814x, ixp4xx).

I am not writing this to complain. I only want to share what someone might find useful.
For more reading: http://techfindings.one/archives/2075

Anyone with more luck?
