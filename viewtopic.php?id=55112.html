The Archer D7 has a Broadcom 63xx modem, which there are no drivers for in OpenWrt. I wouldn't hold my breath for it.

Many Lantiq based devices are supported, also see ADSL2+ devices. I think you'll have better luck finding a suitable device starting from lists of DSL devices and working your way through them. I don't know if any of them have an 11ac radio.

An ac radio combined with a DSL modem doesn't make a lot of sense in the marketplace.  If you only have DSL for internet, the ac radio is no advantage over n or even g unless you are also doing heavy LAN traffic such as serving files or video locally.  Most people interested in high performance prefer two boxes.

makro
16 Jan 2015, 00:44

I don't know. If you would like to contribute, you could send an email on the -devel list and ask if anyone knows this device/chipset, and is interested in working on support for it if you donate a device for development.

Also, I actually found an 11ac DSL modem - Asus DSL-AC68U. It's an RT-AC68U with a (A|V)DSL modem in it. I can pretty much guarantee that this device will not be supported by OpenWrt, given its Broadcom chipset and radios. I've been looking for a replacement modem myself recently, and OpenWrt support would be nice, but not required in this case. I haven't found any modem supported by OpenWrt with a 5 GHz radio (be it 11na or 11ac). The TP-Link TD-W8980 is supported, but its 5 GHz radio is not (2.4 GHz is supported). So I think you need to evaluate your requirements - OpenWrt support or wireless features/connectivity.
