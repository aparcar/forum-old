The problem I see with the SP232ACP is that everything on the datasheet refers to using a +5V supply.  You need +3.3V part.  I used the MAX3233E (http://www.maxim-ic.com/quick_view2.cfm/qv_pk/2008), which has the added benefit of not requiring any external capacitors (might want a 0.1uF bypass cap across the power pins though).  I got mine as free samples from the website...I suggest the MAX3233ECPP, which is the DIP package.

gdijim wrote:

DSUBPCSF9 9-pin female PC straight connector - I have this
IDCMH10 10-pin shrouded male IDC header - I do not have this, I have some ribbon cable, which the guy said I could solder directly to the serial port on the router's board.  Will that be okay?

You could solder the ribbon cable directly to the PCB....but assuming that you are new to this, I would recommend not doing so.  Bite the bullet and find the header.  The link you have shows a shrouded one...I wouldn't worry about getting one with a shroud.  You can  get larger, "breakaway"  headers, which have more pins, but you break off just the size you need.  Perhaps you can obtain one of these more easily?

I suggest getting the header, a new transceiver part, a small PCB, and then just solder everything together.  Do the router bit last, so you get some practice first   It should be a pretty easy job, especially if you get the part that doesn't require external caps.

Good luck!
