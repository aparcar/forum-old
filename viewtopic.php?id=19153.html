I have not tried the latest  stock image file, but have 3 NSLU2s flashed with post-RC2 images built with the make menuconfig build process.  For past stock images, I found that the default ip address, 192.168.1.1, conflicted with my router--and also that the NSLU2 itself tried to be a DHCP server.  To get around that problem I directly linked my PC & the slug with a crossover cable, gave the PC a static IP address (a pain), and then was able to connect to the slug on 192.168.1.1 and go in and change the ip in /etc/config/network and also disable whatever was providing DHCP--I think maybe dmask.

That was what I forgot, the standard openwrt image does not behave like a "normal" nslu2 but tries to be a router :-)
That was exactly what I had disabled in my own builds (but that was also more then a year ago).

So I did :

* /etc/init.d/dnsmasq disable  (to turn dnsmasq off)
* /etc/init.d/firewall disable (to turn the firewall off)
* vi /etc/config/network to swith lan from static to dhcp and put a # infront of type

reboot and all was well
