The default web interface of OpenWrt, LuCI, is a Lua application, not PHP. Are you going to run a PHP application of your own on it? If so, look at the package size of the PHP4/PHP5 packages you need in the packages directory.

You can use tags in the wiki to find routers that support the features you need, such as amount of flash memory, RAM, single- or dual-band and so on.

Post #6
metai
26 May 2014, 04:43

The PHP/LUA remark was warranted because PHP takes up quite a bit of resources. You would probably be looking at a router with lots of flash space, memory, and a decent CPU. Also, if you want to replace the standard web interface with one driven by PHP, you've got quite a task on your hand.

As for your original question, "popularity" really isn't a very good qualifier. There are no (published) numbers on the "popularity" of routers. If you take a look at the forums, some brands seem to come up more often than others. But of course, that highly depends on one's perception, and the forums are certainly not a very reliable indicator.

I would recommend you make your decision based on what machines are readily available to you, within your price bracket, that fit the baseline hardware requirements -- and then check for the availability of OpenWrt on these machines. Once a router is fully supported by OpenWrt, that support usually doesn't go away, regardless whether the machine is particularly "popular" or not.

According to Google, Linksys WRT54GL is the most discussed router in this forum (3310 hits) among various models I tried. The TP-Link WR1043ND, which seems to be regularly discussed from what I see, isn't even close at 2210 hits. The WRT54GL has 4 MB of flash and 16 MB RAM, you won't be able to fit PHP and a web server on it. Does this help you?

Edit: OpenWrt installations don't "report back" anywhere, so I can't think of any more reliable statistics. Statistics from downloads.openwrt.org will be misleading, as they don't cover images built from source. People who do large deployments probably won't download image files per router either.
