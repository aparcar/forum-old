I have two wireless cards in my x86 based machine. One is a Compex WLE350NX (ath9k) 802.11a/b/g/n 2/5Ghz, well known to be supported, while the other is a Z-com AC-920 (ath10k). These are both plugged into a Mikrotik RB14e mini PCI-e to PCI-e adapter.

Both these cards are recognized and can be configured. The WLE350NX is in the US regulatory domain and the ZCom AC-920 is in the AM regulatory domain.

Both these cards have very low txpower and nothing I do can change them. Issuing an "iwconfig wlan1 txpower 20" has no effect at all. It completes the command without error, but the txpower remains the same.

root@OpenWrt:~# iwconfig wlan0
wlan0     IEEE 802.11an  Mode:Master  Tx-Power=0 dBm
          RTS thr:off   Fragment thr:off
          Power Management:off

root@OpenWrt:~# iwconfig wlan1
wlan1     IEEE 802.11abgn  Mode:Master  Tx-Power=5 dBm
          RTS thr:off   Fragment thr:off
          Power Management:off

When booting the same machine off Ubuntu 13.10 or Fedora 17, both these cards have much higher txpower: 27dbm for the Compex and 18dbm for the ZCom.

Is there something different about the way openwrt handles the ath9k and ath10k cards that I'm missing? I assume there must be since both Ubuntu and Fedora don't seem to have this problem.
