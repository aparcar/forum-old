I have been working on a mesh networking project recently where I configure a singe radio to have an adhoc network and to provide an AP for client access.

In the past (bench testing and controlled outdoor tests) I've have observed no trouble with ap+adhoc configuration.  Recently I moved to an outdoor environment where 3 nodes are communicating over distances between 50-300m, and client devices are connected at each node.  For concreteness, Imagine a right triangle where each corner is a node, and the sides adjacent to the right angle are 50m and 250m, respectively.  All nodes see each other with reasonable signal strength (>-65dB).

Diagram:

B
|\
|  \
|   \
A---C


With any two nodes powered, there is no packet loss on the link.  With all three nodes powered, the A-C link experiences >95% packet loss, while A-B and B-C remain perfectly functional.  (it is possible this only occurs when a client is connected to one of the APs).  I have never experienced this before the 2011-11-01 compat-wireless package (last time I built the drivers was probably early september?)

Is there something that changed recently that might have caused the issue above?  Suggestions on how to further debug / fix?

An interesting additional piece of data:  OLSR believes that the link having 95% packet loss is perfectly fine, which suggests that OLSR "hello" messages are being sent and received successfully while other data, including ping, is not.

Thoughts anyone?
