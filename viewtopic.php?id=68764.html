My woes with my RT3070L adapter turned out to be due to the length of the USB cable, not software. The adapters work fine on all my PCs, which leaves me with one of these three theories:

1) The D +/- voltage is weaker on these travel routers
2) These travel routers induce more noise into the USB cable than a PC
3) These travel routers use hardware that is more sensitive to noise

Since I need the long cable I think I need to try adding a second ferrite core on the router end (there is already one on the adapter end) or try a USB amplifier. I don't have either on hand but I can probably find an old cable to salvage another choke from.

Anyone have any ideas or overcame long USB runs (20 to 30 feet, IIRC standard is rated for 15 feet) with these travel routers (GL Inet family, WR703N, etc)?

I disagree with the poster above.  Whenever possible, extend the baseband instead of the antenna.  At 2.4 GHz, losses in even a few feet of good quality coax are significant.  Cell phone towers are now mounting the radio system at the top of the tower, directly connected to the antenna, and running baseband signals over either fiber or Gb Ethernet to the network building below.

There is some really lousy wire being integrated into no-name products these days.  Do not trust the gauge marking on the jacket, it may actually contain very little copper.
