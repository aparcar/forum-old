jlanza wrote:

After installing it my router doesn't boot up and at the end the only light on is the power led.

My case was a bit different from yours. I had two units of my WRT54GS v3 flashed with a DD-WRT v23sp1 firmware. After I did some configuration, a reboot caused both my WRT54GS bricked with power LED flashes fast. To recover my WRT54GS v3 units, all I did was to issue an nvram clear through a serial console. Once that was done, my WRT54GS did an auto reboot with and performed an auto nvram commit. Then, both my WRT54GS v3 units came back and are now flashed and running with a DD-WRT v24sp1 firmware with no problem. I would recommend to get a USB/Serial-console cable to access its serial-console port to first check its boot scripts already properly configured to run the OpenWRT firmware.
