Hi,

I bought a cheap DIR-825 that was sold "spares or repair" and without antennas or power supply and am hoping to recover it.

It's B2 hardware.

It's labelled 12V 2A and I only had 12v 1A or 5v 2A power supplies so I used a 12v 2A car power supply to test but observed the same results when trying the 5v 2A mains adapter.

Anyway, on initially powering up, all I could get out of it was the blue DLink logo lighting up - no other lights, even when plugging a laptop into the ethernet ports.

However, straight after removing the case lid, I plugged it back in and got an orange power light, then the internet light, then it booted up and I could successfully plug in the laptop, get a dhcp lease and connect to the web interface on 192.168.0.1.

I could also successfully connect to the wireless and get a dhcp lease.

I was just trying to work out why the 5Ghz radio wasn't working (couldn't see the SSID on either an iPhone or Macbook Pro), even after turning off the 2.4Ghz in the options when the car power cut out (it's on a timer when the engine isn't running).

Since then, I can only get either just the blue Dlink logo light, or if I unplug and twist/move the case a bit and plug back in, I can sometimes get a constant orange power light but nothing else seems to happen.

I tried powering on while holding down the reset button for a while which several places seems to say initiates a firmware recovery mode, but the power light stayed orange.

I was wondering whether there was anyone with technical experience of these units could offer some thoughts on:

- Thoughts on why I only get a blue logo and then if I twist/fiddle with the unit I can then get an orange light or more? - think it's a faulty pcb and movement is causing it to work?

- What is a solid orange power led showing?

- Any tips on recovery, given that there was life and successful operation of it for a few minutes?

- Can you successfully run these on 5v 2A or 12v 1A?

- Less important now, but any idea why the 5Ghz radio wasn't working? - the 2.4Ghz was and both the 2.4Ghz and 5Ghz led's were on....

Thanks,

Ian
