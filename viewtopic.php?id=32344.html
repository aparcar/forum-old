The simplest solution for a backup on Debian would be this called by crond once a day:

rm /var/lib/dokuwiki/data/pages/backup.tar.bz2
tar -cjf ~/backup.tar.bz2 /var/lib/dokuwiki/data/ --exclude /var/lib/dokuwiki/data/cache
mv ~/backup.tar.bz2 /var/lib/dokuwiki/data/pages

While you are at it, this needs shell access as well:
http://www.dokuwiki.org/namespace_templates

We do have template here:
http://wiki.openwrt.org/meta/templates
If you choose the hardlink-solution, the templates remain editable.

Orca, watch out! Another bump. Make sure you do not trip and fall.
Do you really think bumping openwrt.org admins to death will accomplish anything? Instead of beating your head against the wall give site admins some suggestions. Your robots.txt suggestion is obviously wrong.

Here are my suggestions:
1. Add a description meta tag to all pages. Currently none of WIKI pages have a "meta name='description'" tag. Having a good human readable description tag is important.
2. Fix the "meta name='keywords'" tag. For example the Asterisk page discussed above has the following keywords tag "doc,howto,voip.asterisk". No wonder Google does not find it. There is not even a mention of OpenWRT or a separate word Asterisk in there.
3. Having a sitemap.xml can also be critical. With so many pages, I actually think that the absence of a sitemap is a -1 for an overall OpenWRT pagerank. I have noticed that openwrt.org does not have a publicly available sitemap referenced from robots.txt. It could very well be that this is a sitemap.xml issue.
4. Finally make sure that you can browse the wiki in ELinks. If you cannot do it in ELinks, your chances of being indexed are 0.
