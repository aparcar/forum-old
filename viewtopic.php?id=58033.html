Let's try using LuCi:

* Go to "Network > Interfaces", and edit your LAN network.
* In Physical Settings, enable "Bridge interfaces", then select both the eth0.1 and radio0 interfaces.
* In "DHCP Server", check "Ignore interface"

Now your ethernet and wifi interfaces should be bridged together and act as a single network; your wireless clients will get an IP address from the main router (just as the OpenWRT device gets its IP address). You will also have just one zone in your firewall.
