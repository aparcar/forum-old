Do not connect to a 9 pin RS-232 serial port.   The voltage levels are wrong, and will cause damage.  Also "1" bits are a negative voltage on the port and "0" bits are positive, which is opposite what the SoC requires. You can connect if you have a converter that inverts the logical signal and outputs at 3.3 volts.  Basically using the level converter chip that drives the RS-232 lines in reverse.

Most people don't mess with 9 pin ports at all and use a "USB to TTL serial" adapter.
