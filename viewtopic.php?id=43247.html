No not really.

At the lowest level you need a radio capable of operating in the band you need. These wifi routers only work in the 2.4ghz band  (and sometimes the 5ghz wifi band)

The way one of these routers could possibly be involved is if you found a linux supported USB  zwave dongle     and plugged it into a router with a USB port... and then somehow used the router as a controller... but that doesn't sound like something you're interested in.

OpenWrt can help by providing a net-enabled means to access a microprocessor (usually through usb-serial) which can communicate with your 433mHz senders and receivers.
