Hi Everyone;
I just picked up a WNDR3800 and I am running a trunk build on it. I've run iperf tests to get a sense of the limit of the speed of the wireless and i noticed something I found strange. When getting data from the router I will get 70-100MBit/s which is much less than the connection speed but I can understand there being many reasons for that. However sending data only happens at about 30-35Mbit/s.

Why is there such a difference here based on direction.

Test was done with a laptop running arch linux with a Intel Advanced-N 6205 wireless card if that is relevant.

In ideal conditions the limit is almost always in the CPU.

Sending data at the access point side requires less CPU cycles -> data you receive at your laptop with higher speed.
Receiving data at the access point side requires more CPU effort. <- data you send from your laptop

You may reach the CPU limit at the router when you send data from your laptop.

Why sending data requires less CPU? well I have no answer...
