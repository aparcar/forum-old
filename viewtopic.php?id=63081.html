I flashed my wrt1200ac with:

The first thing we notice is the the wlan comes to a crawl. The lan still transfers data fine but pinging the router and other lan devices performs very poorly. Pinging from the router to devices on the network is fine. I perform software reboot via Luci and still have the same issue. I cycle power and it comes back strong a gain.

No, it is not my modem. The network is soggy. Never did this until flashing with openwrt. I know it is my network by pinging all over within the lan/wan reveals it and it's not every device on my network either it's openwrt. I have done very little by the way of configuration. Just enabled wlan

I am considering going back to stock and then to dd-wrt. I have read about some issues with dd-wrt too. The stock firmware doesn't have Luci. I love Luci and really don't want to dump her.

Also, note that I flashed back to linksys firmware and thoroughly tested connections prior to flashing dd-wrt. I did that due to the fact that the hardware may have been failing even though it is a fairly new router. Needless to say it did well and only after ruling out a hardware issue I flashed dd-wrt. To users of the wrt1200ac. The linksys firmware is fine unless you need to have more specialized function. I don't recommend changing if the router already does what you need it to do. In other words, if it ain't broke don't fix it. I have an Engineering back ground and live by that motto.

One thing I should have done is a reset before disbanding openwrt. For now dd-wrt is working. When I get another wrt1200ac I will do more extensive troubleshooting.
