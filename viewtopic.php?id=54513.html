I would back the screws out and put a serial cable on it and reflash thru the bootloader, because anything else is too much messing around.  If you aren't equipped to do that, consider:

Open the 3500 stock firmware with a hex editor and look in the header (the first couple hundred bytes) for something obvious like '3500' that you could change to 3600 like you were thinking.  You will probably need to regenerate the CRC then to make it look like a valid firmware.  There is a neat project on Google Code called "firmware_mod_kit" which can do that.

Turn router on with an Ethernet cable plugged into one of the LAN ports and notice if the LAN light for that port comes on within a few seconds of power up.  If it does come on (only to go out later after the 3600 firmware boots), the bootloader is probably capable of accepting a telnet or tftp connection.  Specifics on how to do that are likely written up somewhere on the web.
