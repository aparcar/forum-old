All new Airmax devices have this issue and it's in the wiki and in multiple threads on this forum. https://wiki.openwrt.org/toh/ubiquiti/airmaxm Read the special note in the wiki about down grading the firmware before you install Openwrt. The only way to fix it is tftp ubnt firmware back on to the device.
This is the process I used last time I did what you have done.

1. Open up the device and locate the serial jumper, labeled J1.



2. Connect the USB-TTL Cable to the jumper, which has the following pinout:
---1. 3.3V+ (Red: Do not connect)
---2. S-in (GREEN)
---3. S-out (White)
---4. GND(Black)



3. Open up a terminal program, such as tera-term, and configure it to the following settings:
---Bits per second: 115200
---Data Bits : 8
---Stop Bits : 1
---Parity : None
---Flow control : None



4. Plug the POE adapter into the wall.



5. Plug the LAN side of the device into your PC.



6. Configure your PC IP settings to the following:
---IP Address: 192.168.1.123
---Netmask : 255.255.0.0

press any key when prompted to stop boot

urescue -f -e
tftp v 5.5.11 to 192.168.1.20
reboot
urescue -f -e
tftp v 5.6.xxx to 192.168.1.20
reboot
gui flash back to 5.5.11

Then you can load Openwrt on the device safely.
