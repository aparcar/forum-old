fscussel wrote:

Is it possible to connect an USB wifi dongle to a openWRT router lan port thru an USB TO LAN Adapter and use that 2nd radio?

No, it is not possible to use an USB cable between an USB wifi dongle and an USB TO LAN Adapter. These are USB devices and need to be connected to an USB host, such as an openwrt router with USB ports.

fscussel wrote:

Is it possible to connect an USB wifi dongle to a openWRT router lan port thru an USB TO LAN Adapter and use that 2nd radio?

If you meant to use this USB Female to Male Ethernet RJ45 Connector to bridge between one of the RJ45 ports on your router and a USB/WiFi dongle, then I don't think it is possible unless you can somehow to power your USB/WiFi dongle. However, this USB CAT5/CAT5E/6 RJ45 Ethernet Extender claims it is a self-powered. If so, perhaps this may help unless its self-powered rating is below the power needed by your USB/WiFi dongle.

For the price of a USB CAT5/CAT5E/6 RJ45 Ethernet Extender, perhaps you may wanna contemplate to get a Seagate DockStar / GoFLEX Home / GoFLEX NET device from e-bay or even any PogoPlug device to bridge between one of the RJ45 ports on your router and a USB/WiFi dongle. Not only will you be able to achieve your goal, but you can also use the DockStar to host your PBX system, files, printer, etc., for the price of the device. If you take this route, then you will probably need to reconfigure your device as a bridge. I had tried to convert my Seagate DockStar into a router as shown here and it didn't work. However, if you read this post, you will notice I finally managed to get it working after some helps from Paul Fertser.

Note: ONLY some PogoPLug devices are supported by OpenWRT.
