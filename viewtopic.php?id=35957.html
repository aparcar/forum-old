Well, unless you want to isolate and NAT yourself, plug into LAN and receive DHCP IPs from across the street.

In that case LAN and WLAN are switched together and no routing takes place. So disable dnsmsq, configure DHCP on the bridge interface and you're done.

But the question is if any perceived slowness is really due to the routing itself.
