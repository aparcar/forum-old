Try with wireshark (and turn the firewall off). A blinking "Maintenance" LED is usually a good sign.
If the upload failed, the router may by defaults be configured to 192.168.0.1 (at least TL-WR741ND is and those two are relatives, AFAIK.).
Any ARP requests in wireshark, when booting the router with reset key pressed?

Blinking LED for the switchport seems to be available in any state. The relevant leds are config, wlan, usb, etc - but if these aren't initialized there's no clue...

I recently bricked a tl-wr1043nd by flashing it with a self-assembled image (ImageBuilder, probably oversize through sysupgrade). Despite having flashed a new factory firmware, that should contain the new bootloader, I could get it to answer My brick is similar to what you describe, at a first glance, but I have never had a wr841n and the 741nds I had where difficult to restore - until the 4.21 I just recovered.
