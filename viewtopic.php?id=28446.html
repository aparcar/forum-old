It easiest to use both the hosts and ethers files.  The format is as follows.  Use your own host names, IP and MAC addresses, of course.  Yes, you will need to at least "reload" dnsmasq whenever you change the hosts or ethers files.

-John


/etc/hosts:

    192.168.99.18 host-a
    192.168.99.40 host-b
    192.168.99.12 host-c

/etc/ethers:

    00:11:22:33:44:55:20 host-a
    00:11:22:33:44:55:05 host-b
    00:11:22:33:44:55:89 host-c

Then do:

/etc/init.d/dnsmasq reload
