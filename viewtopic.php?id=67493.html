You can do this and the GL-AR300M is one of the most robust devices in it's class.

VPN is an add on for standard OpenWrt (packages required), but GL.inet has built some of this into their GUI.  I am not aware of anyone using this with a home server, but standard functionality works as a fall back plan.  Their current implementation only supports TUN.

Many have requested the fall back features you are looking for, but I am not aware of anyone writing a scripts that do so and are published.

As a traveler you may be interested in this site:  https://www.hotelwifitest.com/
Unfortunately these devices are all a small fraction of the computing power of a PC and VPN runs slower than on a PC.  With a good connection you may get speeds in the lower teens, but running wireless you are probably less.  The real issue is the speeds available in hot spots, which becomes a gating factor, and then you get some fraction of that with VPN on top.
