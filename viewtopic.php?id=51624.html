I opened the case to get to the serial port of my Linksys WRT1900AC.  For anyone who is interested in the details of how the case is put together, I put some pictures online:

http://www.smugmug.com/gallery/n-7PLVB/

There are only four screws in the bottom to hold the case together - one in the center of each foot.  Once the screws are removed, the blue front part slides forward easily.

I'm sure there is a way to get into the rest of it without breaking it, but you probably need some small, thin tool to slide in and push some of the latches open.  There are 5 latches across the back of the unit and just one towards the front on each side.

One thing to mention is the construction around the rear feet - the top half of the case makes up part of each rear foot.  The two halves come apart by sliding the top half directly upwards.  That's especially difficult due to the latches that you have to pry loose (or break some of them as I did), but even with the latches released, it was still quite hard to get the top off - seems that it presses together rather tightly.  One thing that may help is to put something under the top half's part of the foot and pressing down on the bottom half.
