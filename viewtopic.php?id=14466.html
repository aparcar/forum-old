Try this:
0) Connect through intermediate switch.  Set PC to static IP address in 192.168.1.x range.
This is to ENSURE that PC always shows link up instead of waiting for it to notice the
router is powered up and making LAN available, which can take too long especially if
boot_wait was not set.
1) Power up
2) Quickly send a TFTP upload attempt

This may take a number of tries but eventually you will hit the timing where
boot-loader stops to accept a TFTP upload.

I find it MUCH simple to connect a serial console.  From the console it takes
10 seconds to put the router in upload mode and fix it.
