I recently unbricked the WR710N for the first time.

Seems like your list is right.

Yes you'll need a soldering kit and a USB to serial adapter. And some wires, preferably jumper wires with pin headers, so you can easily connect them to the USB adapter. I used an adapter with the CP2102 chip. There are other chips, of course. My adapter came with some jumper wires, but that probably depends on where you buy it from. Of course, you also can't reuse them once you solder them on so I suggest you order some wires separately.

My adapter supports both a 3.3v and 5v output, but the thing is, you don't need to connect it because the router is already powered on when you plug it in.

Then you have to solder it on. Although I would suggest soldering on the ground wire somewhere else, because for me it didn't stay on. I somehow glued it on because i didn't realize I could solder it anywhere else.
