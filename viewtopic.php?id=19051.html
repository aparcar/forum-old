This is a sort of re-post in the effort of keeping the discussion on-focus...

I'm looking for an economical access point or router (currently being produced/sold) which has the ability to operate two entirely separate networks at 2.4 and 5 GHz. I need 802.11g at 2.4 GHz and 802.11a at 5 GHz. OpenWRT compatibility is important, obviously, or I'd not be asking here, but I also don't need any fancy functionality.

I don't require a huge amount of power output, but good sensitivity is important. This probably means an Atheros chipset. I'm looking for something pretty inexpensive as here, around $100 or less online retail price. I know I could toss something together with a board, miniPCI cards, etc. but the cost quickly goes over $100.

I'm really looking for just the board, I don't care about the enclosure, antennas, etc. It would be extra nice if the system had an extra PCI slot, or USB, but such things are not so crucial.

I've been looking around and there's such a profusion of routers hardware out there and my needs are so specific, I was hoping some OpenWRT users might be able to direct me to some hardware or at least some directions to explore.

Hi,

I would suggest a PCEngines' ALIX-Board as a foundation. I have them in several configurations indoor and outdoor here. Seems to be some reliable piece of hardware IMHO - you can have them in several flavours from 1-LAN + 2 minipci to 3-LAN + 1 minipci.

Would be ~ 150 $ for a complete indoor setup, though.

But otherwise you have full flexibility with regard to WLAN-minipci-cards to deploy (a or g or n, easy to replace in future) , USB and a decent amount of RAM and flash (CF). And it's x86, if that's a point for you.

HTH
