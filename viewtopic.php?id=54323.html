I managed all my previous problems, and currently running OpenWRT on a Netgear 3500L. I'm really satisfied about this!
But there is a problem. I made an extrootfs, via a 4-port USB adapter. I put on this adapter a flash drive, and 2 autopowered USB hard drives, both formatted in NTFS.
Well: transferring files between those two disks or among them and any other disk in the LAN is very slow. I constantly get 1-1.5 MB/s or less (sometimes as low as 750KB/s), and network link doesn't make any difference( to be clearer, I run my LAN via powerline adapters, but that is not the issue: my main PC is directly linked to the router, and transfer figures are the same). I tried out three different USB hubs, but performance stays about the same for all.
What can be the culprit? What can I try?
Thanks,
Stargate64

Post #2
drawz
1 Dec 2014, 06:08

NTFS is very slow under linux and tends to be CPU intensive, so this is a major problem for slower CPUs like we have in our routers. Your best bet is to switch the format of the drives to a linux-native format, like ext3.
