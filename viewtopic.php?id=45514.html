The price tag on this thing is calling at me..

1.0GHz sunxi (ARM-based) processor
- already has an opensource linux distro- https://github.com/linux-sunxi/linux-sunxi
- at least double what most AP's have.
8GB Flash... roughly 1,000x more than most access points...
512m of DDR3-800 memory.. ~10x more than most AP's
built-in 802.11n (kinda needed for an AP, yah?)
built-in 10/100 NIC (hello, wan port.)
built in sound and hdmi- dual purpose as a media player..
4 USB 2.0 Ports - Plenty of expansion for a USB NIC or other "toys"
Poor reviews for durability; but given the specs, you can't go wrong, right?

all for $100...


So; even though this will have terrible range compared to a purpose-built access point, Why are we paying nearly double for AP's, when stuff like this is out there, waiting for devs?

Does anyone have one of these yet; any insight into the bootloader/serial/jtag/etc?
