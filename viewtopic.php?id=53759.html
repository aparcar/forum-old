If you're transferring inside your LAN, NAT is not involved, and hardware NAT wouldn't make any difference.

But to answer your question directly, no, openwrt does not currently utilize hardware nat acceleration.

For wired-to-wired configuration, if either the laptop or the PC are running at 100Mbit (vs Gigabit) then 80Mbit would be right about the speed you would normally see for transfers.

Since you mentioned a laptop, I will take a guess that you are using wireless for the transfer, and many more things can make a difference in transfer speed over wireless (2.4Ghz vs 5Ghz, HT20 vs HT40, laptop MIMO/config, range, channel selection, congestion/interference, etc).
