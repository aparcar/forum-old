Nate wrote:

6in4 and 6tunnel.  Do they do the same thing?  Which is preferred? Do I need to upgrade to 10.3.1rc4 to get 6in4?

6in4 is a new wrapper around the 6in4 tunnels for IPv6 traffic.
It was introduced at 10.03.1-rc3 or rc4.
In general, if you want to use Backfire, you should use 10.03.1-rc4 or a newer build. There have been several improvements in the past few months. (The best would be a self-built version with all the improvements/fixes also realted to IPv6 even after rc4)

I have a static 6in4 tunnel from SixXS, but the config shouold be rather similar as with HE. Only the '6in4' package is needed and I have not even installed 6tunnel or 6scripts.

You might read this: https://forum.openwrt.org/viewtopic.php?id=27541

I do have it working and serving IPv6 addresses to my LAN.  As I had upgraded the image while telling Luci to keep my configuration, I found that the 6in4 package still wasn't available.  Following the "Edit package lists and installation targets" link and changing the URL to the correct repository fixed that and I was off and running.  It's likely that I do not need to firewall rule listed in the first post as I'm using HE.net as my IPv6 broker but I added it for completeness.  Also, I did not add any additional firewall rules as the default Incoming and Forward policies are Drop and Outgoing is Accept and Masq is enabled.  I also followed some of the configuration details on the Wiki page for HE.net.

After setting up Radvd, my desktop is receiving its Global IPv6 address and everything "just works".  My laptop on wireless does not get a global IPv6 address, however.  I think that is due to my AP being a WRT55AG even though it is functioning as a bridge and not a router.  I would appreciate any insight into whether this is the case.  In other words, the WRT55AG has a connection to the WL500GP via one of its switch ports and not the WAN port.  This way all wireless devices are part of my main IPv4 private subnet.  I suspect that the lack of IPv6 support in the WRT55AG is preventing my laptop from getting its IPv6.  All my computers are running latest versions of Debian.
