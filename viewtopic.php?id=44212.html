I currently have a Asus RT-N13U-B1 router. I have a external 3.5 bay that is self powered (plugs into AC outlet, and doesn't depend on usb power), and a usb printer. Both work for about 3 months connected to the usb hub. After about 3 months the hubs just die. I confirmed that the hubs are dead by connecting it to my laptop, and plugging a usb stick into the hub, and nothing is detected.

I've gone through two hubs. My previous USB router, an Asus WL-520, ran with dd-wrt and a hub for years and didn't blink.

So, in retrospect, I'm not sure if the router is burning out the hubs, or the fact that I had a self powered external 3.5 bay connected to it. I don't see why it would since the 3.5 bay doesn't depend on USB power. Any ideas would be helpful.

It is very unlikely that the router is blowing the hubs.
In my experience it is the peripherals that can cause trouble by drawing too much power. When you say external 3.5 bay, do you mean a "caddy" containing a 3.5" hard drive? There are notoriously unreliable. We used them to transfer data from wrecked computers at maybe 4 times per week. We replaced them about every 2 months.
