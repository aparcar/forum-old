Hello,
I have an Archer C5 v1.2 running Open WRT CC 15.05. After getting this router, one of the first things I had a look at was IPv6.
My ISP has IPv6 with PD and the prefix 2a00:c1a0/32. After the initial setup everything worked for 2-3 days without a flaw. Then I wasn't able to reach sites by IPv6. My PC had an assigend IPv6 from the router and everything seemed like before except that I couldn't reach the Internet by IPv6. After restarting the wan6 interface in LUCI I recognized that I got a new external IPv6 and everything was working again like a charm.
After 2,5 additional days the same thing happened, Everything looked fine but no IPv6 connection to the outside. The router showed the same external IPv6, my PC had an address but no IPv6 internet. Restarting the wan6 interface, new external IPv6, everything working again...
This is a little bit annoying and I don't know where to look. I didn't change any IPv6 things on the router because it worked "out of the box" (but lasts only 2,5 days ;-))

Some help?

I have seen a similar problem where the DHCPv6 client daemon dies. Once the lease expires, the delegated prefix disappears, and you lose IPv6 connectivity (in v15.05).

Look at see if the client is running after you notice there is no IPv6 connectivity:

# ps -w | grep dhcp
  898 root      1164 S    /usr/sbin/odhcpd
 1358 root       800 S    odhcp6c -s /lib/netifd/dhcpv6.script -Nnone -P0 -t120 pppoe-wan

odhcp6c is the client.

In my case it wasn't, and I had to reboot the router (restarting just the daemon didn't seem to work).
