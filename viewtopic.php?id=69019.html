Though ad-hoc mode is basically blasting out packets to anyone who might be in range to receive them, you still do need support in the driver to do that.  Ad-hoc is also known as IBSS.

Ralink is generally the chip you'll want in your USB adapter, as the Linux drivers for them do the most modes.  For example the TP-Link TL-WDN-3200, though that is likely discontinued now.

If you don't have any strong commitment to BATMAN, consider 802.11s meshing instead, it is very easy to set up as (with a supported card) it is already in the driver.
