Hi.

I'm trying to setup a (new) network at the office. In average, there are around 10-15 devices connected, but the router should be able to handle 30-40 in the worst scenario. Network traffic consists mostly of regular web surfing (viewing online documents, use of communications platforms like slack, etc.) and video conferences with people from other countries. My idea so far is to use TP Link Archer C9, but I'm not sure if its cpu will be able to handle that amount of devices connected.

Which router(s) (openwrt supported) would you recommend for this scenario?


Thanks!

I think such a use-case is out of the scope of a consumer-grade OpenWrt machine. Not because of the robustness of the system, but because of the hardware specs you can get with a SOHO router. You probably have an uplink that's sufficient for 30 to 40 people, consumer-grade devices typically reach their throughput limit somewhere around home line speeds.

Not to mention you will never get any SLAs or warranty with an OpenWrt machine.

To be perfectly honest, I wouldn't trust an office of up to 40 people on consumer-grade hardware, I would look into small to medium office grade products. Zyxel's USG/UAG line comes to mind, but there are certainly others. The price of those can be daunting at first, but consider even a short downtime of half an hour for 15 people and the resulting loss of billable hours.
