Yes, you need to continue using the Arcadyan as a modem; I see three options here:

1) You configure both the Arcadyan and the TP-Link as routers: you have the internet, an intermediate network, and an internal network, and thus need to forward ports twice.
2) You configure the Arcadyan as a bridge, and the TP-Link as a router: your TP-Link is effectively connected to the internet, and only need to forward ports there.
3) You configure the Arcadyan as a router, and the TP-Link as an access point: you only need to forward ports on the Arcadyan.

Now, the question is: considering that you still need to use the Arcadyan, how do you want to use the TP-Link?
