It is possible to detect and block unencrypted torrent traffic by using l7 protocol detection in iptables, but its resource consuming and cant detect encrypted torrent traffic. For anti-piracy monitors there's no difference if someone uses encryption or not - they act as regular client and detect content seeding fact.
So, in my opinion the most effective option is port filtering.
Enable only known ports such as 25,80,53,110,443,465,995,3389 and so on.
Some monitors are passive and do not try to connect to you or detect your connection - they just see the fact your IP participate in a swarm. In some countries they can try to extort some money based only on that fact.
I see no 100% reliable way of blocking announce queries to trackers. Its quite possible to write l7 filter with regular expressions to detect bittorrent announces, but you'll have to monitor every http and udp request , its too hard for slow router CPU. Also if a tracker uses https, its not possible to detect.
