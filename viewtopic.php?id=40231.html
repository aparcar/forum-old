control to work.
i have ultravnc viewer in listening mode on port 5500, on my computer.
i forwarded port 5500 to my computer lan ip address.
this is what i add in /etc/config/firewall :
config redirect
        option name 'UltraVNC'
        option src 'wan'
        option proto 'tcp'
        option dest_ip '192.168.1.10'
        option dest_port '5500'
        option dest 'lan'


i also have a dyndns address that is the same as my wan address.
i configured ddns successfully on openwrt, so even if i restart my router, and my ip changed(i have dynamic ip from my isp), the address of dyndns pointing to my new wan address i get from my ISP.
to be sure, i pluged out my router from power line and connect it again, and after it started, i see that my new wan ip assign to my dyndns address.
i did it twice to be sure :-)

i send the ultravnc server to my friend, and it's configured to connect to my dyndns address on port 5500.
after my friend open the file(ultravnc server) on his computer, nothing happened on my computer.

before i switched to openwrt, i had the original firmware on my router (tp link 1043nd), and there, when my friend open the server, i immediately get his desktop in a window, on my screen.
i didn't changed anything with the server or the viewer configuration.
just changed the firmware in the router.

on the other hand, i open ports also for emule, exactly as i open the port for ultravnc.
the only differences between them are that in emule i opened also udp(tcpudp), but the other parameters are the same(except of course, that the port is different on both :-)), as the configuration of ultravnc, as i wrote in the start of this message.
i get high ID on emule, which if i understand well, telling me that the ports are opened perfectly.
and just to be sure, i also check the port on this site:
http://www.yougetsignal.com/tools/open-ports/

when emule is open, and it says that the port is open.

i have the latest firmware version of openwrt:
12.09-beta2 (squashfs)

any suggestions?

well, the issue solved :-).
in the default configuration of the firewall, it has just one section of zone forwarding.
i don't know if the default is from wan to lan, or the opposite, but after i forwarded both directions, i could also get the vnc to work :-).
