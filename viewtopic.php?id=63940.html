You're right to think about monitoring the ping time between routers, but you're likely going to see the biggest improvement in latency for online gaming by controlling bufferbloat. It's the induced delay/lag that crops up when there's high traffic - even momentary - on your bottleneck link (generally, your connection to your service provider).

Fortunately, the SQM-QoS package of OpenWrt holds latency to very low levels even under very heavy traffic loads. (If you're not controlling latency via SQM-QoS, lag can get very high - 2 or more seconds(!))

To install and configure SQM-QoS, read the Howto at: https://wiki.openwrt.org/doc/howto/sqm
