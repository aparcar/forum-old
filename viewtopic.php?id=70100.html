I would suggest that you perform some speed tests over ethernet ports after your service is in place.  Given your inclination to re-flash the router, likely voiding the warranty, I would look at the cheapest, supported router I could find.

In my instance, my speed tests rarely go above 322mbps.  I was able to get an TEW-732BR directly from TrendNet for $11USD +shipping.  It supports 300mbps and flashed easily.  I liked the form factor of the TEW-732, upright, small footprint with the cables entering at table top height for stability.  I saw other, newer and faster routers on their site.
